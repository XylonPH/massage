Title: Massage Nexus Legal and Public Policy Writing Guide
Document Version: 0.20
Date Created: 2026-07-20
Revision Date: 2026-07-20
Document Description: Defines how Massage Nexus drafts, reviews, approves, versions, translates, publishes, corrects, archives, and presents public legal documents and public-facing policies. It governs writing and publication practice; it does not replace the substantive Terms, privacy, security, NSFW, or other policy requirements owned by docs/18-policy.

================================================================================
1. PURPOSE
================================================================================

Massage Nexus legal and public policy writing should give users accurate, understandable,
findable, and versioned information about their relationship with the platform.

This guide governs:
- the boundary between internal requirements and public documents;
- document classification;
- drafting and plain-language presentation;
- source and jurisdiction handling;
- specialist, legal, privacy, security, and product review;
- approval and publication;
- public versions, effective dates, and change summaries;
- acceptance or acknowledgment when required;
- translation and locale handling;
- corrections, replacement, withdrawal, and archival;
- accessibility and public presentation;
- AI-assisted legal-writing limits.

This guide does not decide the actual contractual clauses, privacy disclosures, prohibited
content, security controls, payment terms, or other substantive requirements. Those decisions
remain in the authoritative requirements and policy documents under docs/18-policy and in the
applicable product specifications.

This guide is an internal writing and publication specification. It is not legal advice and is
not itself a public Terms of Use, Privacy Notice, or policy.

================================================================================
2. INTERNAL REQUIREMENTS AND PUBLIC DOCUMENTS
================================================================================

Massage Nexus must keep internal requirements separate from the public document produced from
them.

Internal requirement documents define:
- product and legal decisions;
- required subjects and disclosures;
- data flows and implementation controls;
- unresolved operator, jurisdiction, vendor, retention, and business details;
- publication prerequisites;
- review and approval requirements;
- internal evidence and risk notes.

Public documents explain:
- what users need to know;
- what rules apply;
- what Massage Nexus does;
- what users may, must, or must not do;
- applicable rights, choices, contacts, reports, appeals, and remedies;
- when the document takes effect;
- what changed when a material revision is published.

An internal requirements file must not be published merely by removing its internal labels.
Placeholders, unresolved decisions, internal paths, technical architecture, moderation evidence,
security details, and drafting notes must be resolved or intentionally excluded before public
publication.

The current internal authorities include:
- docs/18-policy/terms-of-use-requirements.txt;
- docs/18-policy/privacy-notice-and-data-protection-requirements.txt;
- docs/18-policy/nsfw-mature-and-restricted-content-policy.txt;
- docs/18-policy/security-architecture-and-information-disclosure-policy.txt.

Future requirement documents may govern Community Guidelines, review and contribution rules,
media uploads, cookies, copyright complaints, accessibility, bookings, cancellations, refunds,
payments, providers, sellers, advertising, and other public policies.

================================================================================
3. DOCUMENT CLASSES
================================================================================

Public legal and policy material should be classified by function before drafting.

Binding Terms or Agreement
Defines contractual eligibility, rights, obligations, restrictions, enforcement, disclaimers,
disputes, and related terms requiring affirmative acceptance when applicable.

Privacy or Transparency Notice
Explains personal-data collection, use, disclosure, retention, security, rights, choices,
contacts, and other legally required transparency. A notice is not automatically consent.

Public Conduct or Content Policy
Defines platform rules, allowed and prohibited behavior, moderation consequences, reporting,
appeals, and safety boundaries. Examples include Community Guidelines and an approved public
NSFW policy.

Operational Policy
Defines a public transaction or service process, such as booking, cancellation, refund,
payment, provider, seller, promotion, or advertising rules.

Legal Notice or Statement
Provides a focused disclosure such as copyright, licensing, cookies, accessibility, disclaimer,
or legal contact information.

Context-Specific Terms
Apply to one campaign, voucher, gift card, event, provider program, seller program, promotion,
or other bounded activity. Context-specific terms must identify their scope and relationship to
the platform-wide Terms.

Legal or Policy Explainer
Explains a legal or policy subject in ordinary editorial language. An explainer is Article
content and must not be presented as the controlling legal text.

Document labels must reflect actual authority. Do not call a draft, summary, help page, FAQ,
article, or AI-generated explanation the Terms, Privacy Notice, or official policy.

================================================================================
4. DOCUMENT RELATIONSHIPS AND AUTHORITY
================================================================================

Each public document must identify its scope and relationship to other controlling documents.

Rules:
- one authoritative public version exists for each document, locale, jurisdiction, and effective
  period;
- summaries and help pages link to the controlling document;
- an incorporated policy must be easy to find and versioned;
- a lower-level policy must not silently contradict a higher-level agreement;
- context-specific terms state whether they supplement or override a general rule;
- an override must be explicit, narrow, and legally reviewed;
- definitions should be shared or cross-referenced when the same term must keep one meaning;
- duplicated clauses must be reviewed together when duplication is unavoidable;
- a public document must not imply authority that has not been approved.

When two documents conflict, publication must pause until the owner of the controlling decision
resolves the conflict. The drafting team must not guess which clause should prevail.

================================================================================
5. SOURCE, JURISDICTION, AND FACT STANDARD
================================================================================

Legal and policy drafting requires current, authoritative sources appropriate to the launch
market and document type.

Possible sources include:
- enacted law and official regulations;
- official regulator guidance;
- court or administrative decisions when relevant;
- final product and data-flow specifications;
- final vendor and processor contracts;
- approved business, payment, booking, moderation, and retention decisions;
- qualified counsel advice;
- verified operator and contact information;
- current platform behavior confirmed by implementation and testing.

For each material legal proposition, preserve internally:
- the source;
- jurisdiction;
- source date or version;
- access date when relevant;
- reviewer;
- uncertainty or pending verification;
- affected public clause or section.

Never invent or assume:
- operator identity;
- business address;
- governing law;
- dispute venue;
- lawful basis;
- consent requirement;
- retention period;
- processor or recipient;
- consumer right;
- liability limitation;
- regulator approval;
- legal-review completion.

Boilerplate from another service is a research lead, not an approved Massage Nexus clause. Do
not copy terms merely because they are common, strict, or professionally worded.

Current laws, regulations, regulator guidance, and jurisdiction-specific requirements must be
verified at the time of drafting and again before approval. Legal research must not rely on AI
memory alone.

================================================================================
6. WRITING STYLE
================================================================================

Public legal and policy writing should be precise without being needlessly difficult.

Use:
- direct headings;
- short paragraphs;
- defined terms only when they reduce ambiguity;
- consistent names for the platform, operator, user roles, services, and documents;
- active voice where responsibility matters;
- lists for conditions, rights, restrictions, and procedures;
- examples when they clarify a rule without changing it;
- links to related documents and working contact routes;
- plain explanations near high-impact choices or consequences.

Avoid:
- one enormous legal paragraph;
- vague statements such as "we may do anything necessary";
- unnecessary capitalization of ordinary words;
- several labels for the same party or action;
- promises broader than actual implementation;
- absolute security, accuracy, availability, safety, or outcome guarantees;
- clauses that appear to waive rights that cannot legally be waived;
- hidden obligations created only through a linked document;
- promotional language inside required disclosures;
- unexplained internal or technical terminology;
- copied foreign clauses that do not fit the governing jurisdiction or business model.

Use must for a real obligation, may for a genuine discretion, should for guidance, and will only
when Massage Nexus can reliably perform the stated action. These words must not be exchanged
merely for stylistic variety.

Plain language does not mean removing necessary qualifications. A shorter sentence that changes
the legal meaning is not an improvement.

================================================================================
7. PUBLIC DOCUMENT STRUCTURE
================================================================================

The exact structure depends on the document. A public legal or policy document normally needs:
- public title;
- responsible operator or issuing authority;
- intended audience and scope;
- effective date;
- public document version;
- locale and authoritative-language statement when relevant;
- definitions needed to understand the text;
- rights, obligations, restrictions, or disclosures;
- procedures, controls, reports, appeals, or remedies;
- enforcement or consequence information where applicable;
- relationship to other documents;
- change-notice approach;
- applicable contact details;
- links to the current version and accessible prior versions when required.

Long documents should include navigation or a table of contents. Important eligibility,
consent, payment, cancellation, safety, content, privacy, and dispute information must not be
buried under decorative introductory text.

A summary may accompany a legal document, but the summary must:
- be labeled as a summary;
- link to the controlling text;
- avoid contradicting or narrowing the controlling text;
- identify material changes accurately;
- never substitute for required acceptance of the controlling version.

================================================================================
8. VERSION, REVISION, AND EFFECTIVE DATE
================================================================================

Editorial revision and public legal version are related but different concepts.

Editorial revision tracks drafts, edits, review decisions, and restoration history before or
after publication.

Public legal version identifies the exact approved text presented to users for one effective
period. Acceptance and acknowledgment records must refer to this immutable published version,
not merely to a mutable current page.

Every approved public version must preserve:
- exact approved content;
- document type;
- public version;
- locale;
- jurisdiction or market scope when applicable;
- approval record;
- publication time;
- effective time;
- superseded version when applicable;
- public change summary when required;
- archive and withdrawal state.

Once a public legal version becomes effective, do not edit its stored snapshot in place. Create
a new draft and publish a new version. A formatting-only repair may retain the public version
only when it cannot change meaning, acceptance evidence, or the rendered text users relied on;
the repair must still be auditable.

Do not use content revision numbers as public legal versions without an explicit mapping.
Internal draft revision 18 may still become public version 2.1, for example.

The current public route should resolve to the current effective version. Archived prior
versions should remain retrievable for audit and user reference where legally and operationally
appropriate, but should not appear to be the current controlling text.

================================================================================
9. MATERIAL AND NON-MATERIAL CHANGES
================================================================================

A material change may affect user rights, obligations, eligibility, data processing, payment,
content permissions, moderation, dispute handling, liability, or another significant reliance
interest.

Material changes normally require:
- a new public version;
- a clear change summary;
- a future or clearly stated effective date;
- appropriate notice;
- renewed acceptance or acknowledgment when required;
- translation review for every supported authoritative or approved locale;
- preservation of the previous version.

A non-material change may correct spelling, formatting, a broken link, or wording that cannot
reasonably change meaning.

Non-material changes still require:
- review that the meaning is unchanged;
- audit history;
- synchronized translations when affected;
- a visible note when transparency warrants it.

Do not label a consequential correction non-material merely to avoid notice or renewed
acceptance.

================================================================================
10. ACCEPTANCE, ACKNOWLEDGMENT, AND CONSENT
================================================================================

Acceptance, acknowledgment, and consent are different actions.

Acceptance records agreement to binding terms when agreement is required.

Acknowledgment records that a notice or policy was presented or received. It does not by itself
create consent or prove that every clause is enforceable.

Consent records a specific, informed, freely given action where consent is the applicable basis.
Marketing, optional research, optional AI processing, and other separate choices must not be
bundled into acceptance of general Terms when separate choice is required.

The acceptance or acknowledgment system should preserve:
- account and site identity;
- exact public document version;
- locale shown;
- presentation and action time;
- acceptance or acknowledgment method;
- evidence metadata justified by accountability needs;
- withdrawal or replacement where applicable.

Acceptance evidence belongs in a dedicated operational record. It must not be stored only as a
counter or mutable flag on the public content record.

The substantive requirements documents decide which documents need acceptance,
acknowledgment, consent, or renewed action. This writing guide does not make that legal decision.

================================================================================
11. TRANSLATION AND LOCALE
================================================================================

Legal and high-risk translation follows docs/13-localization/translation-system.txt.

Each translated public version must preserve:
- source locale;
- target locale;
- source public version and source revision;
- translation method;
- translator;
- specialist reviewer;
- approval;
- authority label;
- publication and effective dates;
- stale state after a material source change.

A translation must be labeled accurately as one of the approved authority states, such as an
authoritative text, approved translation, explanatory translation, or draft.

AI-only publication is prohibited for Terms, Privacy, security, recovery, age eligibility,
payment, refund, cancellation, consent, intake, clinical safety, and serious moderation text.

Do not present a stale or unapproved translation during an acceptance flow as though it were the
current approved text. If the current approved locale is unavailable, the product and legal
owners must decide the safe fallback and disclosure; the interface must not improvise one.

Names, legal terms, rights, deadlines, amounts, contact details, defined terms, cross-references,
and negation require specific review after translation.

================================================================================
12. CONTENT STORAGE BOUNDARY
================================================================================

Public legal and policy pages may use the shared editorial content system only after that system
supports a Legal or Public Policy content type and the safeguards in this guide.

Shared content identity and localized body storage may hold:
- public title and slug;
- summary;
- current publication state;
- localized approved body;
- public media when appropriate;
- public relationships and links;
- ordinary discovery metadata.

Legal-specific metadata must additionally represent:
- legal or policy document class;
- public legal version;
- jurisdiction or market scope;
- issuing authority;
- effective period;
- approval authority and time;
- whether acceptance or acknowledgment is required;
- superseded and replacement version relationships;
- withdrawal or emergency-replacement state.

Field names, option codes, indexes, and collection placement are finalized in database and
taxonomy documentation, not in this writing guide.

Acceptance evidence, consent records, legal holds, internal counsel notes, privileged material,
and sensitive evidence must not be embedded in the public content body.

Public legal documents use the separate legal_main domain. The article_main, article_body, and
article_revision structure guides do not define legal publication records and must not be reused
as though an Article revision were a controlling legal version. The detailed legal_main structure
must preserve an immutable complete published version, including all user-visible localized text
and legal-specific metadata.

Reviews remain in the separate Review module and are not Legal content merely because a review
discusses a legal issue or policy.

================================================================================
13. PUBLIC ROUTES, DISCOVERY, AND PRESENTATION
================================================================================

Public legal routes follow docs/01-project/site-structure-map.txt. Current route direction
includes /legal and focused routes for Terms, Privacy, Copyright, Cookies, Licensing,
Disclaimer, Accessibility, and Contact.

Presentation rules:
- the current document is available without login when public access is required;
- draft, internal, privileged, rejected, and approval-pending versions are not publicly indexed;
- the current version uses a stable canonical route;
- the version and effective date are visible;
- superseded versions are clearly labeled;
- print and download behavior preserves the version and effective date;
- headings and links remain usable on mobile and with assistive technology;
- important notices are not conveyed by color alone;
- the document remains readable without client-side animation;
- broken contact, report, request, correction, or appeal links block publication when they are
  necessary to exercise a stated right or process.

Public legal pages must not expose internal collection names, repository paths, software or
package versions, server topology, private workflow routes, secret contacts, privileged advice,
or security details prohibited by the Security Architecture and Information Disclosure Policy.

================================================================================
14. MEDIA, EXAMPLES, AND INTERFACE TEXT
================================================================================

Legal documents generally do not need decorative media.

Use diagrams, examples, tables, or layered summaries only when they improve understanding and do
not alter the controlling meaning.

Rules:
- an example must be labeled when a reader could mistake it for a complete rule;
- an illustration must not imply a guarantee or right absent from the text;
- screenshots showing a legal choice must match the current interface;
- icons and color cannot be the only indication of acceptance, warning, or document state;
- generated images must not imitate official seals, regulator approval, or legal evidence;
- decorative media must not delay or obstruct access to the text.

Contextual interface notices near registration, booking, payment, intake, uploads, location,
cookies, or sensitive data collection should link to the relevant controlling document and show
the information needed at the point of decision.

================================================================================
15. AI USE
================================================================================

AI may assist with:
- outline comparison against approved requirements;
- plain-language alternatives;
- defined-term and cross-reference checks;
- duplicated or conflicting clause detection;
- source discovery;
- draft change summaries;
- translation drafts;
- readability and accessibility review;
- version-to-version comparison;
- metadata preparation;
- checklist preparation.

AI must not:
- act as final legal reviewer;
- invent law, rights, obligations, sources, contacts, operator details, or product behavior;
- claim that counsel approved a draft;
- publish or approve high-risk text by itself;
- convert uncertainty into a final legal decision;
- copy another service's protected text;
- expose privileged or sensitive legal material to an unapproved provider;
- silently weaken a restriction, right, disclosure, qualification, or non-waivable protection;
- generate an acceptance record that did not occur.

AI output is a draft or review aid until an authorized human approves it. Current legal research
and product-behavior verification remain required.

================================================================================
16. REVIEW AND APPROVAL
================================================================================

Review should be proportionate to the document and change.

Possible reviewers include:
- document owner;
- product owner;
- qualified legal reviewer;
- privacy or Data Protection Officer function;
- security reviewer;
- payments, booking, provider, seller, advertising, or moderation owner;
- localization reviewer;
- accessibility reviewer;
- implementation owner who verifies actual behavior.

The author must not mark a document legally approved merely because editorial review passed.
Self-review alone is insufficient for a new or materially changed high-risk public document.

Approval must identify:
- exact draft or revision;
- scope and locale;
- approving authority;
- decision and time;
- conditions or unresolved launch blockers;
- approved effective date;
- whether notice, acceptance, or renewed acceptance is required.

Publication is blocked when required operator details, contacts, legal decisions, translations,
implementation controls, or approvals remain unresolved.

================================================================================
17. PUBLICATION WORKFLOW
================================================================================

1. Identify the public document class, owner, audience, jurisdiction, and governing internal
   requirements.
2. Confirm that product behavior, data flows, vendors, contacts, and unresolved decisions are
   sufficiently complete.
3. Gather and preserve current authoritative sources.
4. Draft from the approved requirements rather than from generic boilerplate.
5. Check definitions, cross-references, incorporated documents, and route relationships.
6. Verify the draft against actual product behavior.
7. Complete required product, privacy, security, moderation, payment, accessibility, and legal
   review.
8. Prepare each supported locale under the high-risk translation workflow.
9. Assign the public version, effective date, change classification, notice plan, and acceptance
   behavior.
10. Approve the exact immutable publication candidate.
11. Test rendering, links, accessibility, locale selection, archival, notice, and acceptance
    recording.
12. Publish the approved version and preserve its evidence.
13. Monitor contact channels, legal changes, product changes, complaints, and correction needs.
14. Replace or archive through a new controlled version when required.

Urgency may shorten scheduling but must not create invented approval or silently bypass required
legal, privacy, security, or product review.

================================================================================
18. CORRECTIONS, WITHDRAWAL, AND EMERGENCY REPLACEMENT
================================================================================

Minor correction:
Repairs a spelling, formatting, or link problem without changing meaning. Preserve an audit
record and verify translations and rendered copies.

Material correction:
Changes legal meaning or corrects a consequential error. Publish a new version, explain the
change, assess notice and renewed acceptance, and preserve the superseded version.

Withdrawal:
Removes a document from current effect because it no longer applies or should not have been
published. Identify the replacement or consequence and preserve the internal record.

Emergency replacement:
Publishes urgently because the current text creates serious legal, privacy, safety, security, or
consumer harm. Use the strongest review available, record why the emergency process was used,
notify affected users as required, and complete post-publication review promptly.

Do not silently delete or overwrite a version that users accepted or relied on.

containing:

Public Body:

A draft must retain visible placeholders for unresolved facts and must say that it is not
approved for public use. Do not fabricate a public version, effective date, reviewer, approval,
acceptance requirement, or source.

The Public Body may use clean controlled HTML when prepared for the content system. Internal
review copies may remain plain text when that is easier to compare and approve.

================================================================================
20. QUALITY AND PUBLICATION CHECKLIST
================================================================================

Before approval, confirm:
- the correct internal requirement documents were used;
- document class, audience, scope, and jurisdiction are clear;
- operator, issuer, and contact information are exact;
- the text matches current implemented behavior;
- current authoritative sources were checked;
- no unresolved placeholder is hidden;
- definitions and cross-references are consistent;
- summaries do not contradict controlling text;
- rights, choices, consequences, deadlines, and contacts are understandable;
- no unsupported guarantee or waiver appears;
- legal, privacy, security, product, localization, and accessibility review is complete where
  required;
- version, locale, approval, publication date, and effective date are correct;
- materiality, notice, and acceptance decisions are recorded;
- prior versions are preserved;
- translated versions match the approved source and authority label;
- routes, links, contacts, printing, downloading, and mobile presentation work;
- drafts and internal versions are not publicly indexed;
- the exact publication candidate is immutable after approval;
- acceptance or acknowledgment refers to the exact public version;
- correction, report, request, and appeal channels work where promised.

Title: Massage Nexus Language and Market Localization Strategy
Document Version: 0.60
Date Created: 2026-07-10
Revision Date: 2026-07-19
Document Description:
This document defines the language, locale, content-style, routing, fallback, market-priority,
quality, and rollout strategy for Massage Nexus.

Language priority is not based only on world population or total language speakers. It
considers internet-addressable audience, massage and spa market relevance, likelihood that
users need discovery and booking information, availability of useful provider data, strategic
proximity to the Philippine starting market, translation and moderation capacity, and the
ability to maintain the language after launch.

================================================================================
1. LANGUAGE, LOCALE, AND STYLE ARE DIFFERENT
================================================================================

Language:
A linguistic system such as English, Filipino, Spanish, or Japanese.

Locale:
A language plus script, region, formatting, or jurisdiction where needed.

Examples:
- fil;
- ind-ID;
- por-BR;
- jpn-JP;
- kor-KR;
- zho-Hans;
- zho-Hant.

Style or register:
How the language is written.

Examples:
- formal;
- neutral;
- conversational;
- technical;
- legal;
- editorial;
- humorous.

Mixed registers like Taglish are deprecated for interface, layout, and content representation. All Philippine market resources use standard neutral Filipino ('fil').

================================================================================
2. DEFAULT LANGUAGE
================================================================================

Default source language:
English

Default interface:
English

Fallback when a requested language is unsupported or unavailable:
English, followed by the original content language where appropriate.

English remains the authoritative source for initial interface keys, technical specifications,
internal code names, many first-version policy drafts, and cross-language comparison.

An original work may still be created in another language. The original language must remain
identified.

================================================================================
3. LANGUAGE PRIORITY MODEL
================================================================================

Evaluate a language through:
- online audience;
- digital commerce and discovery behavior;
- massage and spa market relevance;
- wellness tourism;
- number and diversity of establishments and practitioners;
- likely contributor and provider participation;
- ability to research and verify records;
- translation and moderation readiness;
- legal and support readiness;
- maintenance cost.

A language does not become useful merely because the interface can be translated.

A useful localized market needs enough of:
- core interface;
- Terms and Privacy information;
- safety and account messages;
- local establishments and services;
- local terminology and aliases;
- search support;
- moderation and reporting;
- contributor support;
- maintainable translation workflow.

================================================================================
5. COUNTRY AND DATA READINESS
================================================================================

Language rollout and country research are connected but not identical.

Examples:
- Spanish can serve several countries, but each country needs local terminology, prices, laws,
  providers, and data.
- English can serve several markets, but does not eliminate local research.
- Chinese requires separate scripts and a market-specific platform, maps, payment, identity,
  and regulatory strategy.
- Thai is highly valuable for Massage Nexus because of massage relevance even though it is
  not one of the largest general online languages.

A language may be:
- registered;
- internally available;
- interface draft;
- limited public beta;
- market-supported;
- fully supported;
- paused;
- retired.

Do not call a language fully supported merely because some strings exist.

================================================================================
7. INTERFACE, CONTENT, CONVERSATION, AND SUBMISSION LANGUAGES
================================================================================

A user may choose separately:
- interface language;
- preferred content language;
- fallback language;
- conversation display language;
- submission language;
- translation display behavior;
- regional formats;
- site-specific override.

Examples:
- English interface with Filipino articles;
- English interface with Spanish content;
- Thai interface with an English fallback article;
- original Korean review with an on-demand English translation.

The application must not force the interface and all content into one language.

================================================================================
8. LANGUAGE SELECTION ORDER
================================================================================

Working priority:

1. explicit page language or language subdomain;
2. explicit logged-in site override;
3. logged-in universal interface preference;
4. guest cookie preference;
5. supported browser language;
6. English;
7. original content language where appropriate.

A stored universal language preference should be reused across future network sites to avoid
repeated setup.

A site may allow an override because terminology, content availability, or user intent may
differ.

Browser detection is a suggestion, not a permanent choice without user confirmation.

================================================================================
9. STATIC INTERFACE
================================================================================

User-facing text must use semantic translation keys.

Preferred:
auth.login.title
auth.login.identifier
account.registration.birth_date
booking.status.confirmed
research.source.last_verified
analytics.metric.profile_view

Avoid using raw English text as the key.

Different meanings need different keys even when English wording matches.

Examples:
common.action.save_record
user.action.save_favorite
promotion.message.save_money

Static interface includes navigation, buttons, forms, validation, errors, account, email,
notifications, workspace, accessibility, security, booking, research, analytics, translation,
and moderation.

Laravel language files belong under:
apps/web/lang/

Possible locale directories:
eng/
fil/
ind/
spa/

zho_hant/

The final Laravel directory naming must follow the selected framework convention and mapping
contract.

================================================================================
10. DYNAMIC CONTENT
================================================================================

Dynamic content may include articles, profiles, taxonomy labels, provider descriptions,
service descriptions, menu descriptions, reviews, discussions, comments, media captions,
help, policies, and research summaries.

Preserve:
- original language;
- source revision;
- target locale;
- translation revision;
- translation method;
- reviewer;
- status;
- stale state;
- publication state.

A translation never overwrites the original.

================================================================================
11. USER-GENERATED CONTENT
================================================================================

Reviews, discussions, comments, corrections, and field notes remain in their original language.

Default direction:
- show original;
- offer Translate when a supported translation is available or can be generated;
- label machine translation;
- offer Show original;
- cache an accepted machine translation;
- invalidate or mark stale when the original changes;
- permit reporting of harmful or materially incorrect translation.

Do not translate every comment into every language in advance.

This reduces AI cost, storage, stale copies, moderation complexity, and unnecessary processing.

A translated discussion remains one conversation. It does not become a separate language
thread unless the community intentionally creates a separate topic.

================================================================================
12. LEGAL, SECURITY, PAYMENT, AND SAFETY CONTENT
================================================================================

High-risk content requires stronger review:
- Terms;
- Privacy;
- account eligibility;
- password and recovery;
- security notices;
- payment;
- cancellation;
- refunds;
- consent;
- intake;
- clinical and safety warnings;
- serious moderation notices;
- NSFW warnings.

AI-only publication is not sufficient.

Acceptance records must preserve:
- document;
- version;
- locale shown;
- date and time;
- account;
- site.

A translated legal document must identify whether it is authoritative, approved,
explanatory, or pending review.

================================================================================
13. TRANSLATION QUALITY AND COMPLETENESS
================================================================================

Measure coverage by area, not one misleading site-wide percentage.

Example:

Filipino:
- registration: 100%;
- login and recovery: 100%;
- navigation: 100%;
- legal: reviewed;
- booking: 82%;
- provider workspace: 40%;
- articles: 17 of 46;
- discussions: translated on request.

Possible dimensions:
- key coverage;
- approved coverage;
- published coverage;
- critical-flow coverage;
- stale count;
- placeholder correctness;
- glossary compliance;
- route coverage;
- email coverage;
- accessibility coverage;
- content count;
- market-data coverage.

A language is fully supported only when critical account, privacy, security, legal, payment,
navigation, and reporting content meets the approved standard.

================================================================================
14. USER AND COMMUNITY TRANSLATION
================================================================================

Users may suggest translations for public taxonomy labels, public profile descriptions,
selected articles, help, captions, public factual content, and non-sensitive interface
strings when a controlled interface exists.

Users do not directly publish.

Workflow:
suggestion
-> automated validation
-> translator or language editor review
-> authorized approval
-> publication

Community translators must not directly publish Terms, Privacy, security, payment, account
recovery, intake, consent, serious moderation, or clinical guidance.

Approved translation work may contribute to reputation or rewards. Raw volume alone must not.

================================================================================
15. AI AND MANUAL TRANSLATION
================================================================================

Support:
- manual human translation;
- community suggestion;
- professional translation;
- direct AI API draft;
- export to ChatGPT or another approved tool;
- validated import;
- AI-assisted human revision.

Export packages should contain key or content identifier, source locale, target locale, source
revision, source text, context, tone, placeholders, glossary, protected terms, formatting
rules, character limits, and risk classification.

Import validation checks expected keys, unexpected keys, placeholders, tags, source revision,
target locale, duplicates, length, prohibited wording, and review requirement.

================================================================================
16. SEARCH AND DISCOVERY
================================================================================

Multilingual search should consider:
- translated name;
- original name;
- alias;
- transliteration;
- local name;
- common spelling;
- normalized record;
- local service terminology.

One service or provider remains one canonical record even when it has many names.

Search analytics should identify failed searches by language, missing synonyms, missing
translations, language fallback, untranslated popular records, and country or city demand.

Do not expose private search histories publicly.

================================================================================
17. SEO AND INDEXING
================================================================================

Each approved public localized page should use correct language metadata, canonical URL,
alternate-language references, safe translated title and description, and language-appropriate
sitemap participation.

Do not publish thousands of thin machine-translated pages merely to increase index count.

================================================================================
20. CURRENT ACCEPTED DIRECTION
================================================================================

- English is the default and source language.
- Filipino (fil) is the sole local language for the Philippine market.
- Taglish, Tagalog (tl), and regional languages like Cebuano and Hiligaynon are deprecated.
- Indonesian is a high-priority international expansion language.
- Spanish, Brazilian Portuguese, Japanese, Thai, and Vietnamese belong in the first
  international expansion track.
- Korean, Chinese variants, French, German, and Arabic follow according to market readiness.
- Language priority combines online audience and Massage Nexus relevance.
- Public language aliases may be reserved before activation.
- Static interface uses semantic keys.
- Dynamic translations preserve originals and source revisions.
- User discussions and comments are translated on request.
- High-risk content requires stronger human review.
- Completeness is measured by area and critical flow.
- A language is not fully supported without useful local data and moderation capability.

DOCUMENT PURPOSE
----------------
This document records the early product direction for Massage Campus, a future learning and institution-support area within Massage Nexus.

Massage Campus may eventually serve individual learners, instructors, assessors, massage schools, vocational training providers, wellness educators, and institutions that need structured online learning, classroom support, practical assessment, learner records, certificates, reports, and branded public portals.

This is not a final feature specification, database schema, accreditation policy, legal interpretation, or immediate development commitment. It preserves the concept without allowing it to displace the current Massage Nexus priorities of establishment profiles, therapist profiles, articles, reviews, ratings, discovery, and core public browsing.

================================================================================
1. PRODUCT CONCEPT
================================================================================

Massage Campus is a future learning environment connected to Massage Nexus.

It may support two complementary uses:

1. Massage Nexus learning content
   Massage Nexus may publish its own lessons, glossaries, knowledge checks, quizzes, practical guides, videos, learning paths, and completion records.

2. Institution-supported learning
   Registered schools, training centers, instructors, or other approved organizations may use the platform to organize learners, courses, classes, attendance, practical activities, assessments, grades, certificates, documents, and reports.

Massage Campus should not be limited to one national training system. The platform is global and must allow jurisdiction-specific institutions, identifiers, requirements, credentials, terminology, and reporting practices.

================================================================================
2. SUBJECT SCOPE
================================================================================

The initial subject direction may include:

- massage fundamentals;
- massage modalities and techniques;
- anatomy and physiology appropriate to the course level;
- body mechanics;
- hygiene and infection-control practices;
- client communication;
- professional conduct;
- contraindication awareness;
- spa operations;
- therapist self-care;
- reflexology;
- bodywork;
- wellness service knowledge;
- client safety;
- practical service preparation;
- related business and workplace skills.

Adjacent subjects may be included only when they fit Massage Nexus and are presented responsibly.

Regulated professions, medical practice, physical therapy, diagnosis, treatment claims, and jurisdiction-specific professional credentials require separate legal, professional, and editorial review. Massage Campus must not imply that a short online course authorizes a learner to perform regulated work.

================================================================================
8. CERTIFICATES, CERTIFICATION, AND CREDENTIALS
================================================================================

Massage Campus must use careful terminology.

Certificate of Completion

A record that a learner completed the defined requirements of a Massage Campus or institution course. It does not automatically prove professional competency, government recognition, licensure, or authority to practice.

Institution-Issued Certificate

A certificate issued under the name and rules of a participating school, training provider, instructor, or organization. The issuing institution remains responsible for the meaning and legitimacy of the certificate.

Practical Competency Record

A record showing that defined practical tasks were observed and assessed. Its significance depends on the assessor, institution, assessment method, and applicable professional or regulatory rules.

External or Regulated Credential

A credential issued, recognized, or governed by an outside authority. Massage Nexus may record or link to it when appropriate, but should not recreate, rename, or imply that it issued the external credential.

Certificate verification may later include:

- unique certificate code;
- verification page;
- learner name subject to privacy settings;
- issuing organization;
- course or program;
- completion date;
- status;
- revocation or correction status;
- digital signature or other integrity control;
- printable or downloadable certificate.

Exact security, identity, legal, and anti-fraud requirements remain deferred.

================================================================================
9. QUIZ AND ASSESSMENT INTEGRITY
================================================================================

No quiz system should be described as impossible to cheat.

For ordinary low-stakes learning, possible controls include:

- randomized question order;
- randomized answer order;
- question pools;
- attempt limits;
- time limits where appropriate;
- delayed answer review;
- different question sets;
- explanations after completion;
- minimum passing score;
- remediation before another attempt.

For higher-stakes evaluation, stronger measures may include:

- identity confirmation;
- supervised testing;
- oral questioning;
- live demonstration;
- practical assessment;
- assessor observation;
- evidence review;
- instructor sign-off;
- on-site examination;
- audit history.

Online quizzes can support knowledge review. They cannot replace hands-on learning when the skill depends on pressure, touch, body mechanics, positioning, safety, communication, or supervised physical performance.

================================================================================
10. PRACTICAL LEARNING
================================================================================

Massage training includes skills that cannot be judged reliably through reading or video alone.

Practical learning may require:

- in-person instruction;
- guided demonstration;
- supervised practice;
- feedback on pressure and technique;
- posture and body-mechanics correction;
- sanitation and preparation checks;
- draping and privacy practice;
- communication with a practice client;
- time-limited practical performance;
- assessor observation.

Massage Campus may organize and document these activities, but the digital platform should not claim that software alone can confirm physical competency.

Possible delivery combinations include:

- fully online theory course;
- online theory plus live virtual instruction;
- online theory plus local in-person practical session;
- school-managed classroom and clinic training;
- hybrid program;
- institution-recorded external practical assessment.

================================================================================
13. MULTILINGUAL AND GLOBAL DIRECTION
================================================================================

Massage Campus is intended for global use.

The platform should allow:

- interface language;
- content language;
- course language;
- institution-preferred terminology;
- translated or localized learning materials;
- jurisdiction-specific requirements;
- local date, number, and grading formats;
- country-specific external identifiers.

Learning content should not be translated mechanically when terminology, safety guidance, professional scope, or assessment expectations differ by jurisdiction.

A translated course should identify who reviewed or approved the localization when the material affects safety, professional practice, or formal assessment.

================================================================================
17. CURRENT BOUNDARIES
================================================================================

Massage Campus should not initially become:

- a replacement for every country's education system;
- a universal government credentialing authority;
- a promise that online study alone proves massage competency;
- a medical education platform;
- a physical-therapy licensing platform;
- an automatic accreditation service;
- a reason to delay the core Massage Nexus directory and content launch.

The immediate Massage Nexus focus remains discovery and content:

- spa and establishment profiles;
- therapist profiles;
- services;
- articles;
- reviews;
- ratings;
- media;
- search and browsing;
- basic contribution and verification workflows.

CURRENT DECISION SUMMARY
------------------------
- Massage Campus is a valuable major future module, not an immediate MVP feature.
- It may support both public self-directed learning and institution-managed education.
- Hands-on massage competency requires practical learning and human assessment.
- Certificates of completion must not be confused with regulated credentials.
- Schools and training institutions may later use learner, instructor, class, assessment, reporting, and export tools.
- Country-specific learner identifiers remain external or jurisdiction-specific references.
- The unified Massage Nexus workspace should support learner, instructor, assessor, and institution-administrator views.
- Branded institution portals should use the shared Branded Portal system.

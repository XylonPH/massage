================================================================================
1. MONETIZATION PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

- The public directory remains useful without payment.
- Providers may claim and correct eligible profiles without buying favorable treatment.
- Payment must not purchase a higher rating, false verification, review removal, or hidden
  editorial influence.
- Sponsored placement is clearly labeled and separated from organic ranking.
- Personal data is not sold to advertisers.
- Sensitive health, intake, location, NSFW, or private-message data is not used for advertising
  targeting.
- Pricing and fees are disclosed before commitment.
- Provider plans should produce measurable operational or marketing value.
- Monetization should not make small independent therapists invisible.
- New revenue systems require legal, privacy, security, tax, accounting, refund, and support
  readiness.
- A general stored-value wallet remains deferred until separately justified and reviewed.

================================================================================
2. REVENUE MODEL PORTFOLIO
================================================================================

Massage Nexus should not depend on one revenue source.

Potential revenue groups:
1. provider subscriptions;
2. promoted listings and advertising;
3. booking and transaction fees;
4. provider software and operational tools;
5. consumer premium features;
6. AI usage and Embers;
7. products, vouchers, and affiliate commerce;
8. training, jobs, events, and professional listings;
9. sponsorship and branded content;
10. API, research, and data licensing;
11. white-label and branded portals;
12. future enterprise partnerships.

Each revenue group may launch only after its product, policy, accounting, and trust
requirements are ready.

3. PROVIDER SUBSCRIPTIONS
================================================================================

Possible provider tiers:

Free Claim
- claim eligible profile;
- correct basic information;
- verify contacts;
- respond to reviews;
- maintain basic service and business information;
- receive essential account and security features.

Professional
- enhanced profile presentation;
- larger media allowance;
- richer service menus;
- basic analytics;
- promotion management;
- scheduling or inquiry tools;
- translation assistance;
- priority support according to policy.

Business
- branch management;
- staff and therapist roles;
- advanced analytics;
- booking operations;
- resource and schedule tools;
- campaigns;
- integrations;
- exports;
- financial and operational reports.

Enterprise or Group
- organization-wide management;
- many branches;
- advanced permissions;
- API or data integration;
- centralized reporting;
- support agreement;
- custom onboarding.

Subscription rules:
- charge by provider, branch, practitioner, feature, usage, or a clear combination;
- avoid hidden per-user charges;
- provide trial or preview where practical;
- disclose limits;
- support monthly and annual billing;
- allow downgrade and cancellation;
- preserve essential account and data access after downgrade;
- do not hold provider-owned content hostage.

================================================================================
4. PROMOTED LISTINGS
================================================================================

Promoted listings may appear in:
- search results;
- directories;
- city pages;
- service pages;
- related-provider areas;
- newsletters;
- approved social campaigns.

Requirements:
- label Sponsored, Promoted, or Advertisement;
- preserve organic results;
- apply geographic and service relevance;
- prevent prohibited targeting;
- show the paying provider;
- prevent misleading claims;
- exclude suspended or unverified advertisers;
- cap repetition;
- measure impressions and actions;
- identify invalid traffic.

Promotion does not:
- change rating;
- remove criticism;
- create verification;
- guarantee booking;
- guarantee editorial coverage;
- make a provider “best.”

- spas;
- therapists;
- wellness centers;
- resorts;
- massage schools;
- professional associations;
- wellness products;
- booking and business tools;
- travel and hospitality businesses;

- articles;
- newsletter;

Advertising rules:

6. BOOKING AND TRANSACTION REVENUE
================================================================================

Possible booking revenue:
- percentage commission;
- fixed booking fee;
- provider subscription including lower commission;
- consumer service fee where lawful and clearly disclosed;
- payment-processing pass-through;
- cancellation or no-show fee share according to policy.

Requirements:
- claimed and configured provider;
- confirmed booking capability;
- payment provider;
- tax and invoicing;
- refunds;
- chargebacks;
- cancellations;
- disputes;
- payout;
- fraud controls;

Do not collect money for an unclaimed provider as if a reservation were confirmed.

Booking monetization begins only after booking reliability and support are strong enough.

================================================================================
8. CONSUMER PREMIUM
================================================================================

Possible consumer premium features:
- ad-free browsing;
- advanced saved lists;
- private service-history reports;
- enhanced comparisons;
- price tracking;
- alerts;
- travel planning;
- premium AI assistance;
- advanced booking preferences;
- early access to selected features;
- expanded media or organization tools.

The initial personal-plan direction uses a free account tier plus an optional Premium membership.
The final public plan name and price remain unresolved. Premium may add convenience, capacity,
cosmetic, alert, reporting, organization, travel, and approved AI benefits, but it is not a
workspace role and does not grant authority.

Data responsibility:
- subscription_plan defines the reusable plan and included entitlement codes;
- user_subscription records one user's subscription lifecycle;
- user_entitlement records one effective benefit, limit, period, source, and revocation;
- user_main may cache the current plan code, subscription state, end time, and public-label choice.

An active user may choose to display an approved Premium Member label on the public profile.
Payment does not increase Nexus Points, badges, Rank, Reputation, rating weight, organic ranking,
verification, moderation power, or user_access.

Keep essential discovery, safety information, account security, privacy controls, and ordinary
reviews outside a paywall.

9. AI FEATURES AND EMBERS
================================================================================

AI revenue may use:
- subscription allowance;
- pay-as-used credits;
- Embers;
- provider plan allocation;
- premium consumer plan allocation.

Possible paid AI:
- translation;
- content drafting;
- provider description improvement;
- review assistance;
- research summarization;
- marketing variations;
- data cleanup suggestions;
- advanced discovery assistant;
- business analytics explanation.

Rules:
- show the cost before spending;
- require confirmation;
- prevent duplicate charges;
- define refund behavior for failures;
- distinguish draft from approved output;
- maintain provider fallback;
- monitor model cost;
- do not charge for an output blocked by a preventable platform failure;
- do not imply AI output is guaranteed correct.

Embers are not a transferable cash wallet unless a separately approved financial design permits
it.

The Ember System owns allowed Ember redemptions, prohibited trust and authority purchases, and
reward accounting. This document owns prices, subscription allowances, paid credits, revenue
limits, refunds, and the boundary between rewarded access and sustainable paid services.

16. MONETIZATION EXCLUSIONS
================================================================================

Massage Nexus must not monetize through:
- sale of personal data;
- sale of private health or intake information;
- hidden sponsored rankings;
- payment to remove lawful negative reviews;
- payment to obtain false verification;
- purchased rating;
- undisclosed affiliate links;
- unverified professional credentials;
- illegal or exploitative services;
- deceptive health claims;
- coercive subscription cancellation;
- dark patterns;
- a financial wallet without separate legal and technical approval.

17. PRICING PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

Pricing should consider:
- local purchasing power;
- independent therapist affordability;
- branch count;
- provider size;
- feature value;
- transaction volume;
- market maturity;
- support cost;
- AI and infrastructure cost;
- taxes;
- currency;
- payment fees.

Rules:
- show total recurring and transaction cost;
- avoid surprise fees;
- provide clear plan comparison;
- use local currency where practical;
- define plan limits;
- allow plan changes;
- review pricing through research and experiments;
- avoid permanent discounts that destroy sustainable pricing;
- preserve a useful free level.

20. PHASED MONETIZATION
================================================================================

Phase 0: Trust and Data Foundation
- no pressure to monetize incomplete discovery;
- establish useful directory, content, research, accounts, and contribution;
- test provider interest;
- measure demand.

Phase 1: Low-Risk Revenue
- provider claim;
- optional provider subscription;
- clearly labeled local promotions;
- contextual advertising;
- sponsorship;
- affiliate links where appropriate.

Phase 2: Transactional Revenue
- reliable booking;
- booking or service fee;
- provider operations;
- vouchers and gift certificates;
- advanced provider analytics.

Phase 3: Expansion Revenue
- commerce;
- AI and Embers;
- training and jobs;
- consumer premium;
- events.

Phase 4: Enterprise Revenue
- API;
- research reports;
- white-label portals;
- organization agreements;
- international enterprise services.

Do not activate every model at once.

================================================================================
21. CURRENT DIRECTIONS
================================================================================

- Use several revenue sources rather than depend on advertising alone.
- Preserve a useful free public directory.
- Give providers a free path to claim and correct basic information.
- Clearly label promoted placement and sponsored content.
- Keep organic ratings, reviews, and verification independent from payment.
- Do not sell personal data.
- Prefer contextual and geographic advertising over sensitive profiling.
- Introduce subscriptions and promotions before complex financial products.
- Add booking revenue only after booking operations are reliable.
- Keep a general wallet deferred.
- Use provider software, AI, commerce, research, and enterprise services as later revenue
  layers.
- Measure revenue together with trust, provider value, and user experience.

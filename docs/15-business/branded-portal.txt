DOCUMENT PURPOSE
----------------
This document records the early direction for Branded Portal, a future Massage Nexus capability that may allow spas, therapists, schools, training institutions, and other approved organizations to present a customized public website while continuing to use Massage Nexus data, accounts, booking, learning, content, and management systems.

The concept may support Massage Nexus-hosted subdomains, customer-owned custom domains, and selected white-label presentation.

This is not an immediate MVP commitment, final architecture, pricing plan, domain policy, database design, or legal agreement.

================================================================================
1. CONCEPT
================================================================================

A Branded Portal is a public-facing website or microsite whose presentation is controlled by an approved Massage Nexus tenant while its underlying platform services remain connected to Massage Nexus.

The portal may look primarily like the spa, therapist, school, or institution's own website.

Examples:

therestingleaf.massagenexus.com
example-spa.massagenexus.com
learn.example-school.com
www.example-spa.com

The domain examples are illustrative only.

The same organization may have:

- a normal public profile inside Massage Nexus;
- a branded portal;
- a custom domain pointing to that portal;
- access to the unified Massage Nexus workspace.

The branded public website and the shared management workspace are separate presentation surfaces even when they use the same data and account system.

================================================================================
2. TERMINOLOGY
================================================================================

Tenant

An organization, establishment, professional, school, or other approved account that manages its own records, staff access, settings, branding, and public portal within the shared Massage Nexus platform.

Branded Portal

The tenant-controlled public presentation generated through Massage Nexus.

Tenant Subdomain

A Massage Nexus-hosted subdomain assigned to one portal, such as:

example-spa.massagenexus.com

Custom Domain

A domain or subdomain owned or controlled by the tenant and connected to the Massage Nexus-hosted portal, such as:

www.example-spa.com
booking.example-spa.com
learn.example-school.com

White Label

A commercial presentation option where Massage Nexus branding is minimized or removed according to an approved plan and contractual terms.

Powered by Massage Nexus

An attribution model in which the tenant's brand leads the public presentation while Massage Nexus remains visibly credited as the technology or platform provider.

================================================================================
3. POSSIBLE TENANT TYPES
================================================================================

Initial future candidates include:

- spa or wellness establishment;
- independent therapist;
- massage school;
- vocational training provider;
- academy or training center;
- therapist collective;
- home-service network;
- wellness educator;
- seller or wellness business when later supported.

A portal account does not change the real-world classification of the business.

For example:

- a budget neighborhood spa does not become a premium spa because its website looks expensive;
- a school does not become a spa merely because it offers a Massage Campus portal;
- a therapist does not become a spa operator because a custom domain is connected;
- a branded website does not prove verification, accreditation, ownership, or licensure.

================================================================================
4. PRESENTATION LEVELS
================================================================================

A future commercial structure may provide several levels.

Level 1 — Standard Massage Nexus Profile

- public profile on MassageNexus.com;
- Massage Nexus visual identity;
- standard profile layout;
- no separate tenant domain.

Level 2 — Branded Massage Nexus Subdomain

- tenant name and logo;
- selected theme controls;
- tenant subdomain;
- Massage Nexus attribution;
- configurable public sections;
- shared platform navigation where appropriate.

Level 3 — Custom-Domain Portal

- tenant-owned custom domain;
- tenant branding;
- configurable navigation;
- connected Massage Nexus services;
- Massage Nexus attribution according to plan;
- domain verification and certificate management.

Level 4 — White-Label Portal

- tenant-owned custom domain;
- tenant-first identity;
- reduced or removed Massage Nexus branding where permitted;
- advanced theme and content controls;
- commercial and contractual restrictions;
- stronger support and governance requirements.

These levels are conceptual. Names, inclusions, pricing, limitations, and availability remain undecided.

================================================================================
6. SHARED DATA AND CONTENT MODEL
================================================================================

The portal should reuse approved Massage Nexus records where practical rather than creating an unrelated duplicate database.

Possible shared data includes:

- establishment profile;
- therapist profile;
- organization profile;
- service catalogue;
- price information;
- schedule and availability;
- booking options;
- contact and location;
- media;
- promotions;
- articles;
- announcements;
- reviews and ratings;
- verification status;
- courses and programs;
- instructor profiles;
- certificate records.

The public Massage Nexus profile and the tenant portal may show different approved subsets or presentation orders.

The system must distinguish:

- canonical shared record;
- tenant-editable presentation;
- platform-controlled verification or moderation data;
- tenant-only internal records;
- portal-specific page content;
- public visibility settings.

A tenant should not be able to change platform verification, moderation, rating, or independent review records merely by changing the branded portal.

================================================================================
7. BRANDING AND THEME DIRECTION
================================================================================

Possible controls include:

- logo;
- favicon;
- brand name;
- color set;
- typography selection;
- header layout;
- footer layout;
- hero media;
- button style;
- card style;
- image treatment;
- public navigation;
- section order;
- selected page templates;
- social links;
- contact actions;
- attribution level.

Customization should use controlled theme options rather than unrestricted code injection in early versions.

A tenant theme must not compromise:

- accessibility;
- readability;
- security;
- mobile usability;
- legal notices;
- required platform disclosures;
- emergency or safety information;
- verification labels;
- payment and booking clarity.

The Massage Nexus theme system may provide a technical foundation, but tenant themes should be stored and managed separately from the platform's own global theme.

================================================================================
8. DOMAIN MODELS
================================================================================

Massage Nexus Subdomain

A tenant may receive a subdomain such as:

tenant-slug.massagenexus.com

Reserved platform and language subdomains cannot be assigned to tenants.

Possible reserved examples include:

- www;
- api;
- cdn;
- status;
- demo;
- app;
- mail;
- support;
- language codes and approved language labels;
- security-sensitive system words.

Custom Domain

A tenant may connect:

- an apex domain;
- a www domain;
- another subdomain;
- a learning-specific subdomain;
- a booking-specific subdomain.

Examples:

example-spa.com
www.example-spa.com
learn.example-school.com
booking.example-spa.com

The exact supported domain types depend on the future hosting and certificate architecture.

================================================================================
9. CUSTOM-DOMAIN LIFECYCLE
================================================================================

A future domain connection process may include:

1. tenant enters the requested domain;
2. Massage Nexus provides verification instructions;
3. tenant updates DNS records;
4. Massage Nexus verifies domain control;
5. the platform maps the domain to the correct tenant;
6. TLS certificate is issued or connected;
7. the portal is tested;
8. the domain becomes active;
9. renewal, failure, ownership change, or removal is monitored.

The system should handle:

- pending verification;
- verification failure;
- active mapping;
- certificate provisioning;
- certificate renewal;
- DNS changes;
- domain conflicts;
- expired subscription;
- suspended portal;
- tenant closure;
- domain transfer;
- portal deletion;
- security incidents.

A domain must not be connected merely because a user typed it into a settings page.

================================================================================
10. TENANT ROUTING
================================================================================

Every incoming portal request must resolve to the correct tenant.

Possible routing inputs include:

- Massage Nexus tenant subdomain;
- verified custom domain;
- domain alias;
- language or regional host;
- portal path;
- request context.

The platform must prevent:

- one tenant receiving another tenant's content;
- domain collisions;
- unauthorized domain claims;
- cache leakage across tenants;
- incorrect analytics attribution;
- cross-tenant media access;
- shared-session confusion;
- insecure fallback to another portal.

Exact routing, cache, hosting, edge-network, and deployment choices remain technical decisions for later architecture work.

================================================================================
11. IDENTITY, ACCOUNTS, AND ACCESS
================================================================================

The preferred direction is shared identity with tenant-specific authorization.

A person may use one Massage Nexus account while holding different permissions for different tenants.

Examples:

- owner of one spa;
- manager of another branch;
- instructor in one institution;
- learner in another institution;
- therapist connected to several establishments;
- portal editor without access to financial records.

The system should distinguish:

- platform identity;
- tenant membership;
- tenant role;
- record-level permission;
- portal-public visibility;
- workspace access;
- billing authority;
- domain-management authority.

A branded login page may lead into the shared identity system without creating an isolated password database for every tenant.

================================================================================
12. MULTITENANT DATA PROTECTION
================================================================================

Branded Portal requires strong tenant isolation.

Future architecture must address:

- tenant identifiers;
- authorization on every protected request;
- database partitioning or filtering;
- storage paths;
- media permissions;
- cache separation;
- search indexing;
- logging;
- analytics separation;
- export permissions;
- staff access;
- support access;
- audit history;
- backup and restoration;
- tenant deletion and retention.

Theme customization and custom domains must not weaken the underlying access controls.

This document does not choose a final single-database, database-per-tenant, collection-per-tenant, or hybrid storage strategy.

================================================================================

A spa or therapist portal may eventually support:

- service browsing;
- price display;
- availability;
- therapist selection;
- booking request;
- confirmed booking;
- deposit or payment;
- cancellation;
- rescheduling;
- reminders;
- contact or inquiry;
- saved preferences;
- booking history;

The public portal may use tenant branding, but booking status and financial information must remain clear and truthful.

The platform should disclose:

- which business provides the service;
- who receives payment;
- applicable cancellation or refund rules;
- whether the booking is confirmed or requested;
- whether Massage Nexus is acting as a platform rather than the service provider.

Exact payment and legal language remain deferred.

================================================================================
14. MASSAGE CAMPUS INTEGRATION
================================================================================

A school or training provider portal may eventually support:

- institution homepage;
- program catalogue;
- course pages;
- instructor pages;
- enrollment information;
- application or enrollment entry point;
- learner login;
- announcements;
- schedule;
- campus resources;
- public certificate verification;
- institution policies;
- contact and location information.

Internal education management remains inside the unified Massage Nexus workspace.

The Branded Portal system should provide the public presentation and domain layer. Massage Campus should provide the learning, learner, instructor, assessment, certificate, and reporting capabilities.

Neither subsystem should duplicate the other.

================================================================================
15. CONTENT AND EDITORIAL CONTROL
================================================================================

Portal content may come from several sources:

- shared Massage Nexus profile data;
- tenant-authored public pages;
- tenant announcements;
- tenant articles;
- service and course records;
- platform-controlled legal or verification information;
- approved imported content;
- selected Massage Nexus widgets or related content.

The portal should identify which information is:

- supplied by the tenant;
- independently verified by Massage Nexus;
- generated from platform records;
- based on user reviews;
- sponsored;
- AI-assisted where disclosure is required.

A tenant should not be able to hide required warnings, fabricate verification, delete independent reviews, or present platform-generated claims as official accreditation.

================================================================================

Massage Nexus must retain the ability to:

- remove unsafe or unlawful content;
- enforce prohibited-service policies;
- preserve independent review integrity;
- display required legal notices;

White-label presentation does not eliminate platform responsibility or tenant obligations.

================================================================================
20. IMPLEMENTATION PRIORITY
================================================================================

Branded Portal is a substantial future platform capability.

It introduces:

- multitenancy;
- domain verification;
- TLS certificate management;
- tenant routing;
- theme customization;
- billing;
- tenant permissions;
- content separation;
- security;
- support;
- portal lifecycle management.

It should not be included in the first Massage Nexus MVP.

The recommended order is:

1. stable Massage Nexus profiles and data;
2. shared workspace and permissions;
3. reusable theme and page components;
4. booking or learning functions where approved;
5. branded subdomain pilot;
6. custom-domain pilot;
7. white-label offering only after operations and support are mature.

================================================================================
21. RELATIONSHIP TO SPA CLASSIFICATION
================================================================================

Branded Portal is presentation and platform infrastructure.

It is not:

- Spa Market Class;
- Spa Type;
- Physical Setting;
- Service Delivery Mode;
- Access Model;
- Operation Model;
- Establishment Status;
- verification;
- claim status;
- professional credential.

Each real establishment retains its own classification regardless of whether it uses:

- only a Massage Nexus profile;
- a branded subdomain;
- a custom domain;
- a white-label portal.

A single organization may manage several establishments, and each establishment may require its own profile and classification even when they share one brand or portal.

CURRENT DECISION SUMMARY
------------------------
- Branded Portal is a future multitenant website capability, not an immediate MVP feature.
- It may support Massage Nexus profiles, tenant subdomains, custom domains, and later white-label presentation.
- The public tenant portal and the unified Massage Nexus workspace remain separate presentation surfaces.
- The portal should reuse shared Massage Nexus data rather than create an unrelated duplicate system.
- Custom branding must not change classification, verification, rating, review, or credential facts.
- Massage Campus institutions should use the same Branded Portal infrastructure.
- Tenant isolation, domain verification, security, billing, and support must be solved before launch.

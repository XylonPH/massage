DOCUMENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
This document defines the purpose, public content, layout direction, management boundaries, lifecycle rules, seller relationships, safety presentation, and future workspace direction of a Massage Nexus product profile.

The product profile is the normalized public record used for discovery, comparison, specifications, versions, variants, sellers, prices, availability, safety information, reviews, ratings, corrections, and product history. It is not a seller listing, advertisement, inventory record, brand website, or database schema.

Detailed classification definitions remain in Product Classification.

================================================================================
1. PROFILE PURPOSE AND BOUNDARIES
================================================================================

A product profile should answer:

- What is the product?
- Which category and grade apply?
- Who manufactures, publishes, or owns the brand?
- Which version and variants exist?
- What specifications, ingredients, materials, claims, allergens, and warnings are known?
- Which sellers offer it, at what price, and under what conditions?
- Is it current, discontinued, recalled, authentic, disputed, or restricted?
- How is it used and for whom is it intended?
- What do users, therapists, spas, and reviewers say about it?
- Has it been renamed, reformulated, repackaged, replaced, merged, or removed?

The profile should remain useful when the product has no official website, incomplete specifications, limited distribution, several sellers, or no current offer.

The following concepts remain separate:

Product
- stable commercial or published identity.

Product Version
- materially distinct formula, construction, design, or release.

Variant
- selectable scent, color, size, volume, model, or package quantity that does not necessarily create a new product identity.

Seller Offer
- seller-specific price, stock, shipping, pickup, return, affiliate, or commercial terms.

Bundle
- several products or variants sold together.

Seller Profile
- separate public record for the seller or retailer.

================================================================================
2. RECOMMENDED PUBLIC PAGE LAYOUT
================================================================================

A. Identity Header

Show when available:

- product name;
- primary image or fallback;
- short description;
- brand;
- manufacturer or publisher;
- Product Category;
- Product Grade;
- current lifecycle status;
- authenticity or recall warning;
- current price range from active seller offers;
- rating summary and review count;
- sponsored label when applicable.

B. Primary Actions

- View Offers;
- Save;
- Follow;
- Share;
- Compare;
- Suggest a Correction;
- Report.

A purchase action should lead to a specific seller offer rather than imply that Massage Nexus is the seller.

C. Main Sections or Tabs

1. Overview
2. Specifications
3. Ingredients, Materials, and Safety
4. Versions and Variants
5. Sellers and Prices
6. Gallery and Documents
7. Reviews and Ratings
8. Promotions
9. Related Services and Providers
10. About, Verification, and Sources

Empty sections may be hidden or replaced by a concise request for information.

================================================================================
3. IDENTITY AND CLASSIFICATION
================================================================================

The profile may show:

- official product name;
- brand;
- manufacturer, publisher, developer, or responsible organization;
- model or line name;
- alternate or former name;
- transliteration or translated explanation;
- Product Category;
- Product Grade;
- Product Use Context;
- current Product Lifecycle Status;
- Product Authenticity Status;
- Product Moderation Status;
- claim, verification, sponsorship, and source context.

Classification follows Product Classification v1.30.

Important boundaries:

- Product Category describes what the product fundamentally is.
- Product Grade compares the product with direct peers in the same category.
- Grade is assigned or confirmed by Massage Nexus and is not freely selected by a seller.
- Price alone does not determine grade.
- Lifecycle, version relationship, authenticity, moderation, review rating, and seller availability remain separate.
- Gift, retail, travel, training, aftercare, and home use are contexts rather than universal product categories.

When a material version change affects formulation, safety, performance, or construction, the grade and reviews may need to apply to that version rather than automatically to every version.

================================================================================
4. CATEGORY-SPECIFIC SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================

The profile should use specification groups appropriate to the Product Category rather than one universal field list.

Possible specification groups include:

Massage Medium
- base or carrier;
- ingredients;
- scent;
- viscosity;
- glide and absorption;
- volume;
- container;
- professional or home use.

Textile and Linen
- material;
- dimensions;
- weight or GSM;
- weave;
- absorbency;
- color;
- care instructions;
- reusable or single-use status.

Treatment Furniture
- dimensions;
- weight;
- load capacity;
- frame and upholstery;
- padding;
- adjustability;
- portability;
- included accessories;
- warranty.

Powered Device
- dimensions and weight;
- power source;
- voltage;
- battery;
- settings;
- operating limits;
- cleaning;
- warranty;
- certifications and safety information.

Digital Product
- supported platforms;
- version;
- license model;
- language support;
- system requirements;
- data and privacy notes;
- update policy.

The profile should preserve:

- source of each important specification;
- measurement unit;
- regional differences;
- whether a value is verified, manufacturer-declared, seller-declared, estimated, or user-contributed;
- last-confirmed date.

================================================================================
5. INGREDIENTS, MATERIALS, CLAIMS, ALLERGENS, AND WARNINGS
================================================================================

A. Ingredients and Materials

Show complete manufacturer or publisher information when available rather than relying only on summary tags.

Information may include:

- ingredient list;
- material composition;
- active or aromatic components;
- concentration where appropriate;
- fabric or construction material;
- replaceable parts;
- country or place of manufacture;
- batch, model, or regulatory information.

B. Product Characteristics

Examples:

- Scented;
- Heat-Producing;
- Electrical;
- Battery-Powered;
- Single-Use;
- Reusable.

C. Product Claims

Examples:

- Vegan;
- Cruelty-Free;
- Organic;
- Dermatologically Tested;
- Hypoallergenic.

Each claim should preserve:

- claimant;
- certification or standard where applicable;
- jurisdiction;
- evidence;
- verification result;
- review date.

A manufacturer statement is not automatically verified platform truth.

D. Allergen and Sensitivity Information

Possible groups include:

- Peanut;
- Tree Nut;
- Coconut;
- Soy;
- Wheat;
- Milk;
- Egg;
- Shellfish;
- Bee Product;
- Latex;
- Fragrance;
- Essential Oil.

The profile should identify the specific ingredient or material when known. Absence of a recorded allergen does not mean allergen-free.

E. Product Warnings

Possible warnings include:

- Skin Irritation Risk;
- Photosensitivity Risk;
- Heat Risk;
- Electrical Risk;
- Flammability Risk;
- Pregnancy Caution;
- Medical Condition Caution;
- Professional Use Only;
- Sanitation Required.

Warnings should distinguish manufacturer, regulator, verified source, editorial guidance, and user report. Serious safety information should be prominent and not buried in general description.

================================================================================
6. VERSIONS, VARIANTS, AND PRODUCT CHANGES
================================================================================

Product Lifecycle Status may be:

- Current;
- Discontinued;
- Recalled;
- Unknown.

Version relationships may include:

- Replaces;
- Reformulated From;
- Repackaged From;
- Renamed From;
- Superseded By.

A version record may show:

- version or model identifier;
- release or effective date;
- formula, material, design, or feature changes;
- packaging change;
- current or previous relationship;
- affected variants;
- review-continuity decision;
- safety and recall information.

Variant examples:

- scent;
- color;
- size;
- volume;
- model;
- package quantity;
- material choice.

A simple size or scent variation may remain one product version with several variants. A reformulation or material redesign may require a separate version when reviews, safety, performance, or specifications would otherwise become misleading.

Old versions should remain visible when they have reviews, safety notices, historical value, or continuing market circulation.

================================================================================
7. SELLERS, OFFERS, PRICES, AND AVAILABILITY
================================================================================

The product profile should aggregate seller offers without turning seller information into product identity.

A seller offer may include:

- seller;
- offer title;
- specific version and variant;
- price;
- previous or promotional price;
- currency;
- stock or availability;
- condition where relevant;
- minimum quantity;
- shipping;
- pickup;
- delivery area;
- estimated delivery;
- return or exchange policy;
- warranty;
- affiliate relationship;
- sponsored placement;
- source and last-confirmed date.

Important rules:

- Price belongs to a seller offer, not the universal product definition.
- One product may have many sellers and prices.
- One seller may offer several variants.
- A product may remain public when no active seller offer exists.
- Massage Nexus should not imply stock certainty when seller information is old or unverified.
- Sponsored placement and affiliate links must be disclosed.
- Seller quality and seller reviews remain separate from product reviews.

The public profile may show:

- lowest confirmed offer;
- typical price range;
- number of current offers;
- local availability;
- shipping or pickup summary;
- last-updated warning.

================================================================================
8. AUTHENTICITY, MODERATION, RECALL, AND SAFETY HANDLING
================================================================================

Product Authenticity Status may be:

- Verified Authentic;
- Unverified;
- Suspected Counterfeit;
- Confirmed Counterfeit.

Product Moderation Status may be:

- Active;
- Under Review;
- Restricted;
- Removed.

Important rules:

- Authenticity status does not replace seller verification.
- A legitimate product may be sold through a suspicious seller.
- A seller may list a counterfeit version of a legitimate product.
- Suspected or confirmed counterfeit concerns require evidence, moderation, and careful public wording.
- Confirmed counterfeit items should not receive normal buying promotion.
- Recalled products may remain visible with prominent warnings, affected versions, dates, responsible authority, and safety guidance.
- Removal from ordinary discovery should not erase safety, recall, contribution, or audit history when continued reference is necessary.

================================================================================
9. MEDIA AND PUBLIC DOCUMENTS
================================================================================

Public media may include:

- official product images;
- packaging;
- labels;
- ingredients or materials;
- size reference;
- included accessories;
- product in use;
- model or version differences;
- safety labels;
- manuals;
- certificates;
- test reports;
- recall notices;
- user and reviewer media.

Media should be labeled by version and variant when differences matter.

Media must not:

- misrepresent another product or size;
- conceal safety labels;
- imply unverified claims;
- show unsafe use as recommended practice;
- copy seller branding in a way that falsely suggests endorsement;
- expose private purchase or identity information.

Public documents should preserve source, date, applicable version, and redaction where needed.

================================================================================
10. REVIEWS, RATINGS, AND REVIEW CONTINUITY
================================================================================

The profile may include:

- review summary;
- overall and criteria ratings;
- editorial reviews;
- user reviews;
- therapist or spa professional-use reviews;
- discussions;
- manufacturer or brand responses;
- safety reports;
- corrections;
- source notes;
- related articles.

A review should identify when possible:

- tested version and variant;
- purchase source;
- test period;
- professional or home-use context;
- disclosure, sponsorship, or sample status;
- price paid;
- relevant specifications;
- observed performance and safety concerns.

Reviews should not automatically transfer across a materially reformulated or redesigned version.

Possible continuity decisions:

- reviews remain shared because the change is only packaging;
- reviews remain visible but filtered by version;
- ratings restart for the new version;
- selected reviews transfer after editorial review;
- safety reports remain connected across all affected versions.

Product Grade provides review context but must never alter the rating automatically.

================================================================================
11. RELATED ENTITIES AND DISCOVERY
================================================================================

The product profile may connect to:

- brands and responsible organizations;
- manufacturers or publishers;
- sellers;
- related products;
- previous and later versions;
- compatible accessories;
- replacement parts;
- bundles;
- related services;
- spas using or recommending the product;
- therapists using or recommending the product;
- Massage Campus lessons or training materials;
- articles;
- promotions;
- recalls and safety notices.

A spa or therapist recommendation is a relationship and may require disclosure. It is not part of Product Category or Product Grade.

================================================================================
12. CREATION, CLAIM, EDITING, AND PERMISSIONS
================================================================================

Public Visitor
- view, save, follow, share, compare, report, review, rate, and suggest corrections when allowed.

Registered Contributor
- propose a product, version, variant, specification, seller offer, media item, safety source, translation, correction, duplicate report, or merge request.

Authorized Brand, Manufacturer, Publisher, or Developer Representative
- manage approved identity, specifications, versions, official media, instructions, warnings, and public responses;
- submit evidence for claims, grade, authenticity, or product changes;
- cannot erase reviews, reports, historical versions, seller offers, or the complete public record.

Authorized Seller
- manage only its seller offer, price, availability, shipping, pickup, return, and commercial terms;
- cannot control universal product identity or unrelated seller offers.

Editor or Moderator
- review product identity, classification, versions, claims, safety, authenticity, seller offers, corrections, duplicates, and sensitive changes according to permission.

Administrator
- add, edit, translate, restore, merge, soft-delete, restrict, and perform approved cleanup after dependency and safety checks.

================================================================================
13. DUPLICATES, MERGE, AND LIFECYCLE
================================================================================

Duplicate review should compare:

- product name and former names;
- brand and responsible organization;
- manufacturer or publisher;
- model and version;
- ingredients, materials, dimensions, and packaging;
- barcodes, identifiers, and regulatory records when available;
- images;
- release dates;
- whether records are variants, versions, bundles, or genuinely separate products.

A merge should:

- select one canonical product or version;
- remove the duplicate from normal discovery;
- redirect or connect the old route when appropriate;
- preserve identity, contributions, revisions, reviews, ratings, sources, and audit history;
- reconnect valid seller offers and relationships only after review;
- stop new activity on the merged record.

Do not merge:

- materially different formulations;
- separate models;
- variants that require independent review treatment;
- original and counterfeit products;
- a bundle with its individual components.

A discontinued or recalled real product normally remains visible. Soft deletion is reserved for invalid, duplicate, spam, test, unsafe-to-display, unlawfully published, or otherwise unsuitable records.

Hard deletion requires legal, safety, retention, audit, and dependency review.

Contribution history should retain a product-name or version snapshot and indicate merge, removal, restoration, or replacement.

================================================================================
14. CONFLICT OF INTEREST, SPONSORSHIP, AND COMMERCIAL INFLUENCE
================================================================================

Brands, manufacturers, developers, publishers, sellers, affiliates, spas, therapists, and sponsored partners may have conflicts of interest.

They should not receive points, Embers, Inklings, reputation, or ranking influence through:

- self-review or self-rating;
- artificial votes;
- undisclosed sponsored promotion;
- repeated reward-seeking corrections;
- manipulation of competing products;
- reacting to their own official responses.

Official specifications, safety notices, offer updates, brand responses, and legitimate corrections may remain allowed when clearly labeled and unrewarded.

Disclose:

- sponsored placement;
- affiliate link;
- free sample;
- discounted sample;
- manufacturer-requested review;
- seller-requested review;
- professional relationship;
- spa or therapist recommendation;
- editorial purchase.

Product ranking should not be purchased through a premium account without clear sponsored labeling.

================================================================================
15. PRODUCT MANAGEMENT WORKSPACE
================================================================================

Early workspace areas may include:

- product identity and classification;
- versions and variants;
- category-specific specifications;
- ingredients and materials;
- claims and evidence;
- allergens and warnings;
- official media and documents;
- seller offers;
- corrections and contribution review;
- recall and authenticity reports;
- public responses;
- basic analytics.

Later modules may include:

- seller inventory integration;
- price history;
- stock monitoring;
- affiliate and sponsored campaigns;
- brand catalogs;
- wholesale and distributor relationships;
- warranty and support workflows;
- advanced document verification;
- localized regulatory information;
- product comparison tools;
- recommendation management.

A seller workspace and seller profile remain separate from the normalized product profile.

================================================================================
16. IMPLEMENTATION PRIORITIES AND OPEN ITEMS
================================================================================

Initial Priority
- identity, category, grade, brand, current version, variants, specifications, safety, sellers, prices, media, reviews, ratings, and basic actions.

Early Management
- brand and seller corrections, version handling, specifications, seller offers, claims, warnings, media, and moderation.

Open items requiring separate decisions:
- final tabs and mobile navigation;
- brand, manufacturer, publisher, and seller claim rules;
- Product Grade evidence and governance;
- version-versus-variant thresholds;
- review continuity after product changes;
- ingredient and allergen source hierarchy;
- recall and regulatory handling by jurisdiction;
- authenticity evidence;
- seller-offer freshness and price history;
- return, warranty, and shipping normalization;
- affiliate and sponsored disclosure;
- public ranking rules;
- product-focused release phase.

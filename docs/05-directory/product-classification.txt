DOCUMENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
This document defines the controlled fields and option sets used to classify massage, spa, wellness, therapist, training, and related products in Massage Nexus. It includes recommended snake_case field names, stable option codes, concise labels, and internal descriptions.

This is a compact taxonomy reference, not public documentation, a database schema, a product-profile specification, a seller model, an inventory system, or a universal specification engine. Product categories are hierarchical, while detailed specifications remain category-specific. Labels may be translated later without changing their stable option codes.

GENERAL RULES
-------------
- Product Category identifies what a product fundamentally is.
- Product Grade compares a product only with direct peers in the same category.
- Product Grade is assigned or confirmed by Massage Nexus and is not freely selected by a seller.
- Price alone does not determine Product Grade.
- Product Use Context is multi-select.
- Ingredients, materials, dimensions, formula, capacity, electrical requirements, and other detailed specifications remain category-specific.
- Product claims require a source and verification context.
- Lifecycle, version relationship, authenticity, moderation, seller offer, and recommendation are separate concepts.
- Unconfirmed classifications remain unselected and may be explained through a separate internal note.

2. SERVICE AND PRODUCT CLASSIFICATION BOUNDARY
================================================================================

Service Classification describes what happens during a service, how it is performed, and the generic service-defining characteristics involved.

Product Classification describes the physical item, material, formulation, equipment, consumable, textile, tool, device, or branded product used to deliver, support, enhance, or follow the service.

Examples:
- Service Classification records Oil as the Massage Medium; Product Classification records the exact oil type, ingredients, formula, brand, size, and version.
- Service Classification records Towel as a Draping Item; Product Classification records the towel type, fiber, dimensions, construction, absorbency, and actual product.
- Service Classification records Tool-Assisted Work; Product Classification identifies Bamboo Massage Tool, Massage Stone, Massage Cup, or another controlled tool type.
- Service Classification records Mud as a Body Treatment Medium; Product Classification records the actual mud product, formula, ingredients, warnings, and brand.

The service-to-product relationship records whether a product type or product is Required, Commonly Used, Optional, a Permitted Alternative, an Enhancement, or Historically Associated.

A Product Category or Product Type does not prove that every service of a named tradition uses that product. For example, Bamboo Massage Tool exists as a product type, but Lomi Lomi must not automatically be classified as bamboo-assisted unless the particular method or provider offering documents that use.

C    | Budget   | Lower-cost product providing acceptable function with simpler materials, features, formulation, durability, or presentation.
D    | Basic    | Entry-level or minimal product providing only essential function within its category.

Assignment Rules
----------------
- Compare only direct peers; disposable, reusable, digital, textile, topical, and equipment products are not graded by one identical checklist.
- Use category-relevant evidence such as materials, formulation, construction, durability, safety, performance, warranty, professional suitability, and presentation.
- A lower-grade product may receive an excellent review for value and performance.
- A premium-grade product may receive a poor review when it fails premium expectations.
- Seller claims and price may support review but cannot assign the grade by themselves.

================================================================================
22. PRODUCT IDENTITY BOUNDARIES
================================================================================

* Product - the stable commercial or published product identity.
* Product Version - a materially distinct formulation, construction, design, or release of the product.
* Variant - a selectable difference such as scent, color, size, volume, model, or package quantity that does not necessarily create a new product identity.
* Seller Offer - a seller-specific price, availability, shipping, inventory, affiliate, or commercial listing.
* Bundle - several products or variants sold together.
* Seller relationship, recommendation, sponsorship, affiliate link, editorial mention, and availability are not Product Categories.

================================================================================
23. FUTURE PRODUCT TAXONOMY WORK
================================================================================

- Additional product types should be added only when they represent meaningful, reusable distinctions.
- Category-specific specifications remain separate from Product Type.
- Actual ingredient, material, formulation, dimension, electrical, sanitation, and performance structures require Product Profile and Database Structure work.
- Service Classification must link to Product Types or actual Product records rather than duplicate this taxonomy.
- A product's presence in the taxonomy does not imply endorsement, safety, legality, or suitability for every user or service.

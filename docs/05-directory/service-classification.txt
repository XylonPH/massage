Massage Nexus: Service Classification
Document Version: 2.10
Date Created: 2026-07-11
Revision Date: 2026-07-21

DOCUMENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
This document defines the controlled hierarchy and family-specific taxonomies used to classify services in Massage Nexus.
The hierarchy is broad enough to support service records beyond massage, spa, beauty, and wellness without renaming the common fields later.

Massage Nexus currently adopts and displays the sectors, domains, and families relevant to its product scope.
Unrelated services such as carpentry, plumbing, tailoring, repair, transport, and professional services can use the same hierarchy in another project or a later approved expansion.

This document defines classification fields and controlled options. It does not contain the catalog of actual named services.
Thai Massage, Swedish Massage, Manicure, Haircut, Body Scrub, Carpentry, and Garment Alteration are examples of service records in service_main, not option values inside this document.

This is not public documentation, a complete database schema, a provider menu, a price table, a booking specification, or a training manual.

GENERAL RULES
-------------
- Technical field names use English snake_case.
- Controlled option codes use stable uppercase codes, normally three characters for hierarchy records.
- Labels, translations, icons, and public slugs may change without changing stable codes.
- Option codes are unique within their field namespace. The same three-character code may appear in unrelated fields with different meanings; because this document defines many closely related wellness fields, exports, logs, and reports should always pair a code with its field_name to avoid ambiguity (for example, COM means Compression in group_massage_technique but Communal in mode_bathing_access).
- Service Sector, Service Domain, and Service Family form a hierarchy.
- A normalized service has one primary Service Family.
- Cross-family meaning is represented through service relationships, not several competing primary families.
- For services that appear to belong equally to two families, select the family that represents the primary commercial presentation or client expectation. Use service relationships to link to the secondary family. If equal representation is undeniable, use the "Proposed Service" workflow to request a new hybrid family or clarify existing boundaries.
- Named services are service_main records with independent descriptions, aliases, relationships, media, sources, and lifecycle.
- Provider menu names, prices, durations, packages, add-ons, and availability belong to establishment_service or practitioner_service.
- Classification does not automatically establish legality, safety, scientific support, credentialing, public visibility, or review quality. Where the platform uses these stable codes to drive hard content filters, age gates, payment-gateway compliance, or access restrictions, the controlling policy and application rules must be enforced server-side. The interface may explain or reflect those rules but must not be the sole enforcement mechanism.
- Unsupported values remain unselected and may be explained through an internal note.
- Do not use Other as a routine option. If an existing classification is insufficient, providers must use the "Proposed Service" workflow ticket to request a new catalog record.

1. SERVICE HIERARCHY
================================================================================

Service Sector
The broadest grouping, comparable to an economic sector.

Service Domain
A mid-level grouping inside a Service Sector.

Service Family
The specific classification used for a normalized service record. This is the level Massage Nexus assigns directly to service_main records.

Normalized Service
A reusable service concept shared across establishments and practitioners.

Massage Nexus may later adopt another existing domain or add a new family record without changing the common hierarchy fields or code length.

Family Boundary Notes
---------------------
- Massage is the primary family when a service is commercially and publicly presented principally as massage.
- Manual Bodywork is used when presenting the service merely as massage would materially misrepresent its method, professional tradition, or client expectation.
- Reflexology is organized around reflex maps or zones and remains distinct from ordinary massage of the same body area.
- Hydrotherapy and Aquatic Bodywork requires water, buoyancy, water pressure, immersion, or aquatic movement to function as a central treatment method.
- Bathing and Wet Wellness centers on bathing, soaking, bathhouse access, or a wet-wellness experience rather than a structured therapeutic water method.
- Thermal and Recovery Wellness centers on heat, cold, compression, or recovery exposure rather than water itself.
- Device-Led Wellness is used when specialized equipment is the principal delivery mechanism and the service is not more accurately a regulated medical or aesthetic procedure.
- Medical Aesthetic Service remains separate when medical supervision, regulated practice, or medical-aesthetic scope is central.

6. SERVICE RELATIONSHIP TYPE
================================================================================

Field Name: type_service_relationship
Field Label: Service Relationship Type
Selection: One relationship type per connected service pair

Code | Label                 | Description
-----|-----------------------|------------------------------------------------------------
BRD  | Broader               | Source service is broader than the connected service.
NRW  | Narrower              | Source service is narrower than the connected service.
PAR  | Parent                | Source service is the direct parent of the connected service.
CHD  | Child                 | Source service is the direct child of the connected service.
EQU  | Equivalent            | Both records represent the same service concept.
NEQ  | Near Equivalent       | Services overlap substantially but are not fully interchangeable.
REL  | Related               | Services have a meaningful non-hierarchical relationship.
CCF  | Commonly Confused     | Services are frequently mistaken for one another.
CMP  | Component             | Source service may be included as a component of the connected service.
COM  | Often Combined        | Services are commonly performed or sold together.
CLN  | Clinical Counterpart  | Connected service is the regulated or clinical counterpart.
SEN  | Sensitive Counterpart | Connected service is the sensual, sexual-wellness, or adult counterpart.
SUP  | Supersedes            | Source service replaces an older normalized service identity.
SBD  | Superseded By         | Source service has been replaced by a newer identity.
MRG  | Merged Into           | Source record was consolidated into the connected canonical record.
PRE  | Prerequisite For      | Source service is required or recommended before the connected service.
FOL  | Follows               | Source service naturally follows or is performed after the connected service.

Directional inverse relationships must strictly follow these mappings when automatically generated:
- BRD <-> NRW
- PAR <-> CHD
- EQU <-> EQU
- NEQ <-> NEQ
- REL <-> REL
- CCF <-> CCF
- SUP <-> SBD
- PRE <-> FOL
- CLN <-> SEN

For the CLN and SEN inverse pair:
- a sensual, sexual-wellness, or adult service pointing to its clinical counterpart uses CLN;
- a clinical service pointing to its sensual, sexual-wellness, or adult counterpart uses SEN.

33. SERVICE NORMALIZATION AND PROVIDER USE
================================================================================

service_main
- stores one normalized service concept;
- receives its Service Family;
- may receive applicable family-specific classifications;
- may have aliases, translations, relationships, media, sources, articles, and lifecycle history.

establishment_service
- stores the establishment's exact commercial menu item;
- preserves the provider name, description, duration, price, variants, package structure, add-ons, and availability;
- maps to one or more service_main records when supported.

practitioner_service
- stores an independent therapist's exact commercial menu item;
- preserves the therapist's own name, description, price, duration, service area, package structure, and availability;
- maps to one or more service_main records when supported.

practitioner_capability
- represents that a practitioner can competently perform a normalized service even when the practitioner does not independently sell it;
- remains conceptually separate from practitioner_service.

Capability remains valuable because a spa-employed practitioner may perform services sold only through an establishment and may have no independent commercial menu.

A capability may be asserted or supported by:
- practitioner self-declaration;
- establishment-operator confirmation;
- platform editor review based on reliable evidence;
- credential or training record;
- competency assessment;
- documented professional experience.

Capability trust context may distinguish:
- claimed;
- employer-confirmed;
- credential-supported;
- independently verified;
- disputed;
- expired or no longer current.

A capability must not be inferred merely because:
- an affiliated establishment offers the service;
- the practitioner works at that establishment;
- a vaguely similar service appears in an unsourced biography;
- a service is presently available for booking.

The exact storage structure for practitioner_capability belongs to Database Structure documentation. This classification document confirms the conceptual distinction without requiring a particular collection design.

Offering, Capability, Availability, and Credential Support are separate facts:

- Establishment Offering means the establishment presents or sells a provider menu item mapped to one or more normalized services.
- Practitioner Capability means a practitioner can competently perform a normalized service, whether or not it is independently sold.
- Current Availability means a service can presently be booked in a particular context.
- Credential Support means a credential supports a claimed capability within a defined scope and validity period.

An establishment offering a service does not mean every affiliated practitioner can perform it.
A practitioner's capability does not mean every affiliated establishment offers it.
Capability does not prove current availability.
Availability does not prove verified training.
Uncertain provider-menu mappings remain pending rather than guessed.

Examples:

service_main
- Thai Massage

establishment_service
- Royal Thai 90
- maps to Thai Massage
- 90 minutes
- PHP 1,200

practitioner_service
- Adazy Massage
- maps to Sensual Massage
- remains a practitioner-specific name unless independently recognized beyond that practitioner.

34. REMOVED OR SEPARATE CONCEPTS
================================================================================

- Lists of actual named services belong to service_main seed or catalog data rather than this controlled classification document.
- Exact products, brands, formulas, ingredients, materials, dimensions, and product specifications belong to Product Classification and product records.
- Provider-Specified is not a Clothing, Attire, or Draping option; record the real arrangement and an explanatory note.
- Adjustable and By Area are removed from Massage Pressure Level because they describe adaptation rather than pressure levels.
- Portable Setup is removed from Treatment Support because portability describes deployment.
- Aquatic Setup is replaced by Floatation Support together with the existing aquatic Service Family and Water Contact Method.
- Movement-Based and Variable are removed from Client Position and handled by Client Positioning Mode.
- Towel Draping is replaced by the independent Draping Item field.
- Steam and Sauna are removed from Water Contact Method and separated under Thermal Exposure Method.
- Mud and Clay are removed from Body Treatment Action and separated under Body Treatment Medium.
- Combination is removed from Reflexology Target because multi-select already represents combined targets.
- Both is removed from Hair and Scalp Care Target because multi-select already represents both targets.
- Sensitive Service Nature is removed because Service Family and Service Relationship already represent sensual, sexual-wellness, adult, clinical, and counterpart distinctions.
- Reiki and Energy Work are removed from Massage Technique Structure because Reiki is not a massage technique.
- "Other" options are not retained as routine catch-alls. A genuinely new classification uses the approved taxonomy-change workflow.
- Pricing, duration, booking, promotion, inventory, and schedule remain provider-commercial or operational data.

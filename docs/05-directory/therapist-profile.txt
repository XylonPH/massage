The therapist profile is the normalized public professional record used for discovery, services, affiliations, prices, availability, booking enablement, credentials, reviews, ratings, corrections, and professional history. It connects to, but remains distinct from, the therapist's user account, professional-context relationship records, private workspace, booking records, and branded website.

This is not public documentation, a database schema, field catalog, credential schema, form specification, scheduling specification, or complete workspace specification. Detailed classifications, booking behavior, private operations, provenance, and privacy requirements remain in their authoritative related documents.

================================================================================
1. PROFILE PURPOSE AND BOUNDARIES
================================================================================

A therapist profile should answer:

- Who is this therapist professionally?
- What services and massage systems can they perform?
- What specialties and client contexts do they focus on?
- Where and under what arrangements do they work?
- What establishment-assigned rank or title applies in each affiliation?
- What market class, price, and service conditions apply in each work context?
- Are they available and how must a client book?
- What service areas and practice settings are supported?
- What credentials, education, and experience are documented?
- What do clients say about them?
- How current, verified, disputed, merged, inactive, retired, or historical is the record?

The profile should remain useful even when the therapist is independent, has no website, has limited media, works through several establishments, or has not claimed the profile.

A therapist profile and user account remain separate:

Therapist Profile
- represents the professional person and may exist before account registration.

User Account
- controls authentication, personal settings, messages, contributions, and permissions.

Professional Context
- represents an establishment affiliation or independent practice in which work arrangement, provider rank, market class, price, access, and availability may differ.

Stable Identity and Public Username
- the therapist profile uses a stable therapist identifier and public profile slug whether claimed or unclaimed;
- a claimed therapist may connect a user account and may receive or choose an approved public username;
- username is not the database matching key and must not be used to create a second therapist record;
- establishments may propose a relationship to the existing therapist profile through stable identity, an affiliation invitation, or reviewed matching;
- an establishment relationship proposal is not a claim to the therapist profile.

No establishment owns the therapist's complete identity merely because the therapist works there.

================================================================================
2. RECOMMENDED PUBLIC PAGE LAYOUT
================================================================================

A. Identity Header

Show when available:

- approved public name;
- professional photo or neutral placeholder;
- short professional summary;
- primary current location or service-area summary;
- overall availability summary;
- separate claim, identity-verification, and booking-enablement labels;
- official rating summary, rating count, and review count as separate facts;
- starting price or price range with professional context, currency, representative duration,
  material fee limitations, and last-confirmed date;
- principal affiliation or independent-practice summary.

Do not display one permanent Provider Rank or Therapist Service Market Class in the header when several professional contexts differ. Use a concise summary such as Prices and service level vary by location.

Principal affiliation means a therapist-selected or transparently derived current professional
context. It must not be inferred from sponsorship, advertising spend, rating, price, or
establishment preference. When no principal context is established, show a neutral multi-context
summary rather than selecting one silently.

Claim, identity, contact, affiliation, credential, location, and booking verification are separate
facets. Do not present one blanket Verified Therapist badge that implies every profile fact or
professional claim has been verified.

B. Primary Actions

- Book;
- Request Availability;
- Contact;
- Save;
- Follow;
- Share;
- Compare;
- Suggest a Correction;
- Report.

Get Directions should appear only for an approved public practice location.

Book appears only when at least one professional context is booking-enabled for the selected
service. Contact, inquiry, external-booking, and affiliated-establishment actions must identify
their destination and must not imply direct therapist contact or confirmed availability when that
has not been established.

C. Main Sections or Tabs

1. Overview
2. Services and Prices
3. Specialties and Capabilities
4. Availability and Booking
5. Practice Settings and Service Area
6. Affiliations and Independent Practice
7. Experience and Credentials
8. Gallery and Public Documents
9. Reviews and Ratings
10. Promotions and Updates
11. About, Verification, and Sources

Empty sections may be hidden or replaced by a concise invitation to contribute information.

D. Accessibility and Localization

- tabs and actions must remain keyboard accessible and use meaningful headings and labels;
- availability, claim, credential, rating, and booking states must not rely on color alone;
- meaningful images require approved alternative text or an equivalent accessible description;
- maps and directions require a non-map text alternative;
- translated biographies, titles, and source-derived content remain connected to the original
  language and identify machine translation when required;
- safety-critical, consent, eligibility, payment, and policy text follows the high-risk translation
  requirements in the Language Strategy and Translation System.

================================================================================
3. IDENTITY, PUBLIC NAME, AND PRIVACY
================================================================================

Possible identity layers include:

- private legal name used for verification;
- approved public professional name;
- nickname or working name;
- former professional name;
- alternate spelling;
- transliteration.

The public profile should not normally display:

- exact birthdate;
- government identifiers;
- private home address;
- private phone or email;
- family or emergency contacts;
- payroll, tax, or medical information.

Age or age range should appear only after a separate product decision and informed consent.

Public occupational titles, regulated-service claims, and booking eligibility must follow the
rules of the applicable jurisdiction. Claiming a profile or declaring a capability does not
authorize a protected title, licensed activity, medical claim, or regulated practice. Credential,
license, scope, jurisdiction, and verification details must be displayed separately and only to
the extent permitted and necessary.

Unclaimed and Third-Party-Created Profiles
------------------------------------------
An unclaimed profile requires a documented lawful processing basis, source provenance, data
minimization, appropriate notice, and accessible correction, objection, restriction, erasure or
blocking, safety-suppression, and appeal routes. Public availability of a fact does not remove
these obligations.

An unclaimed profile must not expose a private contact channel, exact private location, identity
evidence, or unsupported sensitive inference. Search indexing, cache removal, route redirection,
and third-party delisting must be considered when a profile is suppressed or an applicable data-
subject request is approved.

4. CLASSIFICATION SUMMARY
================================================================================

The profile may summarize classifications from docs/05-directory/therapist-classification.txt:

Therapist-Level or Broad Summary
- Practice Settings;
- Specialty Focus;
- Client Focus;
- credential summary derived from individual credential records;
- Professional Practice Status;
- general availability summary.

Professional-Context Information
- Work Arrangement;
- Provider Rank;
- exact provider title;
- Therapist Service Market Class;
- Availability Status;
- Booking Requirement;
- Client Access;
- services and prices;
- location or service area.

Important boundaries:

- Work Arrangement may differ across affiliations.
- Provider Rank is assigned by an establishment or organized provider.
- Exact titles such as Master Reflexologist or Visiting Master Therapist should be preserved separately.
- Therapist Service Market Class describes one commercial service context and is assigned or confirmed by Massage Nexus.
- A therapist may be Standard at one establishment and Premium in independent practice.
- Ratings, credentials, provider rank, price, experience, and market class remain separate facts.
- Professional Practice Status describes whether the person is currently practicing, inactive,
  retired, or unknown. Availability Status describes whether a service context is accepting or
  providing services. Person life status remains separate from both.

================================================================================
5. SERVICES, CAPABILITIES, SPECIALTIES, AND PRICING
================================================================================

The profile may show:

- normalized services the therapist can perform;
- establishment-specific services currently offered;
- independent-practice menu items;
- massage systems and traditions;
- techniques when useful and supported;
- pressure ranges;
- documented practitioner contact body parts where relevant to a named technique or service;
- body-area competence;
- client adaptations;
- Specialty Focus;
- service restrictions;
- safety or scope notes;
- packages and add-ons where applicable;
- durations and prices;
- last-confirmed date.

Named services such as Hilot, Thai Massage, Shiatsu, Oncology Massage, Manual Lymphatic Drainage, Reflexology, Deep Tissue Massage, and Hot Stone Massage are service capabilities rather than Specialty Focus labels.

Capability does not automatically mean:

- currently accepting bookings;
- available at every affiliation;
- formally credentialed;
- publicly advertised;
- appropriate for every client.

The therapist's own commercial menu and an establishment's menu remain separate. Price belongs to the specific offering or affiliation context.

Pressure, body-area competence, practitioner contact body parts, attire, undressing, treatment
contact, and draping are service- or offering-level facts rather than unrestricted personal
traits. Display them only in the relevant normalized service or booking context, with appropriate
moderation and age, privacy, safety, consent, and localization controls. Do not infer them from
photos, suggestive names, reviews, or free text.

================================================================================
6. AFFILIATIONS, WORK ARRANGEMENTS, RANK, AND MARKET CLASS
================================================================================

A therapist may have several current or historical professional contexts:

- spa;
- salon;
- wellness center;
- private studio;
- clinic;
- rehabilitation facility;
- hospital;
- hotel;
- resort;
- bathing facility;
- fitness or sports facility;
- corporate site;
- event site;
- client residence;
- therapist residence;
- training center;
- community or residential-care facility;
- retreat center;
- transportation hub;
- maritime facility;
- mobile treatment unit;
- outdoor venue;
- independent practice.

Each context may show:

- organization or establishment;
- relationship lifecycle, such as pending, active, temporary, or ended;
- separate confirmation state, such as submitted, therapist-confirmed, independently verified,
  disputed, or rejected;
- Work Arrangement: Employee, Independent, or Contractor;
- public role;
- exact provider title;
- normalized Provider Rank;
- Therapist Service Market Class;
- services available there;
- therapist-specific price or surcharge;
- Availability Status;
- Booking Requirement;
- Client Access;
- public schedule summary;
- source, confirmation, and dispute status.

Provider Rank may be Trainee, Junior, Regular, Senior, Specialist, Master, or Visiting Expert. It describes the provider's internal personnel or commercial structure, not a universal credential.

The establishment may submit its title and rank. The therapist may confirm or dispute it. Massage Nexus may moderate misleading titles.

Relationship lifecycle and confirmation state answer different questions and must not be combined
into one ambiguous status. A temporary relationship may be confirmed; a reported current
relationship may remain unconfirmed or disputed.

The therapist must actively confirm an affiliation before it is eligible for display on the establishment's public roster; a submitted but unconfirmed affiliation must not appear publicly as though it were active. An active disaffiliation or dispute must immediately remove the therapist from that establishment's public roster and availability pools rather than waiting for a moderation pass. This prevents an establishment from presenting a "ghost roster" of therapists who have left or never confirmed the relationship; see docs/05-directory/spa-profile.txt section 6.

Disaffiliation Booking Workflow: When a therapist disaffiliates from or disputes a relationship
with an establishment, stop new bookings and assignments for that context immediately. Existing
pending or confirmed bookings enter the governed resolution workflow. A therapist-specific
booking must not be silently reassigned. Reassignment, cancellation, refund, or transition to an
independent practice requires the notices, permissions, client choice, price and policy snapshots,
privacy controls, and mutual agreement applicable to that booking. Former provider access to
client and intake information ends when no longer necessary or authorized.

A freelance therapist uses an Independent Practice context. Provider Rank is normally not applicable, but service market class, prices, service areas, availability, and booking rules may still be recorded.

Removing one affiliation must not delete the therapist profile.

================================================================================
7. AVAILABILITY, BOOKING, AND SCHEDULE
================================================================================

Availability Status:
- Available;
- Limited Availability;
- Existing Clients Only;
- Temporarily Unavailable;
- Unknown.

Professional Practice Status:
- Active;
- Inactive;
- Retired;
- Unknown.

Availability applies per relevant professional context. Professional Practice Status applies to
the therapist's overall current practice. Neither status is a person life-status field.

Booking Requirement:
- Walk-In Accepted;
- Appointment Preferred;
- Appointment Required.

Claim and Booking Boundary
--------------------------
The public profile exposes booking enablement and safe summaries. The Booking and Appointment
System owns booking modes, selection, confirmation, intake, scheduling, resources, price
calculation, policy snapshots, payment, cancellation, and completion behavior.

An unclaimed therapist profile may support:
- Personal Appointment Plan;
- Personal Service History;
- an unconfirmed inquiry through independently verified public contact;
- Invite Therapist to Claim.

It must not support:
- live availability;
- provider acceptance;
- therapist reservation;
- payment collection;
- confirmed direct booking.

A claimed therapist may enable:
- Inquiry Only;
- Request Approval;
- Instant Confirmation when schedule and required resources are reliable;
- approved External Booking;
- provider manual booking;
- walk-in recording where the practice context supports it.

A claimed profile alone does not enable payment. Identity, booking policies, payout, tax,
financial, service, and contact configuration may still be required.

Direct Practitioner Booking
---------------------------
For practitioner_service booking, the therapist is already selected.

The client may still select:
- independent practice or affiliated establishment;
- provider menu item;
- duration;
- date and time;
- practice location;
- Client Home, Hotel or Accommodation, Corporate or Workplace, Event, Mobile Facility, or
  another supported delivery context;
- travel zone;
- pressure and body-area preference;
- medium and product;
- client-supplied item;
- attire, undressing, treatment contact, and draping preference;
- accessibility and communication preference;
- verified contact;
- multilingual note;
- payment or deposit option.

Direct practitioner_service booking uses Specific Therapist Required unless the client explicitly
leaves the direct-therapist flow. If the user chooses an establishment-first service instead, the
establishment may offer Specific Therapist Required, Specific Therapist Preferred, Provider
Selects Therapist, No Preference, or Select Later according to its policy. Any change that permits
a substitute therapist must be clear before confirmation and must receive the client's explicit
choice.

Only professional contexts that offer the selected service and satisfy current affiliation,
location, client-access, credential or scope, booking, schedule, resource, and travel requirements
may be shown as bookable. Selecting a different context must recalculate and redisplay the
applicable service, price, fees, policies, availability, and booking mode before confirmation.

Capability and Commercial Offering
----------------------------------
practitioner_capability means the therapist can perform a normalized Service.
practitioner_service means the therapist personally sells a provider menu item.

Capability does not prove:
- current booking availability;
- an active independent menu;
- provider acceptance;
- verified credential;
- suitability for every client.

Eligibility, Intake, and Privacy
--------------------------------
The therapist may configure service-specific eligibility rules for independent practice.
An affiliated establishment controls its provider-side rule set for that context.

A privacy-preserving automated result may block an incompatible request before sensitive detail
is disclosed to the therapist.

Eligibility outcomes and their meaning are governed by the Client Intake, Consent, Privacy and
Eligibility specification and must not be redefined by the profile.

The assigned therapist receives only minimum necessary private intake during the permitted
disclosure window. Intake does not become a permanently browsable client health profile.

The client remains able to view retained submitted information subject to applicable access,
safety, legal, and retention limits. Signed snapshots use amendments instead of silent editing.

Home and Mobile Safety
----------------------
Home, hotel, event, and mobile booking may require verified contact, a service address, safe-
workspace information, access and travel details, equipment or treatment support, arrival and
departure check-in, and an emergency contact where justified. These are collected privately
through the booking and safety workflow rather than published on the profile.

The therapist's private residence and habitual travel pattern remain protected.

Schedule and Conflict Prevention
--------------------------------
Availability may combine:
- recurring schedule;
- exception;
- affiliation schedule;
- independent-practice schedule;
- confirmed booking;
- travel;
- leave;
- break;
- external calendar.

The system should prevent double booking across establishments and independent practice.

Public live status must not expose private leave reasons, sensitive client identity, or detailed
travel patterns.

Payment, Cancellation, and Completion
-------------------------------------
The public profile may summarize accepted payment methods, deposit or prepayment requirements,
cancellation and rescheduling limitations, and whether online payment is enabled. The accepted
booking retains the authoritative price and policy snapshots. Detailed payment, refund, payout,
compensation, finance, and completion events remain governed by their booking and finance
specifications.

Provider loyalty, platform rewards, public ratings, public reviews, booking risk, and Reputation
remain separate.

================================================================================
8. PRACTICE LOCATION, SERVICE AREA, AND CONTACT
================================================================================

The profile may show:

- public practice locations;
- affiliated establishment directions;
- independent studio;
- broad service area;
- city, district, province, travel radius, or supported zones;
- home, hotel, corporate, event, maritime, or mobile service;
- travel fees or restrictions;
- supported spoken or signed languages and communication methods, with source and proficiency
  context when known;
- specific accessibility features, adaptations, and known limitations for each public practice
  location or delivery context;
- approved business contact methods.

For home and mobile service:

- show service area rather than the therapist's private address;
- reveal exact appointment details only through an appropriate booking process;
- avoid exposing habitual travel patterns or private residence information.

The therapist chooses which public contact channels are allowed:

- Massage Nexus messaging;
- business phone;
- business email;
- official website;
- approved social account;
- external booking link;
- affiliated establishment contact.

Each action must identify whether the client is contacting the therapist, an independent practice,
an affiliated establishment, or an external booking provider. A verified telephone, email, social
account, or website verifies that channel only; it does not verify identity, affiliation,
credentials, availability, or booking authority.

Accessibility must use specific known features and limitations rather than a generic Accessible
label. Unknown or unconfirmed information must not be presented as No. A therapist's own
disability, health information, or accommodation needs remain private unless the therapist gives
specific informed consent for public disclosure.

================================================================================
9. EXPERIENCE, EDUCATION, AND CREDENTIALS
================================================================================

Each credential should retain:

- credential type and name;
- issuer;
- issuing jurisdiction and scope where applicable;
- issue date;
- expiry date where applicable;
- supporting evidence;
- Credential Verification Status;
- Credential Lifecycle Status;
- verification method and last verification date;
- public-redaction rules;
- source and review history.

Verification Status may be:

- Self-Declared;
- Submitted;
- Verified;
- Needs Review;
- Rejected.

Lifecycle Status may be:

- Active;
- Expired;
- Suspended;
- Revoked;
- Unknown.

The public summary should be derived from individual records, for example:

- 2 verified active credentials;
- 1 submitted credential;
- 1 expired credential.

A single conflicting profile-level credential status should not replace the underlying records.

Supporting evidence and identity-matching material are private by default. A public credential
image, identifier, holder name, signature, address, or other document detail requires a lawful
purpose, publication permission where required, data minimization, and redaction. Rejected,
disputed, or review-pending evidence must not automatically publish as an adverse professional
claim. Public wording must distinguish verified active credentials from submitted, self-declared,
expired, suspended, revoked, unknown, or otherwise unverified claims without overstating the
issuer's authority or geographic scope.

Other professional information may include:

- education and training;
- supervised practice;
- years of experience;
- past affiliations;
- memberships;
- awards;
- interviews;
- teaching or assessor roles;
- public portfolio.

Credentials support trust but do not automatically establish service quality, Provider Rank, or Therapist Service Market Class.

Experience summaries should preserve their basis, source, and relevant dates. Do not calculate or
display a precise number of years from an uncertain start date, ignore material gaps silently, or
present self-declared experience as independently verified.

================================================================================
10. MEDIA AND PUBLIC DOCUMENTS
================================================================================

Public media may include:

- approved profile photo;
- work-related photos;
- demonstrations;
- short videos;
- event or interview media;
- public credential images;
- approved promotional media.

Public media metadata should preserve, when relevant, the owner or uploader, source, permitted-
use basis, represented professional context, capture or publication date, caption, accessible
alternative text, language, moderation state, and last-confirmed date.

Use a neutral placeholder when no approved photo exists.

Media must not:

- expose a client without consent;
- reveal sensitive treatment areas improperly;
- show private documents;
- imply adult services merely through appearance or identity;
- misrepresent another person;
- present unsafe conduct as professional practice.

An establishment may propose media only with appropriate permission. The therapist may request removal or replacement.

Photographs may support a human-reviewed duplicate investigation but must never be the sole merge
evidence. Automated facial recognition or biometric matching requires a separate approved legal,
privacy, security, bias, retention, and consent decision and is not authorized by this profile
guide.

================================================================================
11. REVIEWS, RATINGS, TRUST, AND RELATED CONTENT
================================================================================

The profile may include:

- review summary;
- overall and criteria ratings;
- editorial reviews;
- user reviews;
- therapist responses;
- discussions;
- corrections;
- identity and credential verification;
- source notes;
- last meaningful update;
- affiliated spas;
- related services;
- interviews and articles;
- promotions;
- similar therapists.

Reviews and rating events should preserve the service, professional context, price, location,
disclosure, and date when available. A review of one affiliation must not be presented as a review
of every context in which the therapist works.

The primary therapist rating is the official therapist-target score governed by the Rating System.
Eligible rating events from multiple professional contexts may contribute to that score because
therapist criteria focus on the therapist rather than the establishment environment. The interface
must retain and expose context attribution and may provide context filters or breakdowns. A
therapist-wide rating must not be described as a rating of an establishment, price tier, Provider
Rank, Therapist Service Market Class, credential, or every commercial promise made in each
context. A context breakdown must not be labeled an official score unless the Rating System
defines it as one.

The public summary must show rating count separately from review count and identify the official
score type and material limitations. Editorial reviews, user reviews, private satisfaction,
content reactions, Reputation, and the official rating remain separate signals.

================================================================================
12. SOURCES, VERIFICATION, AND FRESHNESS
================================================================================

The profile must preserve field-level evidence and freshness according to
docs/14-research/data-research-provenance-and-field-verification-system.txt and
docs/02-governance/edit-system.txt.

Relevant properties may include:

- source type, title, link, document, image, interview, or evidence reference;
- source date and observation date;
- contributor or researcher;
- verification method and verifier;
- confidence and verification status;
- last checked and last confirmed dates;
- conflicting observations and dispute state;
- field language and translation context;
- public explanation when useful and safe.

Verification is faceted. The platform may independently verify public identity, authorized claim,
contact channel, affiliation, location or service area, service capability, menu and price,
credential, schedule source, booking configuration, payment readiness, or payout readiness. No one
facet automatically verifies another.

A claimed profile is not necessarily current; an unclaimed profile is not necessarily inaccurate;
a verified contact does not prove professional identity or booking authority; and a credential
must not be represented beyond its issuer, jurisdiction, scope, and validity.

Freshness expectations vary by field. Availability, affiliations, contact channels, prices,
promotions, service menus, credentials with expiry, and booking enablement normally require more
recent confirmation than former names, education, or historical affiliations. Conflicting sources
must remain reviewable and must not be silently resolved by selecting the most convenient value.

================================================================================
13. CREATION, CLAIM, EDITING, AND PERMISSIONS
================================================================================

Public Visitor or Guest
- view, share, compare, report, and suggest corrections when allowed;
- must authenticate before saving, following, reviewing, rating, or performing another action
  reserved for a registered user.

Registered User or Contributor
- save, follow, review, and rate when eligible under the applicable community rules;
- propose a profile, correction, translation, media, source, credential information, duplicate
  report, or merge request.

Claimed Therapist
- manage approved identity, biography, contact choices, services, independent prices, availability, service areas, credentials, media, and affiliation responses;
- confirm or dispute provider titles and ranks;
- submit evidence relating to service market class;
- request correction, merge, inactivity, retirement, or removal review;
- cannot directly rewrite audit history, but may exercise applicable correction, objection,
  restriction, erasure or blocking, safety-suppression, and appeal rights.

Claim-Lock and Impersonation Prevention: An establishment role grants no authority to claim
another person's therapist profile. A person who is both an establishment representative and the
therapist may claim their own profile only through the individual therapist identity-verification
workflow. Profile claims must authenticate the claimant's individual user account and verify the
person-to-profile relationship. This prevents establishments from locking therapists out of their
professional identity or impersonating them to retain bookings and client relationships.

Authorized Establishment Owner or Manager
- manage only the relationship to that establishment;
- submit role, Work Arrangement, exact title, Provider Rank, services, prices, schedule, availability, and booking information for that context;
- cannot control unrelated affiliations or private identity information.

Editor or Moderator
- review claims, identity, credentials, affiliations, titles, ranks, classifications, corrections, duplicate reports, and sensitive changes according to permission.

Administrator
- add, edit, translate, restore, merge, soft-delete, and perform approved cleanup after dependency and safety checks.

================================================================================
14. DUPLICATES, MERGE, INACTIVITY, AND LIFECYCLE
================================================================================

Duplicate review should compare:

- verified identity when available;
- public and former names;
- photographs;
- affiliations and dates;
- service areas;
- contacts;
- credentials;
- education and work history;
- social accounts;
- contributor evidence.

Do not merge people merely because they share a name, workplace, service list, or similar appearance.

A merge should:

- select one canonical profile;
- remove the duplicate from normal discovery;
- redirect or connect the old route when appropriate;
- preserve identity, contributions, revisions, and audit history;
- review each affiliation, credential, review, service, booking, media, and source before transfer;
- stop new activity on the merged record;
- retain enough restricted evidence and audit history to support correction, appeal, and an
  approved unmerge or recovery when a mistaken identity decision is discovered;
- avoid redirecting an old public route when it would expose a sensitive former name, preserve a
  false association, defeat an approved suppression, or create another privacy or safety risk.

Professional availability, practice status, person life status, and record lifecycle remain
separate:

- Temporarily Unavailable is an Availability Status.
- Active, Inactive, Retired, and Unknown are Professional Practice Status values.
- Deceased belongs to separate restricted person life-status handling and may use a respectful
  legacy presentation only after appropriate evidence, review, notification or representation,
  and appeal handling. Do not publish a cause of death through this status.
- Soft deletion is reserved for invalid, fraudulent, duplicate, unsafe, test, unlawfully published, or otherwise unsuitable records.

Hard deletion requires privacy, legal, safety, audit, retention, and dependency review.

Contribution history should retain a limited target snapshot and indicate merge, removal, restoration, or replacement.

A retained audit or dependency record does not require continued public display. Approved
correction, objection, erasure or blocking, safety-suppression, and legal requests may restrict,
delist, de-identify, or remove public information while preserving only records that remain
lawfully necessary.

================================================================================
15. CONFLICT OF INTEREST, SAFETY, AND SENSITIVE CONTENT
================================================================================

A therapist and affiliated representatives must not receive points, Embers, Inklings, Reputation,
or other rewards for self-review, self-rating, vote manipulation, artificial engagement, or
reward-seeking corrections. Any connected-party rating permitted by the Rating System must be
identified as connected, receive only the official influence defined there, and remain subject to
conflict-of-interest and abuse controls. Manipulation remains prohibited.

Official responses, credential submissions, affiliation confirmations, title or rank corrections, and schedule updates may remain allowed when clearly labeled and unrewarded.

The profile must not infer adult or sexual services from:

- gender;
- sexual orientation;
- LGBTQ focus;
- client access;
- home service;
- photos;
- body type;
- nudity policy;
- massage system;
- spa affiliation.

Safety reports, misconduct allegations, legal complaints, precise private location, identity evidence, client medical data, and disciplinary information require restricted moderation workflows. Unverified accusations should not publish automatically.

Publication of substantiated disciplinary or legal information requires an authoritative source,
scope and jurisdiction review, necessity and proportionality review, appropriate notice and
response opportunity, correction and appeal handling, and careful distinction among allegation,
pending proceeding, finding, sanction, reversal, and expiry. Open or private investigations must
not be exposed merely because a report exists.

Professional-boundary guidance and report actions may be shown without turning the profile into an accusation page.

================================================================================
16. THERAPIST WORKSPACE
================================================================================

The Therapist Workspace is a permission-aware context inside the general User Workspace.
Every therapist user remains a client in Personal context.

The public profile specification governs what may appear publicly and what each public state or
action must mean. docs/11-workspaces/therapist-workspace.txt governs private editing interfaces,
delegation, affiliations, credentials, schedules, bookings, messages, private documents,
analytics, payments, finance, and later professional tools. The workspace may update permitted
profile information but must not silently control independent reviews, official ratings,
community contributions, universal Service identity, editorial content, or accepted source
history.

A therapist employed by a spa may see assigned booking and compensation information without
receiving owner-level finance, payroll, tax, or unrelated-client access.

The first public therapist profile should not wait for later financial, scheduling, document,
or portal modules.

Minimum Acceptance Invariants
-----------------------------
- an unclaimed profile cannot create a confirmed Massage Nexus reservation or collect payment;
- direct practitioner_service booking keeps the selected therapist unless the client explicitly
  chooses another flow;
- changing professional context refreshes the eligible service, price, fee, policy, availability,
  and booking presentation before confirmation;
- an unconfirmed or actively disputed affiliation does not appear on the establishment's public
  roster or enter its availability pool;
- a private residence, private contact channel, identity evidence, sensitive intake, client
  identity, or detailed travel pattern never appears through the public profile;
- claim, identity, contact, affiliation, credential, location, booking, and payment verification
  are not collapsed into one global verified state;
- guests cannot submit official ratings, and rating count remains distinct from review count;
- profile suppression can remove public display and indexing without requiring deletion of every
  lawfully necessary restricted audit or dependency record;
- accessibility and state meaning remain available without color, pointer, image, or map use.

Current implementation state (2026-07-20):
The first public therapist profile page is implemented at /therapist/{therapist_slug}
(TherapistProfileController and resources/views/therapist/profile.blade.php), rendering sample
demo records (one claimed booking-enabled profile, one unclaimed profile) from the temporary
Support/Demo/SampleContent provider pending persisted practitioner_main records. The page honors
the minimum acceptance invariants that are observable at this stage: separate claim,
identity-verification, and booking-enablement labels; rating count distinct from review count
with the below-threshold "not enough ratings" state; no booking panel, payment, or private
contact on an unclaimed profile, which instead shows the unclaimed notice and invite-to-claim
route; service-area display without a private address for home service; and keyboard-accessible
tabs and text-labeled states. Reviews and ratings on the page are served by the shared review
domain. The proposed collection structure is recorded in
data/structure_guide/practitioner_main.php.

Current working decisions:
- therapist profile identity is independent of employment;
- stable therapist ID, not username, prevents duplicate records;
- unclaimed therapist profiles cannot receive confirmed Massage Nexus reservations or payment;
- direct practitioner booking does not ask the client to choose another therapist;
- the client may still choose professional context and delivery location;
- practitioner_capability remains separate from practitioner_service and availability;
- therapist-wide official rating may use eligible events from several contexts while preserving
  visible context attribution and avoiding market-class implications;
- sensitive intake access is minimum, assigned, time-limited, and audited;
- a therapist remains a client by default in Personal workspace context.

DOCUMENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
This document defines provider-side financial events generated by bookings, therapist compensation, tips, payouts, manual records, reporting, expense and supplier boundaries, tax-report support, reconciliation, and accounting integration.

Massage Nexus may support provider finance without attempting to replace a complete professional accounting, payroll, banking, or tax system in the first implementation.

================================================================================
1. FINANCIAL EVENT PRINCIPLE
================================================================================

Booking creates factual financial events:
- quote;
- deposit;
- payment;
- balance;
- discount;
- tax;
- travel fee;
- product charge;
- refund;
- chargeback;
- platform fee;
- provider payout;
- therapist compensation;
- tip;
- loyalty redemption;
- manual adjustment.

Financial reporting derives from events. Do not repeatedly rewrite one total without preserving events that changed it.

================================================================================
2. PROVIDER FINANCE EVENT TYPE
================================================================================

Field Name: type_provider_finance_event
Field Label: Provider Finance Event Type
Selection: Single-select

Code | Label                   | Description
-----|-------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------
SAL  | Service Sale            | Revenue from completed service.
DEP  | Deposit                 | Advance amount received.
BAL  | Balance Payment         | Remaining service balance.
PRD  | Product Sale or Upgrade | Product or product-related charge.
TRV  | Travel Charge           | Home, hotel, event, or transport charge.
DSC  | Discount                | Reduction in price.
TAX  | Tax                     | Tax amount classified under provider jurisdiction.
TIP  | Tip                     | Tip amount recorded separately.
REF  | Refund                  | Amount returned.
CHB  | Chargeback              | Payment reversed through payment channel.
COM  | Platform Commission     | Provider-funded platform fee.
CBF  | Client Booking Fee      | Client-paid platform fee.
PYO  | Provider Payout         | Platform settlement to provider.
TCP  | Therapist Compensation  | Amount payable or paid to therapist.
EXP  | Provider Expense        | Approved expense record outside sale.
ADJ  | Adjustment              | Authorized correction or reconciliation.
LOY  | Loyalty Redemption      | Provider loyalty value applied.
VCH  | Voucher or Gift Value   | Voucher, gift card, or provider benefit applied.

================================================================================
3. THERAPIST COMPENSATION METHOD
================================================================================

Field Name: type_therapist_compensation
Field Label: Therapist Compensation Method
Selection: Multi-select per agreement where methods combine

Code | Label                   | Description
-----|-------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------
PCT  | Percentage Commission   | Therapist earns percentage of eligible amount.
FIX  | Fixed Per Service       | Therapist earns fixed amount for completed service.
HRL  | Hourly                  | Therapist earns based on eligible time.
SAL  | Salary                  | Therapist is salaried; booking may produce no direct amount.
TIR  | Tiered Commission       | Percentage or amount changes by threshold.
PRD  | Product Commission      | Therapist earns from eligible product upgrade or sale.
BON  | Bonus                   | Therapist earns condition-based amount.
POL  | Pooled Compensation     | Eligible amount is distributed from pool.
MAN  | Manual                  | Authorized staff enters compensation.
NON  | No Booking-Based Amount | Booking does not generate direct compensation.

Agreement may be establishment-specific and time-bounded.

================================================================================
4. COMPENSATION BASIS
================================================================================

Possible bases:
- service subtotal;
- amount after discount;
- amount before tax;
- amount after tax;
- collected amount;
- completed duration;
- product upgrade;
- tip;
- number of participants;
- therapist role;
- service family;
- therapist tier;
- late-night;
- home-service travel;
- team split.

Rule must state basis, percentage or amount, exclusions, minimum, maximum, rounding, effective date, expiry, provider, branch, therapist, service, currency, and approval.

A booking snapshots applicable compensation rule at acceptance or completion according to provider policy.

================================================================================
5. TEAM AND GROUP COMPENSATION
================================================================================

Where several therapists serve one client, a couple, a group, or a multi-stage service, provider may use equal split, weighted split, role-based amount, time-based amount, primary and assistant, or manual approved allocation.

Total therapist allocation must reconcile with configured compensation pool.

================================================================================
6. TIP DISTRIBUTION
================================================================================

Tip may be assigned to one therapist, split among therapists, pooled for staff, retained for later distribution, or paid directly outside provider custody.

Tip record preserves payer, intended recipient, method, actual recipient, provider custody, split, confirmation, dispute, and tax or payroll classification by jurisdiction.

Service charge is not automatically a tip.

================================================================================
7. PROVIDER FINANCE DASHBOARD
================================================================================

Possible summaries:
- gross booking sales;
- collected;
- outstanding;
- refunds;
- chargebacks;
- deposits;
- tax;
- travel fees;
- product upgrades;
- platform fees;
- provider payout;
- therapist compensation;
- tips;
- discounts;
- loyalty;

- branch;
- therapist;

Dashboard must distinguish booked, confirmed, completed, invoiced, collected, and settled.

================================================================================
8. TAX REPORT SUPPORT
================================================================================

Massage Nexus may preserve taxable line items, preserve tax profile, summarize sales, summarize tax amounts, export transactions, generate provider-configured documents, identify missing invoice configuration, and support accountant access.

Massage Nexus should not claim universal tax correctness, file taxes automatically without approved jurisdiction-specific implementation, infer tax status from public profile, or treat tips and commission identically in every country.

================================================================================
9. EXPENSE AND SUPPLIER BOUNDARY
================================================================================

Future Provider Finance may record oil, linen service, laundry, utilities, rent, staff, supplies, transportation, equipment, repair, advertising, and platform subscription.

These are not part of core Booking document. A booking may create usage or allocation data supporting expense analysis.

Full purchasing, supplier orders, accounts payable, inventory valuation, depreciation, bank reconciliation, and general ledger are separate future systems.

================================================================================
10. INVENTORY CONNECTION
================================================================================

Booking may reserve or consume product unit, oil quantity, disposable garment, towel or linen set, herbal compress, sanitation item, or retail product.

Initial implementation may record product selection without decrementing inventory. Later integration may create usage events on confirmation, check-in, completion, or manual adjustment.

Do not reduce inventory from cancelled draft.

================================================================================
11. RECONCILIATION
================================================================================

Reconciliation compares booking total, payment transactions, refund, payout, provider record, gateway, cash, bank, compensation, and invoice.

Field Name: status_reconciliation
Field Label: Reconciliation Status
Selection: Single-select

Code | Label            | Description
-----|------------------|------------------------------------------------------------
UNR  | Unreconciled     | Event has not been matched.
MAT  | Matched          | Amounts and references agree.
PRT  | Partially Matched| Only part was matched.
MIS  | Mismatch         | Amount, currency, or reference differs.
REV  | Under Review     | Authorized review is in progress.
ADJ  | Adjusted         | Approved adjustment resolved difference.
CLS  | Closed           | Reconciliation is complete.

Manual records require evidence and actor.

================================================================================
12. ACCOUNTING EXPORT
================================================================================

Possible exports include booking sales, payments, refunds, tax, payouts, therapist compensation, tips, expenses, and reconciliation.

Formats include CSV, spreadsheet, JSON, PDF statement, and accounting software connector later.

Exports preserve date, document, account mapping where configured, direction where supported, currency, tax, reference, branch, therapist, and provider.

================================================================================
13. FINANCIAL ACCESS
================================================================================

Possible permissions:
- view booking price;
- record payment;
- verify payment;
- issue refund;
- view payout;
- manage payout account;
- view therapist compensation;
- edit compensation rule;
- approve adjustment;
- view tax profile;
- issue invoice;
- export finance;
- reconcile.

A receptionist may collect payment without seeing therapist compensation or provider payout.

High-risk actions may require recent authentication, reason, second approval, notification, and audit.

================================================================================
14. MANUAL OPERATIONS
================================================================================

The system supports early manual operation:
- manually confirm bank transfer;
- manually record cash;
- manually settle provider;
- manually pay therapist;
- manually upload invoice;
- manually adjust discrepancy.

Manual workflow must remain structured and auditable. Manual does not mean unrecorded.

================================================================================

- payout;

- accounting integration.

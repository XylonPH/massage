DOCUMENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
This document defines the private intake, eligibility, consent, access, retention, translation, printing, and safety-information direction for Massage Nexus bookings and walk-in sessions.

The system helps providers identify whether a requested service may proceed, needs adaptation, requires clarification, or should be declined. It does not convert an ordinary massage therapist or spa into a medical clinic and must not imply diagnosis or treatment outside documented professional scope.

================================================================================
1. SEPARATE DATA AREAS
================================================================================

Ordinary booking data:
- provider;
- service;
- schedule;
- participant;
- therapist;
- price;
- policies;
- contact.

Preference data:
- pressure;
- focus area;
- avoid area;
- products;
- attire;
- draping;
- conversation;
- accessibility.

Private intake data:
- pregnancy;
- illness;
- skin integrity;
- injury;
- surgery;
- medication-related caution;
- intoxication or impaired consent;
- allergies;
- current symptoms;
- other sensitive suitability information.

Provider observation:
- answers confirmed;
- measurement;
- visible concern;
- adaptation;
- acceptance or decline;
- referral note;
- signature.

These areas must not be collapsed into one unrestricted booking document.

================================================================================
2. ELIGIBILITY BEFORE PROVIDER DISCLOSURE
================================================================================

A provider may configure structured eligibility rules.

Examples:
- provider does not accept pregnant clients for this service;
- provider accepts pregnancy only with an approved service or condition;
- provider does not accept clients with active fever or contagious symptoms;
- provider does not perform a service over active skin lesions;
- provider requires manual review after recent surgery;
- provider requires a guardian for a minor;
- provider or facility is limited to a permitted client access group;
- service requires a particular professional credential or referral.

Field Name: status_booking_eligibility
Field Label: Booking Eligibility Result
Selection: Single-select per participant service line

Code | Label                    | Description
-----|--------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------
ELG  | Eligible                 | Request may proceed under configured rules.
ADP  | Eligible with Adaptation | Request may proceed with a permitted modification.
DSC  | Disclosure Required      | Minimum necessary information must be shared with an authorized provider role.
REV  | Manual Review            | Authorized provider review is required before submission or confirmation.
RFR  | Referral Recommended     | Provider cannot accept under ordinary workflow and recommends another service or professional.
INE  | Ineligible               | Request cannot proceed for the selected provider, service, or context.
ANS  | Answer Required          | Required eligibility question was not answered.
UNS  | Uncertain                | Available answers do not support a reliable automated result.

Privacy-preserving rejection:
- required answers are evaluated through the authorized eligibility service;
- when the result is Ineligible, the booking is not submitted to the provider;
- the provider receives no booking and no sensitive reason;
- the client receives a clear but minimally revealing explanation and may be offered alternatives;
- an internal audit records the rule result without exposing unnecessary health detail to the provider.

Example:
"This provider cannot accept the selected service under the information supplied. Your sensitive answer was not sent to the provider."

================================================================================
3. ELIGIBILITY RULE STRUCTURE
================================================================================

A rule may specify provider, branch, service, service variant, delivery context, client access group, intake question, accepted answer, outcome, alternative service, message, language, effective date, expiry date, legal or professional source, reviewer, and verification status.

Rules must be enforced server-side. The browser may provide immediate guidance, but client-side logic alone is insufficient.

Rules must not silently discriminate based on irrelevant identity. Client demographic or sex-related access rules should be used only where establishment, facility, service, privacy arrangement, safety policy, or applicable law legitimately requires them.

================================================================================

Field Label: Intake Answer

Code | Label             | Description
-----|-------------------|------------------------------------------------------------
YES  | Yes               | Statement applies.
NO   | No                | Statement does not apply.
UNS  | Unsure            | Client cannot confidently answer.

================================================================================
7. REUSABLE DEFAULTS AND PER-BOOKING CONFIRMATION
================================================================================

Reusable client preferences may include pressure, focus and avoid areas, preferred medium, fragrance sensitivity, conversation preference, attire and draping preference, accessibility needs, language, and therapist-selection preference.

Time-sensitive intake must be reconfirmed, including current fever, active skin lesion, intoxication, recent recreational-drug use affecting safety, recent injury or surgery, pregnancy status when relevant, medication change, recent meal, or current unusual pain, numbness, or swelling.

Every booking displays saved date, last confirmed date, expiry or reconfirmation requirement, Change, and Confirm No Change.

A stale default must never silently answer a time-sensitive safety question.

================================================================================
8. PROVIDER INTAKE AND OBSERVATIONS
================================================================================

Authorized staff may record intake reviewed, answer clarified, client statement, visible concern, affected area, agreed adaptation, service accepted, service changed, service postponed, service declined, referral, measurement, client signature, practitioner signature, and witness.

Field Name: type_provider_intake_outcome
Field Label: Provider Intake Outcome
Selection: Single-select

Code | Label             | Description
-----|-------------------|------------------------------------------------------------
ACP  | Accepted          | Service proceeds as requested.
ADP  | Adapted           | Service proceeds with documented modification.
CHG  | Service Changed   | Client accepts a different service.
PST  | Postponed         | Service is deferred.
DCL  | Declined          | Provider does not perform the service.
RFR  | Referred          | Client is directed to another provider or professional.
STP  | Stopped at Intake | Check-in or intake ends before service begins.

Blood pressure, pulse, temperature, or another measurement is optional and provider-configured. The system should not encourage collection without equipment, training, scope, need, and privacy controls.

================================================================================
9. ROLE-BASED ACCESS
================================================================================

Client
- may view submitted answers and consent snapshots;
- cannot alter a signed historical snapshot;
- may add an amendment or correction;
- may revoke future reuse where allowed;
- may download or request deletion subject to retention rules.

Receptionist
- sees booking logistics and eligibility result;
- sees only the minimum prompt needed for check-in;
- does not automatically see full sensitive history.

Spa owner or general operator
- sees aggregate and operational status;
- does not automatically see detailed sensitive intake;
- may configure templates and retention policy through authorized privacy permission.

Assigned therapist
- sees the minimum necessary intake and preferences for the assigned participant;
- access begins at a provider-defined point before service;
- access expires after the service and documentation window;
- later access requires authorized reason.

Administrator
- technical access is restricted, audited, and used only for approved support, security, legal, or incident reasons.

The client should not be locked out of their own information. They may be prevented from editing a signed snapshot, but they retain the right to see what they submitted unless an exceptional legal restriction applies.

10. TIME-LIMITED DISCLOSURE
================================================================================

Field Label: Sensitive Disclosure Status
Selection: Single-select

Code | Label            | Description
-----|------------------|------------------------------------------------------------
SEA  | Sealed           | Data exists but is not visible to provider staff.
ELG  | Eligibility Only | Only the eligibility result is visible.
REL  | Released         | Minimum necessary detail is visible to authorized roles.

LGL  | Legal Hold       | Deletion or access rules are controlled by a documented hold.
DEL  | Deleted          | Data was deleted according to policy.

Possible release window:
- after provider acceptance;
- after therapist assignment;
- a configured number of hours before service;
- at check-in;
- until a configured period after completion.

================================================================================
11. PROVIDER RETENTION POLICY
================================================================================

Every booking-enabled provider must choose or accept a retention policy allowed by Massage Nexus and applicable jurisdiction.

Retention may differ for ordinary booking contact, private intake, signed consent, payment record, tax invoice, incident report, minor or guardian record, and clinical or regulated service.

Providers must agree not to disclose private information outside authorized purpose, use intake for marketing, browse unrelated client history, copy data into uncontrolled personal devices or chats, or retain local copies beyond policy.

================================================================================
13. PRINTING, SIGNATURE, AND PAPER WORKFLOW
================================================================================

Supported outputs include blank intake form, prefilled intake form, consent form, provider observation sheet, combined booking and intake summary, amendment form, and incident attachment.

Supported execution includes digital signature, typed acknowledgment, checkbox plus recent authentication, stylus signature, paper signature, scanned or photographed signed paper, and witness signature.

Every signed version stores form version, policy version, language, answer snapshot, signature method, signed_at, signer, witness, source device, uploaded copy, hash, and amendment list.

A signature documents disclosure and consent. It does not automatically eliminate provider responsibility or excuse negligent, unsafe, unlawful, or out-of-scope conduct.

================================================================================
14. TRANSLATION OF INTAKE
================================================================================

Structured intake questions use approved translations. Free-text safety notes display both original and translation.

Safety-critical translation rules:
- original text remains visible;
- automatic translation is labeled;
- provider may request clarification;
- client may approve a translation;
- an interpreter may be recorded;
- uncertain translation may force Manual Review;
- automatic translation alone must not produce a diagnosis.

================================================================================
15. PRIVACY-PRESERVING ANALYTICS
================================================================================

Approved aggregates may count eligibility rejections, common requested adaptations, form completion, language use, provider response time, cancellation after eligibility, and intake question abandonment.

Analytics must not expose identifiable health answers, rare conditions linked to a person, therapist access history to unauthorized operators, cross-provider client intake, or client identity in benchmark reports.

================================================================================
16. IMPLEMENTATION PRIORITIES
================================================================================

Initial:
- provider intake template;
- required questions;
- eligibility outcome;
- sealed private answer store;
- client view;
- assigned therapist limited access;
- consent snapshot;
- print or PDF.

Later:
- rule engine;
- provider-specific alternatives;
- translated free text;
- time-limited access;
- measurement records;
- guardian workflows;
- incident hold;
- advanced anonymized analytics.

DOCUMENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
This document defines the complete working direction for booking and appointment handling in Massage Nexus.

It also contains the formerly separate Booking Loyalty, Rewards, Gamification and Post-Service Engagement specification and the Booking Database Structure specification. These subjects are integral parts of the booking system and are maintained in this single document rather than as separate numbered files.

The system uses a reusable appointment engine, but its initial behavior is deliberately massage-, spa-, bodywork-, beauty-, and wellness-aware. It must not be reduced to a generic date, time, name, and note form.

The booking system consumes normalized Service information and provider-specific establishment_service or practitioner_service information. It also coordinates client preferences, therapist assignment, group participation, eligibility screening, consent, scheduling, resources, price computation, policies, communication, payment, history, rewards, and post-service activity.

This document defines planning decisions and implementation boundaries. It is not a public booking policy, legal agreement, tax specification, complete database schema, or promise that every provider will activate every feature.

================================================================================
1. CORE PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

1. Client-centered
   A booking begins with what the client or booking party wants to receive.

2. Provider- and resource-aware
   A request becomes confirmable only when the provider can supply the required service, therapist, room, equipment, location, time, preparation, and capacity.

3. Service-driven
   The normalized Service and provider menu item determine which questions, options, cautions, products, supports, and arrangements are relevant.

4. Massage-first depth
   Massage Nexus supports detailed pressure, body-area, therapist, oil, draping, attire, product, and safety preferences that generic booking systems normally omit.

5. Service-flexible scope
   The same engine may support other approved Massage Nexus services such as reflexology, body treatments, facials, nail care, hair and scalp care, grooming, hydrotherapy, thermal wellness, bathing, mind-body sessions, and consultations.

6. Privacy by design
   Sensitive intake data is separated from ordinary booking data, shown only to authorized people, retained only as justified, and never exposed merely because a profile exists.

7. Multilingual by design
   Structured options use controlled translations. Free-text notes preserve the original and may include a clearly labeled translation.

8. Claim- and verification-aware
   The system must distinguish unclaimed, claimed, verified, and fully booking-enabled provider profiles.

9. Snapshot-based
   Prices, policies, service configuration, translations, consent text, and selected options are preserved as they existed when the booking was accepted.

10. Progressive disclosure
    A client who wants a fast booking can answer only required questions. A client who wants detailed customization can expand additional sections.

11. Human override with audit
    Authorized staff may adapt, approve, reject, or correct a request, but important changes require reason, timestamp, actor, and notification.

12. Complete planning, staged implementation
    The full system is documented now even when implementation is divided into later roadmap phases.

3. PROVIDER BOOKING ELIGIBILITY
================================================================================

A public profile may exist before it is claimed. Public visibility does not automatically grant booking authority.

A. Unclaimed provider
- client may create a Personal Appointment Plan;
- client may privately record past Service History;
- client may send an unconfirmed inquiry only through an independently verified public contact route;
- Massage Nexus may offer an Invite Provider to Claim action;
- no live availability;
- no appointment reservation;
- no payment collection;
- no implication that the provider accepted, reserved, or even received the request.

Required notice:
"This provider has not claimed or configured booking through Massage Nexus. This entry is not a confirmed appointment unless the provider confirms it directly."

B. Claimed but not booking-verified
- authorized operator or therapist account is linked;
- request-and-approval booking may be enabled after contact verification;
- provider must publish or accept booking policies;
- payment and payout remain disabled until required financial and identity verification is complete.

C. Claimed, verified, and booking-configured
- live or managed availability;
- therapist assignment;
- request or instant confirmation;
- deposits or full payment when enabled;
- automated reminders;
- cancellation and refund handling;
- completion and post-service workflow.

Field Name: status_provider_booking_enablement
Field Label: Provider Booking Enablement Status
Selection: Single-select

Code | Label                    | Description
-----|--------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------
OFF  | Disabled                 | Provider does not use Massage Nexus booking.
INQ  | Inquiry Only             | Provider accepts inquiries but no structured reservation.
REQ  | Request Approval         | Every booking requires provider approval.
INS  | Instant Confirmation     | Eligible requests may confirm immediately from live capacity.
EXT  | External Booking         | Massage Nexus redirects or synchronizes with an approved external booking system.
SUS  | Suspended                | Booking is temporarily disabled because of policy, verification, safety, dispute, or operational concern.

4. BOOKING PARTY AND SERVICE PARTICIPANTS
================================================================================

A booking may contain one booking party and one or more service participants.

Booking Party
- person responsible for creating the booking;
- may be a participant or may book for companions, dependents, guests, family members, or a group;
- receives the primary confirmation unless another contact is explicitly assigned;
- accepts booking-level terms and financial responsibility when applicable.

Service Participant
- person receiving one or more services;
- has an individual service line, intake, preferences, therapist assignment, price, consent, and completion status;
- may use a limited guest identity when no Massage Nexus account exists.

Each participant may select:
- normalized Service;
- provider menu item;
- duration or variant;
- requested date and time;
- therapist preference;
- pressure and body-area preferences;
- product and medium choices;
- attire and draping preferences;
- accessibility needs;
- intake answers;
- language;
- consent;
- price and payer.

Field Name: type_participant_relationship
Field Label: Participant Relationship to Booking Party
Selection: Single-select per participant

Code | Label              | Description
-----|--------------------|------------------------------------------------------------
SELF | Self               | Booking party is the participant.
CMP  | Companion          | Participant is accompanying the booking party.
FAM  | Family Member      | Participant is a family member.
DEP  | Dependent          | Participant depends on the booking party for consent, support, or payment.
GST  | Guest              | Participant is a guest without another specified relationship.
GRD  | Guardian-Supported | Participant requires guardian involvement.
ORG  | Organization Guest | Participant is booked through an employer, event, hotel, or organization.

A group booking must not be represented only by one guest count. Each service participant needs an individual service line when the service, therapist, duration, intake, price, or consent differs.

================================================================================
13. MULTILINGUAL BOOKING
================================================================================

Structured booking options use translation keys.

Free-text notes preserve original text, original language, translated text, target language, translation method, translation timestamp, translation warning or confidence, and client-approved translation where available.

Display rule:
- always preserve the original;
- show automatic translation as a separate labeled block;
- never silently replace safety-critical original text;
- allow provider clarification through structured translated questions;
- record which language version of provider policy the client accepted.

Suggested public value statement:
"Book in your language. Let the provider receive your service preferences in theirs."

================================================================================
17. ANTI-ABUSE AND BOOKING RISK
================================================================================

The system may detect repeated no-shows, repeated last-minute cancellations, suspicious simultaneous bookings, mass requests to competing providers, invalid contact, payment failures, identity manipulation, harassment, provider-side false completion, competitor interference, or reward farming.

Booking risk is internal. Providers should normally receive an operational requirement such as deposit required, manual approval required, contact re-verification, booking limit, or temporary block rather than unrelated private history.

Do not automatically publish a client's cancellation history or reduce public Reputation merely because a booking was cancelled.

================================================================================
18. EDUCATION, ONBOARDING, AND SUPPORT
================================================================================

Massage Nexus should provide client and provider tutorials, contextual tips, onboarding checklists, Help Center articles, Massage Campus lessons, short videos, recorded sessions, downloadable setup guides, and a demo booking environment.

Complexity should be absorbed by defaults, templates, progressive disclosure, and education rather than deleting valuable capability.

================================================================================
19. COMPETITIVE VALUE AND PLATFORM RETENTION
================================================================================

Massage Nexus remains valuable even when a provider has an internal system because it supports one client history across many providers, therapist identity across affiliations, normalized Services and products, multilingual communication, portable preferences, budgeting, analytics, discovery, reviews, ratings, reputation, claim and verification, loyalty, consent, and external integrations.

A provider's internal system primarily serves that provider. Massage Nexus must serve the client, therapist, establishment, seller, contributor, and wider knowledge base together.

Therapist profiles remain independent of employment. A claimed therapist may receive a unique public username or stable public identifier. Establishments connect to that existing identity rather than create duplicate therapist records.

22. SEPARATION OF SYSTEMS
================================================================================

Provider Loyalty
- controlled by provider;
- rewards repeat business with that provider;
- may use visits, spend, service count, package, stamps, or credits.

Massage Nexus Rewards
- platform-wide;
- Nexus Points;
- Embers;
- Inklings;
- badges;
- rank-path progress;
- user statistics.

Reputation
- trust quality;
- must not be purchased;
- must not automatically rise merely because a booking occurred.

Rating and Review
- experience feedback;
- separate from provider loyalty and platform currency.

25. REWARD ANTI-ABUSE
================================================================================

Do not reward:
- Personal Appointment Plan;
- unconfirmed inquiry;
- draft;
- duplicate booking;
- same event imported repeatedly;
- provider-created fake completion;
- cancelled appointment;
- unverified manual history;
- self-dealing arrangement designed only for rewards;
- refunded event where service did not occur.

Controls:
- same client, provider, therapist, service, and time-window checks;
- completion verification;
- payment signal where applicable;
- provider connection check;
- reward cap;
- reversal;
- audit;
- risk review.

A provider cannot sell Reputation or rating weight through a booking benefit.

================================================================================
31. PRIVATE SATISFACTION SURVEY
================================================================================

Private survey may ask service met expectation, comfort, pressure, communication, cleanliness, privacy, likelihood to return, issue requiring contact, and private comment.

Private survey:
- is visible to authorized provider roles;
- is not automatically public;
- does not automatically become testimonial;
- may be anonymous to ordinary staff while linked internally;
- may trigger service recovery;
- may be withheld from public Reputation and rating systems.

Provider must obtain permission before publishing private response.

================================================================================
32. PUBLIC RATING AND REVIEW
================================================================================

Completed booking may support spa rating, therapist rating when known, product rating when eligible, review linked to booking, and verified-experience label.

One completed event must not multiply rewards through repeated rating deletion and resubmission.

A poor provider execution does not automatically redefine normalized Service.

================================================================================
33. REPUTATION BOUNDARY
================================================================================

Booking completion alone does not prove trust.

Possible Reputation effects:
- valid review;
- accurate rating behavior;
- constructive report;
- reliable attendance over time only as small conduct signal;
- abuse or fraud after review.

Do not publicly label a client as unreliable based only on cancellations.
Internal booking risk and public Reputation remain separate.

================================================================================
34. SERVICE RECOVERY
================================================================================

Negative private survey may create provider follow-up, apology, re-service offer, credit, refund review, safety review, moderation report, or no-contact preference.

A service-recovery benefit must not require removal of honest review or positive rating.

================================================================================
35. AFTERCARE
================================================================================

Aftercare may come from normalized Service, provider, therapist, product, clinical source, or editorial source.

Store source, language, booking, participant, sent_at, acknowledgment, expiry, and emergency caution.

Aftercare is informational and must not exceed provider scope.

================================================================================
36. REBOOKING
================================================================================

Rebooking may offer same provider, same therapist, same service, saved preferences, new schedule, loyalty reward, or series continuation.

Rebooking reconfirms current price, availability, policy, time-sensitive intake, therapist affiliation, and product choices.

A prior booking is a template, not automatic authorization.

- completion event;

- shared reward event.

Later:

================================================================================
PART III. BOOKING DATABASE STRUCTURE
================================================================================

This part defines the working document-store collection model for Booking and its integrated
loyalty, reward, engagement, history references, scheduling, payment, finance, privacy,
translation, audit, and anti-abuse structures.

It is a planning structure rather than executable validation code. Exact BSON types, Laravel
models, indexes, encryption, transactions, migrations, and API resources remain implementation
tasks.

================================================================================
38. DOCUMENT-STORE DESIGN RULES
================================================================================

Embed when:
- data belongs only to one parent booking;
- list size is naturally bounded;
- data is normally read with booking;
- separate lifecycle and access are unnecessary;
- independent query is not important.

Use separate collection when:
- data is sensitive with different access or retention;
- one parent may create unbounded events;
- independent query, scheduling, finance, or reporting is required;
- data is reusable across bookings;
- independent lifecycle or versioning exists;
- several bookings reference same rule, policy, resource, or program.

Use stable identifiers. Technical names use singular snake_case. Public labels may use Therapist while technical references may use practitioner_id where required by accepted entity model.

================================================================================
69. REWARD AND GAMIFICATION INTEGRATION
================================================================================

The Booking System does not directly mutate Nexus Points, Embers, Inklings, badge, rank, or Reputation totals.

On qualifying event, Booking sends or creates shared reward event containing:
- source_type: booking;
- booking_id;
- participant_key;
- service_line_key;
- actor_user_id;
- provider_context;
- event_type;
- qualification;
- value suggestion;
- anti_abuse_key;
- occurred_at.

Shared Rewards system validates, caps, awards, reverses, and updates balances and milestone progress.

Reputation remains governed by Reputation System and does not rise merely because booking completed.

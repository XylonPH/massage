================================================================================
1. MAINTENANCE MODE
================================================================================

Massage Nexus enters maintenance mode when:
- the intended public foundation is released;
- the roadmap’s core launch stages are implemented;
- ordinary operations no longer depend on continuous foundational construction;
- remaining work is primarily improvement, expansion, correction, optimization, and support.

Maintenance mode does not mean development stops.

Maintenance includes:
- publishing;
- moderation;
- data research;
- provider and user support;
- security;
- dependency updates;
- bug fixes;
- performance;
- refactoring;
- documentation;
- translation;
- analytics;
- market research;
- product improvement;
- new compatible features.

Large new systems return to normal planning and implementation workflow rather than being
treated as routine maintenance.

================================================================================
2. OPERATING PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

- Keep the platform useful, current, safe, and technically supported.
- Automate repeatable work, but retain human review where trust, safety, law, or quality
  requires it.
- Do not publish low-quality content merely to satisfy a numerical schedule.
- Preserve backups and rollback before significant changes.
- Patch urgent security issues immediately.
- Update public data according to field freshness rather than one profile-level date.
- Use analytics and surveys to prioritize work.
- Refactor before code, tests, prompts, or documentation become unmanageable.
- Record maintenance work only at the level needed for accountability and continuation.
- Do not expose internal technology, vulnerabilities, or operational details publicly.

3. CONTENT PUBLISHING CADENCE
================================================================================

Minimum editorial targets after the publishing workflow is stable:

Daily:
- at least one article;
- at least one Massage Nexus comic;
- social posts adapted from current content, research, profiles, or community activity;
- moderation of newly submitted public content;
- review of scheduled publication failures.

The daily article and comic are publishing targets, not permission to release incomplete,
unsafe, repetitive, inaccurate, or poorly translated work.

Maintain:
- a rolling 30-day article backlog;
- a rolling 30-day comic backlog;
- emergency replacement content;
- evergreen content;
- timely content;
- market-specific content;
- publication status and source revision;
- translated versions according to readiness.

Content balance should include:
- service education;
- spa and therapist discovery;
- field research;
- safety and boundaries;
- reviews and experiences;
- provider operations;
- massage culture and history;
- wellness tourism;
- products;
- accessibility;
- data and statistics;
- community;
- The Resting Leaf and other approved comics.

4. SOCIAL MEDIA OPERATIONS
================================================================================

Primary social channels are defined in the Marketing and Growth Strategy.

Maintenance responsibilities:
- maintain consistent account names, bios, links, logos, and contact information;
- publish frequently enough for each platform;
- adapt content rather than post identical material everywhere;
- respond to messages and comments;
- moderate harassment, spam, impersonation, solicitation, and prohibited content;
- monitor account security;
- archive reusable assets and captions;
- track post source, campaign, language, and performance;
- correct outdated promotions and links;
- preserve access recovery and administrator ownership;
- review platform policy changes.

Working cadence after the team and backlog can support it:

Daily:
- one to three posts across each primary active platform, adapted to platform format;
- Stories or short updates when useful;
- replies and moderation.

Weekly:
- three to five short-form videos;
- one longer video or substantial visual feature when production capacity permits;
- one research or data update;
- one provider, therapist, contributor, or community spotlight;
- one newsletter or weekly digest after newsletter launch.

Do not create platform accounts that cannot be maintained.

================================================================================

- reviews;
- ratings;
- discussions;
- comments;
- profiles;
- corrections;
- messages where policy permits review;
- images;
- videos;
- animated media;
- advertisements;
- provider responses;
- translations;
- AI-generated content.

Daily:
- triage urgent reports;
- remove or quarantine clearly prohibited content;
- review spam and automated abuse;
- respond to safety escalations;
- review high-risk AI flags;
- preserve required evidence.

Weekly:
- review unresolved queues;

- identify repeated abuse patterns;
- adjust filters and rate limits;
- review appeals and response times.

Monthly:
- review rule effectiveness;
- review moderator consistency;

Urgent categories:
- minor safety;
- exploitation or trafficking;
- non-consensual intimate content;
- threats;
- doxxing;
- account takeover;
- payment fraud;
- credible security incidents.

- operating status;
- hours;
- contact details;
- prices;
- service menus;
- therapist rosters;
- facilities;
- accessibility;
- map positions;
- branch relationships;
- social accounts;

Automated maintenance may:
- identify stale fields;
- create research assignments;
- compare official sources;
- detect broken links;
- identify conflicting values;
- identify missing localities;
- suggest possible duplicates;
- summarize candidate changes.

AI and automation do not automatically verify:
- active status;
- price;
- credential;

- interview;

Field review cadence should depend on volatility:
- fast-changing fields: frequent review;
- moderately changing fields: periodic review;
- stable historical fields: review when contradicted or corrected.

Public pages should display meaningful last-checked or last-verified information.

11. TECHNICAL UPDATES
================================================================================

Maintain supported versions of:
- PHP;
- Laravel;
- MongoDB integration;
- database services;
- Apache or production web service;
- Node.js and frontend packages;
- Livewire;
- Alpine.js;
- Tailwind CSS;
- Vite;
- Filament where used;
- Tiptap;
- mobile frameworks later;
- cloud services;
- external APIs;
- testing tools.

Update process:
1. review release and security notes;
2. verify compatibility;
3. create backup and recoverable Git state;
4. update development environment;
5. run targeted tests;
6. run full automated tests;
7. run manual critical-flow checks;
8. test migration and rollback;
9. deploy to staging;
10. monitor;
11. deploy to production;
12. record the final implemented change.

Urgent security patches may use an accelerated process but still require backup, testing, and
rollback.

Do not remain on unsupported versions merely to avoid maintenance work.

Do not upgrade every dependency immediately without compatibility and benefit review.

================================================================================
12. REFACTORING
================================================================================

Refactor when:
- a file mixes several responsibilities;
- repeated bugs occur in the same area;
- tests become difficult to target;
- a change requires opening many unrelated files;
- code complexity blocks safe modification;
- performance degrades;
- duplicate business logic appears;
- an AI repeatedly consumes excessive context for one task;
- a package or architecture is no longer appropriate.

Refactoring rules:
- preserve behavior unless behavior change is explicitly planned;
- add or strengthen tests before risky restructuring;
- split by coherent responsibility;
- avoid excessive fragmentation;
- update documentation and implementation records;
- measure performance before and after when performance is the reason.

================================================================================
13. DOCUMENTATION MAINTENANCE
================================================================================

Review documentation when:
- implementation changes;
- policy changes;
- terminology changes;
- files become difficult to edit;
- AI repeatedly loads irrelevant content;
- references become stale;
- a document reaches the size review threshold;
- an inventory grows independently from its governing rules.

Actions:
- preserve current accepted decisions;
- remove obsolete concepts;
- archive superseded OneDrive versions;
- use Git history after repository migration;
- split large files by responsibility;
- separate catalogs, article ideas, prompt libraries, and source lists from governing
  specifications;
- update related references;
- keep internal files English-only;
- keep public translations synchronized by source revision.

Working Notes remain user-maintained unless explicitly supplied.

16. BACKUP, RESTORE, AND BUSINESS CONTINUITY
================================================================================

Maintain backups for:
- databases;
- media;
- source code;
- configuration;
- documentation;
- translation resources;
- research evidence;
- critical financial records.

Rules:
- encrypt;
- restrict access;
- retain according to policy;
- maintain an independent copy;
- verify completion;
- test restoration;
- document recovery order;
- protect keys;
- test disaster scenarios;
- ensure deletion obligations are reflected in backup rotation.

Git is not the backup for user data or uploaded media.

================================================================================
17. INCIDENTS AND CORRECTIONS
================================================================================

Incident types:
- outage;
- data corruption;
- security incident;
- privacy breach;
- payment failure;
- mass email or notification error;
- incorrect public statistic;
- harmful content exposure;
- broken booking flow;
- failed migration;
- social account compromise.

Response:
1. detect;
2. assess severity;
3. contain;
4. preserve evidence;
5. communicate internally;
6. restore service;
7. notify affected people or authorities when required;
8. correct public information;
9. document final cause and remediation;
10. prevent recurrence.

Public corrections should identify material errors without exposing sensitive internal detail.

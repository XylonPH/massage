================================================================================
1. PURPOSE
================================================================================

The Support System helps:
- visitors understand site functionality;
- users resolve account, profile, review, discussion, language, privacy, and booking questions;
- contributors submit and track corrections;
- providers and therapists manage claims and profiles;
- advertisers and paying customers receive campaign, billing, and account support;
- people report bugs, complaints, abuse, security concerns, and accessibility problems;
- staff identify recurring questions, documentation gaps, and defects.

Support is separate from public Discussions, Reviews, private Messages, emergency services,
formal legal representation, and medical advice.

4. AI SUPPORT ASSISTANT
================================================================================

The AI Support Assistant uses:
- approved public support articles;
- approved policy explanations;
- user-visible feature documentation;
- current service-status information;
- authorized account context;
- provider or advertiser plan details visible to the requester;
- approved troubleshooting procedures.

Process:
1. identify intent;
2. determine requester context and authorization;
3. search approved current knowledge;
4. answer directly when confidence is sufficient;
5. ask only necessary clarifying questions;
6. provide steps and links;
7. state limitations honestly;
8. create or escalate a ticket when action or human judgment is needed;
9. summarize the conversation into the ticket.

AI must not:
- invent functionality;
- promise refund, credit, ranking, verification, restoration, or campaign performance;
- reveal private data without authorization;
- expose internal technology or security details;
- provide legal or medical conclusions;
- close serious complaints automatically;
- mark a bug fixed without verified deployment.

================================================================================
5. AI SELF-SERVICE OR ESCALATION
================================================================================

AI may answer without a human when:
- approved guidance covers the question;
- the answer is informational;
- no sensitive account action is required;
- no financial adjustment is requested;
- no serious complaint, safety, privacy, security, or moderation issue is involved;
- no policy discretion is required.

Examples:
- How do I add a spa?
- How do I translate a comment?
- Where do I see saved providers?
- What image formats are accepted?
- Where do I see campaign statistics?

Create or escalate a ticket when:
- documented steps fail;
- a bug or technical problem is reported;
- account ownership is disputed;
- data needs manual correction;
- a provider claim needs review;
- billing, refund, chargeback, or credit needs action;
- an advertisement is rejected or disputed;
- content or moderation is contested;
- harassment, exploitation, doxxing, fraud, or prohibited content is reported;
- a privacy or security request is made;
- current documentation does not cover the issue;
- AI confidence is insufficient.

================================================================================
6. ESCALATION LEVELS
================================================================================

Level 0: Automated Information
- FAQ;
- feature guidance;
- public status;
- basic troubleshooting.

Level 1: General Support
- ordinary account questions;
- basic provider assistance;
- non-sensitive data correction;
- basic advertiser guidance.

Level 2: Specialist Support
- billing;
- campaign;
- booking;
- payment;
- provider verification;
- technical investigation;
- translation;
- accessibility;
- complex correction.

Level 3: Trust, Safety, Privacy, or Security
- harassment;
- exploitation;
- minors;
- doxxing;
- account takeover;
- identity dispute;
- privacy request;
- serious moderation appeal;
- security report;
- payment fraud.

Level 4: Owner or Authorized Decision
Required when:
- a new policy precedent is created;
- a major commercial settlement or exception is requested;
- a permanent platform-wide decision is required;
- significant legal or reputational risk exists;
- pricing, scope, or public commitments may change;
- no approved rule authorizes a lower-level decision.

Owner escalation contains a concise decision summary, relevant evidence, risk, options, and
requested action rather than an unorganized transcript.

10. PRIORITY
================================================================================

Critical:
- active compromise;
- privacy breach;
- data corruption;
- payment duplication;
- outage;
- immediate-danger prohibited content;
- privileged account takeover.

High:
- core workflow unavailable;
- advertiser spending incorrectly;
- paid provider cannot use paid operations;
- booking or payment failure;
- serious harassment or impersonation;
- major accessibility blocker.

Normal:
- functionality question;
- isolated bug with workaround;
- data correction;
- campaign setup question;
- translation or display issue.

Low:
- suggestion;
- cosmetic defect;
- wording;
- low-impact inconsistency.

Paying status may affect response targets but never lowers safety priority for non-paying
users.

- promotion-review explanation;
- statistics interpretation;

Entitlements must be documented and visible.

================================================================================
12. COMPLAINT HANDLING
================================================================================

Complaint types:
- service;
- provider;
- therapist or staff;
- advertiser;
- support conduct;
- moderation;
- billing;
- discrimination or accessibility;
- privacy;
- content;
- platform conduct.

Process:
1. acknowledge;
2. identify requested outcome;
3. classify urgency and sensitivity;
4. preserve evidence;
5. separate fact from opinion;
6. avoid retaliation;
7. assign an impartial reviewer where needed;
8. gather records;
9. provide updates;
10. decide under current policy;
11. explain the result;
12. provide appeal or escalation;
13. record systemic improvement.

Do not promise confidentiality beyond what the process can provide.
Do not disclose the complainant unnecessarily.

================================================================================
13. TECHNICAL PROBLEMS AND BUGS
================================================================================

Request:
- platform;
- page or feature;
- date and time;
- steps;
- expected result;
- actual result;
- screenshot or recording where safe;
- browser, device, and version where relevant;
- repeatability;
- public error reference;
- urgency and impact.

Triage:
1. search known issues and guidance;
2. reproduce with safe data;
3. check status and logs;
4. identify guidance, configuration, data, external service, or software defect;
5. provide workaround;
6. create or link internal development work;
7. inform requester without exposing internals;
8. verify fix;
9. close or monitor.

A support ticket is not automatically a GitHub Issue.

Create a private GitHub Issue when:
- a reproducible defect exists;
- technical investigation requires development;
- an accepted feature request needs work;
- refactoring is required;
- documentation or tests must change with code.

Only necessary sanitized support data enters GitHub.

================================================================================
14. SECURITY AND PRIVACY
================================================================================

Security reports:
- use a dedicated restricted route;
- bypass public discussion;
- preserve evidence;
- never create a public Issue automatically;
- receive urgent review;
- follow coordinated disclosure when adopted.

Privacy requests:
- may include access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, withdrawal, portability,
  and complaint;
- receive process guidance from AI;
- require approved identity and legal workflow for final action;
- must not request excessive identity evidence.

- knowledge recommendation;

- AI draft response;
- escalation suggestion;

================================================================================
17. RESPONSE TARGETS
================================================================================

Suggested initial targets:
- critical safety or security: immediate acknowledgment and urgent human review;
- high-impact paid-service failure: same operating day where possible;
- ordinary paid support: one operating day;
- ordinary user support: two operating days;
- low-priority suggestion: acknowledgment without guaranteed personal response.

Do not publish targets until staffing and monitoring can support them.

22. CURRENT DIRECTIONS
================================================================================

- Provide a public Support Center and authenticated ticket system.
- Use AI for repeated questions and documented feature guidance.
- Escalate unresolved, sensitive, financial, policy, safety, privacy, security, moderation,
  complaint, and technical matters.
- Route precedent-setting decisions to the owner or another authorized decision-maker.
- Convert confirmed defects into sanitized private GitHub Issues.
- Give paying advertisers and providers defined support without overriding trust and safety.
- Use recurring questions to improve guidance and software.

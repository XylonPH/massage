DOCUMENT PURPOSE
----------------
This document defines the data-protection architecture and the content required in the
future public Massage Nexus Privacy Notice.

It is not yet the final public notice.

Before publication, insert:
- exact personal information controller;
- Data Protection Officer or privacy contact;
- legal and business address;
- final processors and vendors;
- final purposes and lawful bases;
- exact retention periods;
- international-transfer arrangements;
- data-subject request procedure;
- complaint and escalation channels;
- launch jurisdictions;
- effective date and version.

================================================================================
1. PRIVACY PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

Massage Nexus should follow:
- transparency;
- legitimate purpose;
- proportionality;
- data minimization;
- purpose limitation;
- accuracy;
- privacy by design and by default;
- security appropriate to risk;
- restricted access;
- retention limitation;
- accountability;
- user control;
- protection of sensitive personal information;
- documented incident response.

Consent is not the only possible basis for processing. The final notice must identify the
appropriate basis for each purpose and must not ask for unnecessary consent when another
lawful basis applies.

When consent is used, it must be specific, informed, freely given, demonstrable, and
withdrawable where applicable.

================================================================================
2. CONTROLLER AND NETWORK ACCOUNT STRUCTURE
================================================================================

The final notice must explain:
- who controls the shared network account;
- who controls Massage Nexus site-membership data;
- whether one legal entity controls both;
- whether future network sites act as the same controller, joint controllers, or separate
  controllers;
- which universal settings are reused;
- which data remains site-specific;
- whether enforcement or fraud information may be shared across participating sites.

Registering through Massage Nexus creates:
- a shared network account; and
- a Massage Nexus membership.

It does not automatically create public profiles on every future network site.

Universal information may include:
- username;
- verified contacts;
- security credentials;
- language;
- time zone;
- universal avatar;
- security events and account recovery information.

Massage Nexus-specific information may include:
- public profile;
- massage preferences;
- bookings;
- service history;
- reviews;
- contributions;
- rewards;
- provider roles;
- mature-content preference;
- Massage Nexus restrictions.

================================================================================
3. DATA CATEGORIES
================================================================================

3.1 Registration and Identity Data
- username;
- date of birth;
- age-eligibility result;
- public display name when provided;
- private legal identity when needed for a specific verified function;
- account and membership statuses;
- policy-acceptance records.

3.2 Contact Data
- email address;
- email-verification status;
- mobile number when added;
- mobile-verification status;
- contact-change history where justified;
- communication preferences.

3.3 Authentication and Security Data
- password hash, never plaintext password;
- external identity-provider identifiers;
- passkey public credential information;
- TOTP configuration data protected appropriately;
- recovery-code verifiers;
- session and device records;
- login times;
- failed attempts;
- approximate IP and network information;
- risk signals;
- security-event and recovery history;
- administrative security actions.

Massage Nexus does not need the user's biometric template for passkeys. Face, fingerprint,
or device-PIN verification remains with the device or authenticator.

3.4 Profile and Preference Data
- avatar and cover references;
- biography;
- profile visibility;
- languages;
- time zone;
- regional formats;
- accessibility settings;
- interests;
- notification settings;
- saved and followed items;
- blocked users;
- public and private profile fields.

3.5 Location Data
- general home or preferred search area;
- manually entered location;
- location permission state;
- approximate or precise device location only when an approved feature requires it;
- booking, service, delivery, or provider location;
- map and route interactions where logged.

Precise location should not be collected during basic registration.

3.6 Booking and Service Data
- booking party and participants;
- selected services;
- provider;
- therapist preference;
- date and time;
- delivery context;
- booking status;
- communications;
- cancellation;
- payment status;
- loyalty and post-service actions.

3.7 Sensitive Intake and Consent Data
- health-related intake;
- contraindications;
- injury or condition information;
- medication or allergy information where requested;
- pregnancy information where relevant;
- accessibility and assistance needs;
- guardian or support-person information where applicable;
- service consent;
- body-area, draping, contact, and boundary preferences.

Sensitive intake must remain separate from ordinary profile, booking, analytics, and public
content. Access must be limited to authorized persons for an approved time and purpose.

3.8 Content and Community Data
- reviews;
- ratings;
- discussions;
- comments;
- corrections;
- contributions;
- moderation history;
- reports;
- appeals;
- translations;
- sources;
- reaction and reputation activity.

3.9 Media and Document Data
- images;
- videos;
- animated profile media;
- captions;
- accessibility text;
- technical metadata;
- moderation and NSFW classification;
- storage and processing metadata;
- ownership, release, or consent evidence where required.

3.10 Communication Data
- messages;
- notifications;
- support requests;
- provider inquiries;
- security notices;
- email and SMS delivery records;
- communication preferences.

3.11 Payment and Commerce Data
- amount;
- currency;
- booking or order reference;
- payment-provider token or reference;
- payment status;
- refund;
- chargeback;
- payout;
- tax and financial-document information;
- limited fraud signals.

Avoid storing full payment-card details. Use approved payment-provider tokenization.

3.12 Provider and Professional Data
- business identity;
- practitioner identity;
- qualifications;
- claims and verification evidence;
- affiliations;
- roles;
- permissions;
- schedules;
- financial and payout information;
- managed records;
- professional moderation and dispute history.

3.13 Technical and Usage Data
- browser and device type;
- operating-system family;
- application version;
- request logs;
- security logs;
- error identifiers;
- cookie and session data;
- performance;
- page and feature use;
- search and interaction history where approved;
- referral and campaign information.

3.14 AI and Translation Data
- prompt or input sent to an approved AI provider;
- output;
- provider;
- purpose;
- human-review status;
- translation source and output;
- retention and opt-out controls where applicable.

Do not send private intake, secrets, payment credentials, or restricted information to a
general AI provider without an accepted, necessary, protected processing arrangement.

================================================================================
4. PURPOSES OF PROCESSING
================================================================================

Purposes may include:
- create and secure an account;
- verify eligibility and contacts;
- provide shared network settings;
- activate Massage Nexus membership;
- personalize language, time zone, accessibility, and content;
- provide directory and discovery features;
- process bookings;
- support provider operations;
- provide reviews, ratings, discussions, and contributions;
- manage claims and verification;
- process payments and refunds;
- send operational, verification, booking, and security communications;
- provide support;
- prevent fraud, impersonation, abuse, and unauthorized access;
- moderate content;
- meet legal obligations;
- preserve evidence and resolve disputes;
- measure performance and improve the service;
- provide approved AI, search, and translation functions;
- maintain backups and business continuity;
- enforce Terms and policies.

The final notice must map each data category and purpose to an appropriate lawful basis.

================================================================================
5. REQUIRED, OPTIONAL, AND INFERRED DATA
================================================================================

The interface should label:
- required registration data;
- optional profile data;
- required booking data;
- optional booking preferences;
- sensitive intake;
- automatically suggested settings;
- inferred or derived results;
- public information;
- private information.

Browser language and time zone may be suggested automatically, but users must be able to
change them.

Location permission must be contextual and optional unless a selected function genuinely
cannot operate without it. A manual alternative should be provided when practical.

================================================================================
6. PUBLIC AND PRIVATE DATA
================================================================================

Public by design may include:
- username;
- approved display name;
- public avatar;
- public biography;
- published reviews and contributions;
- public provider affiliations;
- selected public badges and reputation information.

Private by default includes:
- email;
- mobile number;
- date of birth;
- password hash;
- passkeys;
- recovery information;
- active sessions;
- precise location;
- saved items;
- followed items and following relationships;
- most history;
- booking details;
- intake;
- private messages;
- payment and payout information;
- security logs;
- identity evidence.

A field is not public merely because it is stored in a public-profile document. Server-side
authorization and explicit visibility rules are required.

================================================================================
7. EMAIL, SMS, AND NOTIFICATIONS
================================================================================

Operational messages may include:
- verification;
- password reset;
- security alert;
- booking confirmation;
- cancellation;
- refund;
- provider response;
- policy update;
- account action.

Marketing communication requires separate handling and an appropriate basis.

Security notices may not be fully disabled.

Local development may use:
- Laravel log mail;
- Mailpit or another local-only mail catcher;
- development-only OTP logging.

Production email may use Azure Communication Services Email or another approved provider.
Production SMS requires a separately approved provider, sending identity, country coverage,
cost, consent, rate-limit, and abuse plan.

The notice must identify provider categories and material processing.

================================================================================
8. COOKIES AND SIMILAR TECHNOLOGIES
================================================================================

The final notice or separate cookie notice must explain:
- essential session cookies;
- remember-me cookies;
- security cookies;
- preference cookies;
- analytics;
- advertising or campaign cookies if introduced;
- third-party embeds;
- consent choices where required;
- retention;
- how to withdraw or change choices.

Essential authentication and security cookies should not be presented as optional when the
requested service cannot operate without them.

Do not introduce advertising trackers merely because the application can support them.

================================================================================
9. SHARING AND PROCESSORS
================================================================================

Potential recipient or processor categories:
- cloud hosting;
- object storage;
- email and SMS;
- social identity providers;
- payment processors;
- maps and geolocation;
- search services;
- analytics;
- content delivery and security;
- customer support;
- moderation;
- AI and translation providers;
- professional advisers;
- government or law-enforcement recipients when legally required;
- establishments, therapists, sellers, or booking participants as required for the selected
  transaction.

The final notice must:
- identify important named providers where appropriate;
- explain the purpose;
- limit provider access;
- use contracts and security controls;
- prohibit unauthorized secondary use;
- document subprocessors;
- review provider changes.

Massage Nexus must not sell personal data to advertisers.

Personalized advertising, data brokerage, or cross-site tracking requires a separate
explicit decision and privacy review before introduction.

================================================================================
10. INTERNATIONAL AND CROSS-BORDER PROCESSING
================================================================================

Cloud, communication, identity, AI, payment, and support providers may process information
outside the Philippines.

Before production:
- map storage and processing locations;
- identify applicable transfer requirements;
- use approved contractual and organizational safeguards;
- assess recipient-country and provider risk;
- disclose material cross-border processing;
- preserve user rights;
- document provider and subprocessor locations where required.

================================================================================
11. RETENTION
================================================================================

Create a formal retention schedule before public launch.

The schedule must separately cover:
- abandoned registrations;
- unverified accounts;
- verification and reset tokens;
- active and expired sessions;
- login and security logs;
- account records;
- policy-acceptance evidence;
- public profile;
- deleted profile;
- booking and financial records;
- intake and consent;
- messages;
- reviews and contributions;
- moderation evidence;
- provider claims and verification;
- media originals and derivatives;
- backups;
- support records;
- legal holds;
- analytics.

Retention should be based on:
- service need;
- legal obligation;
- contract;
- security;
- dispute;
- fraud prevention;
- accounting;
- user expectations;
- data sensitivity.

Do not retain everything indefinitely.

Deletion may mean:
- delete;
- anonymize;
- detach from the account;
- restrict;
- archive under legal hold;
- expire from backups according to backup rotation.

The final notice must explain important exceptions to deletion.

================================================================================
12. DATA-SUBJECT RIGHTS
================================================================================

The final notice must explain applicable rights, which may include:
- information;
- access;
- correction;
- objection;
- erasure or blocking;
- withdrawal of consent where consent is the basis;
- data portability where applicable;
- complaint;
- damages and other rights provided by law;
- review of certain automated decisions where applicable.

Provide:
- request method;
- identity-verification process;
- expected response process;
- authorized-agent procedure;
- exceptions;
- appeal or complaint route;
- National Privacy Commission complaint information where appropriate.

Do not require more identity data than reasonably necessary to verify a request.

================================================================================
13. CHILDREN AND AGE
================================================================================

Massage Nexus membership is 18+.

The public notice should state:
- ordinary public content may be viewed without registration;
- the platform does not knowingly activate Massage Nexus membership for a person under 18;
- an age-ineligible registration is rejected;
- a correction process exists for an entered birth date;
- future network sites may have different age and guardian rules;
- child-oriented services in the directory do not mean a child can create a Massage Nexus
  account.

If a future site permits minors, create a separate child and guardian privacy specification
before launch.

================================================================================
14. ACCOUNT SECURITY AND BIOMETRICS
================================================================================

Passwords are stored as one-way hashes.

Massage Nexus should support passkeys rather than storing facial or fingerprint templates.

The notice should clarify:
- device biometric verification remains on the user's device or authenticator;
- Massage Nexus receives public-key credential information and authentication results;
- the platform does not receive the raw fingerprint or face template from standard WebAuthn
  passkeys.

Mobile numbers are vulnerable to recycling and SIM swap. SMS is not the sole recovery
method.

================================================================================
15. LOCATION PRIVACY
================================================================================

Location controls should distinguish:
- manually selected area;
- approximate location;
- precise location;
- booking or service address;
- provider location;
- background location;
- imported location.

Initial web registration must not require precise location.

The notice and interface should explain:
- when location is collected;
- whether it is one-time or continuous;
- how long it is retained;
- who receives it;
- how to revoke permission;
- how to use a manual alternative.

Massage Nexus does not initially require background location tracking.

================================================================================
16. SENSITIVE INTAKE
================================================================================

Sensitive client intake requires stricter rules:
- separate storage or access boundary;
- minimum necessary questions;
- service-specific purpose;
- assigned therapist access only when necessary;
- limited receptionist and owner visibility;
- time-limited access;
- no ordinary marketing use;
- no public exposure;
- no cross-provider client-history exposure;
- retention and deletion rules;
- audit where appropriate;
- protected export;
- careful translation;
- no unreviewed AI diagnosis or eligibility decision.

Eligibility rejection should disclose only the minimum necessary result to the provider.

================================================================================
17. MEDIA, ANIMATED AVATARS, AND PEOPLE DEPICTED
================================================================================

Uploaded images and videos may contain personal data.

Requirements:
- confirm uploader rights;
- obtain consent or releases where required;
- provide reporting and removal processes;
- remove unnecessary location metadata where appropriate;
- classify NSFW level;
- moderate;
- create safe thumbnails;
- protect originals;
- handle faces and identifiable persons carefully;
- comply with the separate NSFW policy.

Public avatars and cover images must not be NSFW.

Animated avatars:
- are optional;
- require transcoding and moderation;
- must have a poster image;
- must support reduced motion;
- must not expose bystanders or private settings without authority.

================================================================================
18. AI, PROFILING, AND AUTOMATED PROCESSING
================================================================================

Before an AI or profiling system processes personal data:
- document purpose;
- identify data;
- assess necessity;
- assess risk;
- identify provider and transfer;
- define human review;
- prevent unauthorized training or reuse;
- define retention;
- provide notice and controls where required;
- conduct privacy impact assessment when appropriate;
- register automated decision-making or profiling where legally required.

AI may assist moderation, translation, classification, search, or support. It must not:
- expose private intake;
- infer sensitive identity unnecessarily;
- make an unreviewed high-impact decision;
- permanently ban a person without required human process;
- diagnose a user.

================================================================================
19. SECURITY INCIDENTS AND BREACHES
================================================================================

Massage Nexus must:
- document security incidents and personal data breaches;
- maintain an incident register;
- investigate;
- contain and remediate;
- assess notification duties;
- notify the National Privacy Commission and affected persons when legally required;
- preserve evidence;
- submit required reports;
- learn from incidents;
- avoid misleading silence.

The public notice should explain how users may report a privacy or security concern.

================================================================================
20. PRIVACY GOVERNANCE
================================================================================

Before public launch:
- identify the personal information controller;
- designate the appropriate privacy or DPO function;
- determine NPC registration obligations;
- register required data processing systems and automated decision-making or profiling;
- complete data inventory and flow maps;
- conduct privacy impact assessments;
- maintain processing records;
- approve retention schedule;
- approve incident procedure;
- approve data-subject request procedure;
- approve vendor contracts;
- train authorized personnel;
- separate development, test, and production data;
- prohibit real personal data in ordinary automated tests.

================================================================================
21. NOTICE DESIGN
================================================================================

The public Privacy Notice should be:
- readable;
- layered;
- available from registration;
- available without logging in;
- mobile friendly;
- accessible;
- versioned;
- translated according to the accepted language plan;
- specific about what, how, and why data is collected;
- accompanied by contextual notices near sensitive collection.

Do not hide important information inside one enormous legal paragraph.

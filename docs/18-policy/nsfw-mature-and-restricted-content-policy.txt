DOCUMENT PURPOSE
----------------
This document defines how Massage Nexus classifies, stores, displays, restricts, moderates,
and reports mature, NSFW, adult, intimate, sexual-wellness, sensitive-service, and prohibited
content.

Massage Nexus is a massage, spa, wellness, and bodywork discovery platform. It is not
designed as an adult-content platform. Accurate documentation of sensitive services,
experiences, boundaries, risks, and media may still require neutral classification and
controlled access.

A content label does not legalize, excuse, or permit prohibited material.

2. CORE PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

- Ordinary public browsing should not unexpectedly expose NSFW material.
- Mature and NSFW content is hidden by default.
- Massage Nexus membership is 18+.
- Email verification and an active membership are required before restricted access.
- Access to NSFW content requires an explicit user choice.
- A self-declared birth date is ordinary eligibility evidence, not necessarily strong age
  verification.
- Stronger age assurance may be required by risk, law, jurisdiction, payment provider, or
  content type.
- Search engines and public previews should not index or expose NSFW content.
- Sensitive-service taxonomy may remain neutral and complete even when public visibility is
  restricted.
- Educational, clinical, safety, and documentary context matters.
- Consent and rights of depicted persons are required.
- Minor-related sexual content, exploitation, trafficking, non-consensual content, and
  illegal activity are prohibited.
- NSFW classification is not a substitute for moderation.
- Users need reporting and appeal routes.
- Privacy and safety override engagement or advertising goals.

3. CLASSIFICATION LEVELS
================================================================================

Working field:
level_nsfw

Default:
N

A missing level_nsfw is interpreted as N only when the content type and workflow permit a
safe default. User-uploaded media may remain unpublished until classification completes.

N - NONE / ORDINARY PUBLIC
- ordinary massage, spa, wellness, business, educational, or community content;
- no mature sexual theme;
- no nudity beyond approved ordinary context;
- suitable for general public discovery;
- may appear in search, thumbnails, notifications, feeds, and previews.

M - MATURE CONTEXT
- non-explicit discussion of sexual wellness, intimate boundaries, sensual or adult service
  terminology, anatomy, abuse prevention, harassment, or other mature subject;
- educational, clinical, documentary, safety, legal, or review context;
- no explicit sexual act;
- may require warning or adult membership depending on presentation and jurisdiction;
- hidden from child-oriented or broad incidental surfaces.

A - NSFW / ADULT RESTRICTED
- adult-only text or media that is lawful and relevant but inappropriate for ordinary public
  display;
- non-explicit adult nudity or strongly sexualized context only when later approved by
  moderation and legal rules;
- detailed descriptions of intimate or adult services;
- access restricted to eligible verified adult members who explicitly opt in;
- excluded from public indexing, previews, ordinary notifications, and broad recommendations.

R - HIGHLY RESTRICTED
- retained for moderation, legal, safety, research, evidence, dispute, or authorized
  professional handling;
- not ordinarily available to members;
- access limited by role, purpose, jurisdiction, and audit;
- may include content removed from publication but preserved under hold.

P - PROHIBITED
- must not be published or made available as an allowed NSFW item;
- remove, quarantine, preserve evidence, report, or escalate according to law and policy.

4. PROHIBITED CONTENT
================================================================================

Prohibited includes:
- sexual content involving a minor or a person presented as a minor;
- age-ambiguous sexual depiction;
- grooming or sexual solicitation of a minor;
- child sexual abuse material;
- trafficking or exploitation;
- non-consensual sexual content;
- voyeurism or hidden-camera sexual content;
- sexual violence presented for gratification;
- intimate media shared without permission;
- sexual deepfake or deceptive sexual depiction of a real person;
- coercion;
- extortion;
- blackmail;
- doxxing;
- personal contact information used for sexual solicitation;
- illegal sexual service solicitation;
- content facilitating a crime;
- bestiality;
- necrophilic sexual content;
- sexual harassment;
- provider or client bait-and-switch solicitation;
- malware or deceptive links;
- content that violates rights in a depicted person;
- explicit sexual-activity media during the initial platform policy unless a later dedicated
  policy, legal review, age-assurance system, processor approval, and moderation capability
  explicitly authorize a narrow category;
- any content prohibited by applicable law, hosting provider, payment provider, or approved
  platform policy.

A prohibited item must not become allowed merely by choosing level A.

================================================================================
6. ACCESS CONTROL
================================================================================

6.1 Public Visitors

Public visitors may access level N content.

They should not receive:
- NSFW thumbnails;
- explicit snippets;
- search-result previews;
- autoplay media;
- sensitive notifications;
- hidden metadata that reveals restricted content.

6.2 Pending or Unverified Accounts

Pending or unverified users have no NSFW access.

They cannot use authenticated content preferences because they cannot enter the normal User
Workspace.

6.3 Active Adult Members

Possible access to M or A requires:
- active Massage Nexus membership;
- verified email;
- age eligibility of at least 18;
- explicit NSFW or mature-content preference;
- acknowledgment of an age and content warning;
- jurisdictional permission;
- no account restriction that removes access.

6.4 Stronger Age Assurance

Self-declared date of birth is not government-ID verification.

Stronger age assurance may later be required for:
- higher-risk visual media;
- a jurisdiction with a stronger requirement;
- a payment or distribution provider requirement;
- repeated age-related risk;
- a dedicated adult-only feature.

Stronger assurance must be privacy-preserving and separately specified before implementation.

6.5 Staff Access

Moderator, legal, safety, and security access must be:
- role-limited;
- purpose-limited;
- auditable;
- protected from casual browsing;
- supported by staff-wellbeing procedures where necessary.

7. USER PREFERENCE AND DISPLAY
================================================================================

NSFW is hidden by default.

User settings may include:
- allow mature context;
- allow NSFW restricted content;
- blur restricted thumbnails;
- require click-to-reveal every time;
- remember reveal choice for the session;
- exclude NSFW from search;
- exclude from recommendations;
- exclude from history display;
- hide from shared-device surfaces.

The interface should:
- display a content warning before reveal;
- avoid explicit text in the warning itself;
- provide a back or cancel action;
- respect reduced motion;
- not autoplay;
- not preload unnecessary restricted media;
- avoid exposing content in page titles, browser notifications, email subject lines, lock
  screens, or generic calendars.

================================================================================
8. CONTENT-TYPE RULES
================================================================================

8.1 Articles
- level N may be public;
- level M may be public with a warning or member-restricted depending on detail;
- level A requires verified adult membership and opt-in;
- explicit images require separate media approval;
- educational context must be clear;
- SEO metadata and public summaries must remain safe;
- translations inherit or increase restriction, never silently reduce it.

8.2 Reviews
- a review may discuss an adult or sensitive experience neutrally;
- explicit unnecessary detail may be edited, restricted, or removed;
- private identities and contact details must be removed;
- solicitation is prohibited;
- the review's access level may differ from the reviewed profile's ordinary public level;
- provider responses follow the same policy.

8.3 Discussions and Comments
- require topic-level classification;
- replies may increase the required level;
- thread previews remain safe;
- adult solicitation, harassment, and contact exchange are prohibited;
- moderation and reporting must be prominent.

8.4 Images and Videos
- classify before publication;
- use safe poster and thumbnail;
- remove unnecessary metadata;
- confirm rights and consent;
- no incidental person without authority;
- no unapproved explicit activity;
- no public URL for restricted originals;
- no search indexing;
- no unrestricted download;
- access checks on every request.

8.5 Avatar and Cover Media
- must be level N;
- must not contain NSFW nudity, sexualized display, solicitation, or restricted material;
- animated avatars follow the same rule;
- a user may not use an NSFW avatar even when their account permits restricted content.

8.6 Advertising and Promotions
- ordinary advertising surfaces must be level N;
- no deceptive adult-service advertising;
- no NSFW creative in broad feeds;
- no targeting based on sensitive sexual or health inference without a separately approved
  lawful policy;
- payment and advertising-provider restrictions apply.

8.7 Messages
- private messaging is not exempt from law or abuse policy;
- consensual adult conversation is not automatically public content;
- harassment, exploitation, illegal solicitation, non-consensual media, and prohibited
  content remain prohibited;
- automated scanning or moderation of private messages requires a separate privacy and
  proportionality decision;
- reports may allow users to submit relevant message evidence.

9. CLASSIFICATION AND MODERATION WORKFLOW
================================================================================

Possible workflow:
1. creator or uploader selects a content level and reason;
2. server applies type-specific minimum restrictions;
3. automated tools may flag risk;
4. item remains pending when classification is uncertain;
5. human moderator reviews higher-risk material;
6. system generates safe derivatives;
7. access, indexing, search, and notification settings are applied;
8. reports and appeals remain available;
9. later edits trigger reclassification when material changes.

Automated classification:
- may assist;
- must not be the only authority for serious enforcement;
- must not infer protected characteristics unnecessarily;
- must preserve the original for authorized review where lawful;
- must not downgrade a restriction without evidence.

11. SEARCH, INDEXING, RECOMMENDATION, AND API
================================================================================

Level A and R content:
- no public search-engine indexing;
- no public sitemap;
- no open-graph image;
- no explicit social preview;
- no public RSS content;
- no public API payload;
- no unauthenticated CDN path;
- no broad recommendation;
- no default autocomplete suggestion;
- no explicit analytics label visible to unauthorized staff.

Authorized APIs must enforce the same:
- membership;
- verification;
- age;
- preference;
- jurisdiction;
- role;
- content status;
- record authorization.

================================================================================
12. LOCATION AND JURISDICTION
================================================================================

Content legality and access may vary by country, region, or locality.

The system may apply:
- geoblocking;
- jurisdiction-specific warning;
- booking prohibition;
- payment prohibition;
- advertising prohibition;
- search exclusion;
- stronger age assurance;
- complete removal.

Location-based restrictions should not expose precise user location unnecessarily.

A VPN or uncertain location does not create permission. The application should fail safely
when the legal or policy result cannot be determined for a high-risk action.

================================================================================
13. MEDIA STORAGE AND PRIVACY
================================================================================

Restricted media:
- stored outside public document root;
- kept in private or restricted object storage;
- referenced by opaque identifiers;
- delivered through authorized requests or short-lived protected URLs;
- protected from public CDN caching;
- excluded from public backup exports where inappropriate;
- logged when sensitive access justifies audit;
- removed from caches after access expires;
- retained or deleted according to policy and legal hold.

Do not expose:
- raw storage path;
- object-storage account;
- internal container;
- local server path;
- original file name when unsafe;
- embedded GPS metadata when unnecessary.

================================================================================
14. CONSENT AND RIGHTS OF DEPICTED PEOPLE
================================================================================

An uploader must have authority to upload media.

Depending on content, require:
- confirmation of ownership;
- confirmation all depicted adults consented;
- model or participant release;
- provider authorization;
- evidence of age;
- evidence of professional or educational context;
- confirmation that no minor appears;
- confirmation the content was not secretly recorded;
- confirmation that editing or AI generation is disclosed.

A person depicted may report:
- lack of consent;
- impersonation;
- deepfake;
- privacy violation;
- harassment;
- inaccurate identity;
- continued publication after consent withdrawal where applicable.

================================================================================
15. REPORTING, ENFORCEMENT, AND APPEAL
================================================================================

Report categories:
- minor or age concern;
- non-consensual content;
- exploitation or trafficking;
- illegal solicitation;
- harassment;
- doxxing;
- privacy violation;
- wrong NSFW classification;
- unblurred preview;
- explicit content;
- misleading edit or deepfake;
- copyright or ownership;
- prohibited advertising;
- other urgent safety concern.

Possible actions:
- immediate hide;
- quarantine;
- restrict;
- reclassify;
- remove;
- preserve evidence;
- disable download;
- suspend uploader;
- lock messaging;
- refer to legal or safety review;
- report where legally required.

Appeals should:
- protect reporters;
- protect depicted persons;
- avoid redistributing the content;
- use trained reviewers;
- record reasons;
- not expose sensitive evidence to unauthorized parties.

================================================================================
16. NOTIFICATIONS AND COMMUNICATION
================================================================================

Notifications concerning restricted content must use neutral wording.

Do not place explicit detail in:
- email subject;
- SMS;
- push notification;
- lock-screen preview;
- calendar event;
- generic activity feed;
- provider reception display.

Example safe wording:
"Content you submitted requires review."

Not:
"Your explicit adult massage image was rejected."

================================================================================
17. ANALYTICS
================================================================================

NSFW analytics should be:
- aggregated;
- access-limited;
- purpose-limited;
- protected from user re-identification;
- excluded from ordinary advertiser targeting;
- careful with small groups;
- separated from private health or sexual inference.

Do not create an advertising profile that infers sexual behavior or sensitive health status
from restricted-content access without an explicit later legal and product decision.

================================================================================
18. AI AND TRANSLATION
================================================================================

AI may assist:
- classification;
- safe-summary generation;
- moderation triage;
- duplicate detection;
- translation;
- thumbnail risk detection.

Requirements:
- preserve restriction level;
- do not send prohibited or highly sensitive material to an unapproved provider;
- do not use private content for model training without an approved basis;
- require human review for serious enforcement;
- label AI-generated sexual or intimate depictions;
- prohibit sexual deepfakes of real persons;
- do not translate a euphemism into a more explicit public preview;
- do not reduce a restriction merely because a translation appears milder.

================================================================================
19. INITIAL RELEASE BOUNDARY
================================================================================

For the first implementation:
- level N public content is supported;
- level M classification and warnings should be supported;
- level A textual content may be supported only after verified-adult access, opt-in, routing,
  indexing, moderation, and reporting are implemented;
- level A images and videos remain unpublished until secure restricted-media processing,
  consent checks, moderation, safe derivatives, storage, and legal review are operational;
- explicit sexual-activity media remains prohibited unless a future dedicated policy
  explicitly changes this;
- level R is internal only;
- level P is prohibited;
- avatars and cover images remain level N only.

Do not launch an NSFW upload option merely because the database has a level_nsfw field.

22. ACCEPTED DECISIONS SUMMARY
================================================================================

- NSFW content is not publicly displayed by default.
- Access requires an active, verified, adult Massage Nexus membership and explicit opt-in
  where the content is allowed.
- Massage Nexus membership is 18+.
- Public visitors can still use the ordinary directory and articles.
- Articles, reviews, discussions, images, videos, and other content carry classification.
- Sensitive taxonomy remains neutral and complete even when public access is restricted.
- NSFW avatars and cover images are prohibited.
- Explicit sexual-activity media is not approved for the initial platform.
- Restricted media requires secure storage and per-request authorization.
- Search engines, notifications, previews, and broad recommendations must not leak NSFW
  content.
- Minor-related, exploitative, non-consensual, trafficking, illegal solicitation, and sexual
  deepfake content is prohibited.
- AI may assist but does not make the sole final serious moderation decision.
- Article Writing, Review Writing, User Workspace, Site Structure Map, Features, and media
  specifications must later be synchronized with this policy.

Title: Massage Nexus Account Identity Registration and Authentication System
Document Version: 1.70
Date Created: 2026-07-18
Revision Date: 2026-07-22

DOCUMENT PURPOSE
----------------
This document defines the accepted account, identity, registration, verification, login,
recovery, session, external-provider, mobile-number, passkey, preference, and avatar
direction for Massage Nexus.

It is an implementation specification and planning authority. It is not a public Terms of
Use document, Privacy Notice, database schema, route map, or completed interface design.

================================================================================
1. ACCOUNT ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================

1.1 Shared Network Account

Massage Nexus is the first active website in a planned network of sites. Authentication
should therefore be designed so that one person can eventually use the same account for
Massage Nexus, Place Trace, People Atlas, Telebisyon.net, and other approved network sites.

The shared network account may contain:
- global username;
- password credential;
- linked external identities;
- verified email addresses;
- verified mobile numbers;
- preferred interface language;
- fallback language;
- time zone;
- regional formatting preferences;
- universal avatar or identity media;
- security settings;
- passkeys;
- two-factor authenticators;
- recovery codes;
- active sessions and recognized devices;
- account-level security history.

1.2 Site Membership

A network account does not automatically create a public profile or active membership on
every network site.

Registration through Massage Nexus creates:
1. one shared network account; and
2. one Massage Nexus site membership.

A future visit to another network site may reuse universal settings, but the user must still:
- satisfy that site's age and eligibility rules;
- accept that site's current Terms of Use;
- acknowledge that site's current Privacy Notice;
- create or activate that site's membership;
- configure genuinely site-specific preferences.

1.3 Universal and Site-Specific Settings

The authoritative profile, preference, Settings-interface, avatar, cover, and detailed booking-
preference boundaries are defined in docs/07-accounts/user-profile-and-preference-system.txt.
This section preserves the account-architecture summary.

Universal settings should not be repeatedly requested by every network site unless a user
chooses to override them:
- interface language;
- fallback language;
- time zone;
- regional date, time, and number formatting;
- universal avatar;
- verified contacts;
- core security settings.

Massage Nexus-specific settings remain separate:
- massage and wellness interests;
- preferred service types;
- booking preferences;
- review and community preferences;
- NSFW or mature-content preference;
- Massage Nexus notifications;
- Massage Nexus public profile;
- Massage Nexus roles, restrictions, and membership status.

1.4 Public Access Without Registration

Registration is not required to:
- read ordinary public articles;
- browse the public directory;
- view ordinary public profiles;
- use public search and discovery features;
- read other content explicitly classified as public.

An active account is required for personal or participatory features such as:
- saving and following;
- personalized recommendations;
- gamification and rewards;
- contributions and corrections;
- discussions and comments;
- booking;
- ratings and reviews;
- uploads;
- messaging;
- profile claims;
- provider operations;
- mature or NSFW access where policy permits it.

================================================================================
2. MASSAGE NEXUS ELIGIBILITY
================================================================================

2.1 Minimum Age

Massage Nexus membership is restricted to persons who are at least 18 years old.

A person under 18:
- may view ordinary public Massage Nexus content;
- may not create or activate a Massage Nexus membership;
- may not enter the User Workspace;
- may not book, review, rate, upload, contribute, message, or access member-only content.

This is a Massage Nexus rule. It is not automatically the age rule for every future network
site. A future shared network account system may allow a minor to use an eligible site such
as Place Trace or Telebisyon.net while remaining ineligible for Massage Nexus.

2.2 Birth Date

Date of birth is required for Massage Nexus registration.

Rules:
- store the date of birth, not a permanently stored numeric age;
- calculate current age whenever eligibility must be checked;
- keep the date of birth private by default;
- do not show it on the public profile unless a future explicit policy permits a different
  birthday-only presentation;
- do not permit ordinary self-service editing after registration;
- allow correction only through an authenticated, support-mediated process;
- require evidence or additional verification when appropriate;
- record the correction and previous value in a protected audit history;
- re-evaluate eligibility after a correction.

A self-declared date of birth supports ordinary age eligibility. It is not equivalent to
government-identity age verification or stronger age assurance.

Not required during initial registration:

4. USERNAME POLICY
================================================================================

4.1 Format

A username must:
- contain only lowercase English letters a-z and digits 0-9;
- begin with a letter;
- contain no spaces;
- contain no dash;
- contain no underscore;
- contain no period or other symbol;
- be at least 4 characters;
- be no more than 30 characters;
- be unique after normalization.

Validation pattern:

^[a-z][a-z0-9]{3,29}$

The server is authoritative. The browser may convert input to lowercase for convenience,
but server validation must repeat normalization and uniqueness checks.

4.2 Accepted Examples

xylon
xylon2026
massagefan7

4.3 Rejected Format Examples

Xylon
7xylon
xylon_reyes
xylon-reyes
xylon.reyes
xylon reyes

4.4 Protected and Reserved Username Families

Users may not register names that imply platform authority, ownership, administration,
moderation, security, billing, legal authority, official brand status, or system operation.

Protected families include, but are not limited to:
- admin, admins, administrator, administrators, administration;
- mod, mods, moderator, moderators, moderation;
- support, supports, supporter when likely to imply official support, helpdesk, help;
- staff, staffs, team, official, officials;
- system, systems, root, superuser;
- webmaster, webmasters, postmaster, postmasters;
- security, abuse, privacy, legal, billing, payment;
- account, accounts, login, signin, signup, register;
- api, mail, email, www;
- massagenexus, nexus, and approved brand variations;
- official department names;
- founder, owner, operator, and similar authority terms when misleading;
- names reserved for official Neural Agents, mascots, platform characters, or service
  accounts;
- names of future network sites and their official departments;
- current and reserved route keywords.

4.5 Protected-Family Matching

The protection check must not be limited to exact strings.

The system should use a maintained protected-name registry containing:
- canonical singular root;
- explicit plural forms;
- common abbreviations;
- common misspellings;
- common leetspeak substitutions;
- authority-related prefixes and suffixes;
- numeric suffix patterns;
- brand combinations.

Examples that must be rejected:
- admin;
- admins;
- admin1;
- admin123;
- adm1n;
- administrator;
- administrators;
- moderator;
- moderators;
- mod123;
- support;
- supports;
- support123;
- officialsupport;
- supportmassagenexus;
- massagenexussupport;
- massagenexusofficial;
- supp0rt.

For high-risk authority and brand roots, a name may be rejected when it begins with, ends
with, contains, or is a clear derivation of the protected family. A false positive may be
resolved through a controlled support review, but ordinary registration must fail safely.

4.6 Reservation and Release

A reserved username may be:
- permanently reserved;
- reserved for a platform-owned account;
- temporarily held during registration;
- released after an abandoned registration period;
- blocked after abuse, impersonation, or deletion;
- retained after account deletion to prevent impersonation.

Username-change rules, history, redirects, and cooldown periods remain to be specified
before self-service username changes are enabled.

================================================================================
5. PASSWORD POLICY
================================================================================

5.1 Accepted Password Requirements

Password minimum length:
15 characters

Password maximum length:
128 characters

Allowed:
- lowercase letters;
- uppercase letters;
- numbers;
- symbols;
- spaces;
- password-manager-generated values;
- long passphrases.

Massage Nexus will not require every password to contain one lowercase letter, one
uppercase letter, one number, and one symbol. Length, compromised-password rejection,
rate limiting, secure hashing, multifactor authentication, and passkeys provide a stronger
and more usable baseline than predictable composition rules.

5.2 Rejected Passwords

Reject a password when it:
- appears in an approved compromised-password source;
- is extremely common;
- equals or substantially reproduces the username;
- equals or substantially reproduces the email address;
- contains only simple repetition or a trivial sequence;
- uses an obvious Massage Nexus password pattern;
- uses a protected administrative term as the entire or obvious password;
- fails the minimum length;
- exceeds the supported maximum;
- cannot be safely normalized and processed.

Examples of obvious rejected patterns:
- massagenexus;
- massagenexus123;
- MassageNexus2026!;
- passwordpassword;
- adminpassword;
- aaaaaaaaaaaaaaa;
- 123456789012345.

Do not reject a strong passphrase merely because it contains an ordinary dictionary word
such as massage, leaf, spa, or wellness.

5.3 Strength Indicator

Registration and password-change screens should display a JavaScript strength indicator.

Rules:
- it is advisory and immediate;
- it must not be the authoritative validator;
- the server repeats all validation;
- it should explain weaknesses without revealing protected blocklist details;
- it should not encourage predictable substitutions merely to change a color;
- it should work with pasted password-manager values;
- it should not prevent password managers or browser autofill.

5.4 Password Storage

Passwords must never be stored in plaintext and must never be reversibly encrypted.

Use Laravel's password-hashing facilities with Argon2id as the accepted direction.

Conceptual environment setting:

HASH_DRIVER=argon2id

The stored value is a one-way Argon2id hash. The hash may be moved from local MongoDB to
Azure DocumentDB without recovering the original password.

The application must use framework hashing and verification functions. It must not create
a custom password-hashing or encryption algorithm.

5.5 Rehashing

After a successful login, the application may rehash the password when the approved
Argon2id settings have changed. Rehashing must use the submitted password only during
that authenticated login and must never require decrypting a stored value.

6. REGISTRATION AND EMAIL VERIFICATION FLOW
================================================================================

Accepted flow:

1. User opens the full registration page.
2. User enters required registration fields.
3. Server validates age, username, protected-name rules, email, password, and current
   required policy versions.
4. Account is created with pending_email_verification status.
5. Verification email is sent.
6. User is not granted normal login or User Workspace access.
7. User opens the signed, expiring verification link.
8. Email is marked verified.
9. Massage Nexus membership becomes active when all eligibility requirements are met.
10. User may then log in normally.
11. Optional onboarding follows after first successful login.

Unverified users may only:
- view public content;
- see a verification-required message;
- resend a verification message subject to rate limits;
- change an unverified email through a controlled flow;
- abandon registration;
- contact support.

Unverified users may not:
- enter the User Workspace;
- book;
- save or follow;
- review or rate;
- contribute;
- upload;
- message;
- earn rewards;
- access mature or NSFW content;
- claim or manage a profile;
- use any other authenticated-member function.

================================================================================
7. LOGIN
================================================================================

Initial login identifier:
Username or email address

Initial authentication:
Password

The login interface must use standard HTML username and password fields so browsers and
password managers can offer credential storage and autofill.

The application must not create its own plaintext "save password" feature.

Login options:
- Remember me;
- Forgot password;
- Resend verification email when the account remains pending.

The login process must:
- normalize the username;
- avoid revealing whether a particular username or email exists unnecessarily;
- rate-limit attempts;
- protect against credential stuffing;
- reject inactive, locked, suspended, deleted, and unverified accounts according to status;
- record meaningful security events;
- trigger reauthentication for high-risk actions.

================================================================================
9. PASSWORD RECOVERY AND CONTACT CHANGES
================================================================================

9.1 Password Recovery

Initial recovery method:
Verified email

Recovery links must:
- be signed or securely tokenized;
- expire;
- be single-use;
- be rate-limited;
- invalidate or supersede earlier tokens when appropriate;
- avoid exposing whether an email exists;
- trigger a security notification after a successful reset;
- allow revocation of other active sessions.

SMS must not be the sole recovery method.

9.2 Email Change

Changing a verified email should require:
- recent password, passkey, or approved reauthentication;
- verification of the new email;
- notification to the old verified email;
- a security hold or additional review for suspicious changes;
- preservation of protected audit history;
- careful handling when the old address is unavailable.

9.3 Mobile Number

A mobile number is optional during the initial implementation.

Before a number can be used:
- normalize it to international E.164 form;
- send a one-time verification code;
- store only an appropriately protected verifier or hash for the code;
- expire the code;
- limit attempts and resend frequency;
- mark the number verified only after successful confirmation.

Mobile number risks include:
- SIM swap;
- recycled numbers;
- loss of the device or SIM;
- carrier delay;
- international delivery limitations;
- cost and sender-registration requirements.

Possession of a recycled or newly acquired number must never automatically grant access to
an existing account.

Changing a verified number should require:
- recent password or passkey confirmation;
- confirmation through a verified email or another strong authenticator;
- verification of the new number;
- notification to the old number and verified email where possible;
- revocation of the old number;
- temporary limits on high-risk actions;
- a security-history record.

Preferred strong recovery and second-factor methods:
1. passkey;
2. TOTP authenticator;
3. recovery codes;
4. verified email;
5. controlled support recovery;
6. SMS only as a weaker fallback when approved.

10. SESSION AND DEVICE MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

The User Workspace should provide:
- current session;
- active session list;
- approximate device and browser information;
- approximate login location where appropriate;
- session creation time;
- last activity;
- log out one session;
- log out all other sessions;
- mark a device unrecognized;
- security alert history.

Session identifiers must be random and meaningless.

Production cookies should be:
- Secure;
- HttpOnly;
- SameSite according to the accepted flow;
- scoped narrowly;
- regenerated after authentication and privilege changes.

Remember-me behavior must:
- use a revocable token;
- not store the password;
- have a defined lifetime;
- be revocable from session management;
- be invalidated after sensitive security changes where appropriate.

================================================================================
11. MULTIFACTOR AUTHENTICATION AND PASSKEYS
================================================================================

11.1 TOTP

Planned stronger authentication:
- time-based one-time password through an authenticator application;
- recovery codes;
- confirmation before activation;
- recent authentication before changing or disabling it;
- security notification when enabled, reset, or disabled.

11.2 Passkeys

Passkeys are the accepted direction for facial-recognition, fingerprint, Windows Hello,
Face ID, Touch ID, device PIN, and hardware-key login.

Massage Nexus must not build or store its own facial-recognition templates or fingerprint
database.

With WebAuthn passkeys:
- biometric verification remains on the user's device or authenticator;
- Massage Nexus stores public-key credential information, not the user's biometric;
- each credential is scoped to the approved relying-party domain;
- users may name, view, and revoke passkeys;
- recovery must not rely on one device alone.

11.3 High-Risk Actions

Recent authentication may be required for:
- changing password;
- changing primary email or mobile number;
- changing date of birth through support;
- disabling MFA;
- deleting the account;
- viewing recovery codes;
- changing payout or financial details;
- accessing especially sensitive records;
- assigning privileged roles.

12. EXTERNAL IDENTITY PROVIDERS
================================================================================

Future priority:
1. Google;
2. Apple;
3. Microsoft;
4. Facebook;
5. passkey-first and passwordless options;
6. email magic link where approved;
7. mobile-number OTP where approved;
8. X only if access, cost, policy, and user demand justify it;
9. regional providers such as LINE, Kakao, or WeChat only when target-market need justifies
   them.

A social identity provider does not replace Massage Nexus requirements.

After first-time external sign-in, the user may still need to:
- choose an available compliant username;
- enter date of birth;
- satisfy Massage Nexus age eligibility;
- accept the current Terms of Use;
- acknowledge the current Privacy Notice;
- confirm missing contact information;
- configure site membership.

External identities must use the provider's stable subject identifier. Do not rely only on
email text.

Accounts must not be merged automatically merely because two providers return the same
email address. Linking requires authenticated proof of control over both identities or a
controlled recovery process.

Microsoft Entra External ID is a future customer-identity option to evaluate before the
second network site or production federation is finalized. Initial local implementation may
use Laravel authentication while preserving the architecture needed for later federation.

External login identities are stored in user_external_identity. A provider-confirmed public
Facebook page, Instagram profile, YouTube channel, X account, or other creator profile is stored
separately in user_social_connection because login authority and public social presentation are
different responsibilities. Public social connections require provider-supported connection or
verification rather than an unrestricted user-entered URL.

After verification and first login, optional onboarding may ask for:
- display name;
- general location;
- interface and content languages;
- notification preferences;
- accessibility preferences;
- Massage Nexus interests;
- mature-content preference, subject to the NSFW policy.

Skipping optional onboarding must not block ordinary account use.

15. DEFAULT AVATAR AND USER MEDIA
================================================================================

The complete user-profile and media-reference boundary is defined in
docs/07-accounts/user-profile-and-preference-system.txt. This section preserves the account and
authentication requirements that affect onboarding and safe identity presentation.

15.1 Default Avatar

Do not use a generic gray human silhouette.

Create a set of distinctive Massage Nexus default avatars based on approved brand elements,
such as:
- Resting Leaf abstractions;
- leaf-and-ember combinations;
- massage-stone forms;
- Ink and Ember geometric wellness marks;
- other non-gendered approved visual identities.

Assign the default deterministically from an account identifier so that the same account
continues to receive the same default unless changed.

Store a default-avatar key rather than duplicating a file for each user.

Proposed source location:
apps/web/resources/images/avatar/default/

15.2 Uploaded Avatar

During development, uploaded media may use Laravel-controlled local storage.

In production, user media is planned for Azure Blob Storage or another approved object
storage provider.

The main account or profile should reference a media record by identifier rather than store
a permanent raw storage URL.

The media record should own:
- storage provider;
- container or logical area;
- object key;
- media type;
- dimensions;
- duration;
- file size;
- crop information;
- poster image;
- processing state;
- moderation state;
- NSFW classification;
- accessibility text;
- ownership and consent records where needed.

15.3 Animated Avatar

A short moving profile image is a planned differentiating feature, not an initial
registration requirement.

Future baseline:
- short duration;
- looping;
- no audible audio;
- poster image;
- reduced-motion fallback;
- strict size and format limits;
- server-side transcoding;
- malware and content checks;
- moderation;
- public avatars remain subject to the NSFW policy.

NSFW avatars and cover images are not permitted because they appear in broad, incidental,
or public contexts. Restricted user media, when allowed, belongs in an explicitly restricted
media area rather than the avatar surface.

17. LARAVEL IMPLEMENTATION DIRECTION
================================================================================

Current environment:
- Laravel Framework 13.20.0;
- PHP 8.5.8;
- MongoDB PHP extension 2.3.3;
- MongoDB application package: mongodb/laravel-mongodb 5.8.2, connected to the massage_main
  database (see Technology Stack section 3 for the full integration record).

Implementation decision: ordinary authentication (registration, email verification, login,
logout, remember me) was built directly on Laravel's native Auth guard, RateLimiter, and
signed-URL facilities, using a MongoDB-backed App\Models\User (via MongoDB\Laravel\Auth\User)
as the Authenticatable model. Laravel Fortify was not installed for this scope: Fortify's main
contribution is ready-made controllers and routes, and Massage Nexus already required fully
custom branded controllers and views, so adding Fortify would mean immediately overriding its
actions rather than reusing them. Fortify remains the candidate direction for TOTP and passkey
support later in the implementation order (section 20, items 18-19), where its action classes
have more to offer than a hand-rolled equivalent.

The four conditions below gated the start of this work. All four are now satisfied for the
ordinary-authentication scope implemented so far:
- this specification is accepted (the user directed implementation to begin);
- MongoDB integration is selected and tested (see Technology Stack v1.90);
- account and membership record boundaries are accepted for the initial embedded user_main
  scope described in section 18 below;
- Terms and Privacy requirements are ready for versioned acceptance (draft placeholder pages
  at /legal/terms and /legal/privacy with a tracked version string in config/legal.php, pending
  real legal text).

Password recovery is implemented through Laravel's password broker with MongoDB-backed,
hashed, expiring, single-use tokens. Delivery is limited to verified email addresses, public
request responses remain generic, request and reset endpoints are rate-limited, the complete
project password policy is reapplied, remember tokens rotate, and a security notification is
sent after a successful reset.

Session review, remote revocation, password-reset revocation, and coarse user-recognized device
summaries are implemented. Device records are private presentation/grouping data, do not use an
invasive fingerprint, and never grant authentication. TOTP enrollment and challenge enforcement,
recovery-code issuance, passkeys, recent-authentication gates, security-event history, and external
identity providers remain unimplemented, matching their later position in the section 20 order.

Registration submission (POST /register) is rate-limited, matching the password-recovery
pattern above. A separate advisory endpoint (GET /register/check-username) lets the register
form show real-time username-availability feedback before submission; it runs the same format,
protected-username, and uniqueness rules as registration itself, is independently rate-limited,
and never replaces the authoritative server-side uniqueness check performed in
RegisterController::store(). Page layout and copy for Login and Register are documented
separately in Auth UI (docs/06-user-interface/auth-ui.txt); this section remains authoritative
for the underlying behavior.

18. COLLECTION AND RECORD RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================

The accepted initial project-local account collection boundaries are:

user_main:
- account identity, membership state, public profile, and bounded current preferences normally
  loaded and updated with the user;
- examples include current interface language, time zone, regional formatting, theme,
  accessibility, notification, community, and content-visibility preferences where applicable;
- a current convenience flag may summarize whether a required policy step is outstanding, but it
  is not the authoritative evidence that the user accepted or acknowledged a particular version.
- the accepted profile fields, embedded account_preference shape, media references, and Settings
  distinction are defined in docs/07-accounts/user-profile-and-preference-system.txt.

user_policy:
- history-preserving records of the user's required policy acceptance, privacy or policy
  acknowledgment, optional consent grant, and consent withdrawal;
- identifies the applicable policy or public legal document, exact version, site, locale, action,
  date and time, and approved evidence metadata;
- preserves separate user decisions even though they share one collection;
- does not store the controlling public legal text and does not absorb ordinary preferences;
- is not replaced by a single mutable consent flag in user_main.

Do not create a separate user_consent collection under the current direction. Additional account
collections are created only when an independently growing, secured, searched, retained, or
operated responsibility requires one.

The complete accepted user collection family is defined in
docs/07-accounts/user-profile-and-preference-system.txt. Authentication-specific responsibilities
use these final collection names:

Network account:
- global identity and lifecycle.

Credential:
- password hash and credential state.

user_external_identity:
- provider, stable provider subject, linking state.

user_contact:
- personal email, mobile number, approved messaging channel, verification, replacement, and
  lifecycle history;
- user_main may retain a synchronized primary login-email cache for authentication.

user_access:
- global or scoped roles and direct permissions, separate from membership and profile identity.

Profile:
- public Massage Nexus identity and visibility-controlled information.

Preferences:
- universal and site-specific current settings, initially bounded within user_main as the embedded
  account, appearance, accessibility, notification, privacy, community, and booking-preference
  objects;
- time-sensitive intake and signed booking snapshots remain in their booking-domain records.

user_session and user_device:
- active authentication sessions and recognized devices.

user_mfa_method, user_recovery_code, and user_passkey:
- configured multifactor methods and individually hashed recovery codes;
- WebAuthn public credential information.

user_security_event:
- login, reset, contact change, MFA, passkey, recovery, and administrative action history.

Media asset:
- avatar, cover, image, video, processing, storage, and moderation metadata.

Policy history:
- accepted user_policy responsibility described above.

================================================================================
21. ACCEPTED DECISIONS SUMMARY
================================================================================

- One shared network account may support several sites.
- Registering through Massage Nexus creates a Massage Nexus membership, not automatic public
  memberships on every future site.
- Universal language, time-zone, avatar, contact, and security settings can be reused.
- Massage Nexus membership is 18+.
- Public browsing does not require registration.
- Username uses lowercase letters and numbers only, begins with a letter, and has no symbols,
  dash, or underscore.
- Protected authority and brand families include plural, numeric, abbreviated, misspelled,
  and deceptive variants.
- support123 and similar authority-appearing names are prohibited.
- Passwords use length and blocklist controls rather than mandatory character-category rules.
- Passwords are hashed with Argon2id and are never stored plaintext or reversibly encrypted.
- Email is required initially.
- An unverified user cannot log in normally or use any member function.
- Mobile number is optional and is not the sole recovery method.
- Remember me and revocable session management are required.
- Facial recognition and fingerprints should be used through passkeys, not a Massage Nexus
  biometric database.
- Gender is optional and does not choose the avatar.
- Default avatars are branded, non-gendered Massage Nexus assets.
- User media references a media record rather than a permanent raw storage URL.
- Animated avatars are planned after secure media processing exists.
- NSFW avatars are prohibited; restricted media belongs in controlled content areas.

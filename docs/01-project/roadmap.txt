Title: Massage Nexus Roadmap
Document Version: 1.74
Date Created: 2026-07-16
Revision Date: 2026-07-20
Document Description: This Roadmap organizes Massage Nexus into dependency phases, milestones, release gates, and continuing operations. It maps every major system—including directory profiles, research, editorial content, News, Massage Campus, quizzes, surveys, community, Support, role-specific workspaces, booking, monetization, applications, and global expansion—without duplicating the thousands of detailed feature items kept in the Features document. Completion status belongs in the Simple Checklist, and detailed behavior belongs in each system specification.

================================================================================
1. ROADMAP RULES
================================================================================

- The Roadmap answers what must be built before what, what each major phase produces, and what
  conditions permit a release.
- The Features document preserves the complete feature and subfeature inventory.
- The Simple Checklist preserves completion status for planning, setup, features, releases,
  and operations.
- A phase may contain work for several later releases when the work creates a shared foundation.
- A release may omit nonessential features without deleting them from the planned platform.
- No public release bypasses security, privacy, data integrity, policy, backup, recovery,
  accessibility, moderation, and Support gates.
- Campus, surveys, quizzes, workspaces, comics, News, and other large systems may begin with a
  useful foundation and expand in later releases; they must not disappear into one vague future
  line.
- Each international market receives its own data, language, policy, moderation, Support, and
  operational readiness gate.

Current position:
Massage Nexus has a recoverable Git baseline synchronized with the private remote repository, a
working Laravel and MongoDB foundation, organized repository documentation, root contributor
instructions, public page foundations, and implemented registration, email verification, login,
logout, and password recovery. Pre-coding planning is sufficiently complete for focused feature
development. Documentation review and technical hardening continue alongside implementation
rather than blocking all coding. The immediate product target is Massage Nexus Version 1.00,
the first launch, with persistent, permission-aware spa submission and lifecycle management.

FIRST LAUNCH — MASSAGE NEXUS VERSION 1.00

Audience:
Initial Massage Nexus users, approved contributors, and authorized internal reviewers.

Required account capabilities:
- browse permitted public pages without registration;
- register, accept the required Terms and Privacy information, and verify email;
- log in by username or email, remember the session when requested, log out, and recover a
  forgotten password.

Required spa capabilities:
- list and view persisted spa profiles;
- submit a new spa after authentication and email verification;
- view, edit, and delete an unused private draft owned by the current user;
- view and edit a returned or pending submission according to its moderation state;
- withdraw a pending submission;
- publish only after authorized approval;
- propose revisions to a published spa without erasing accepted history;
- close, archive, merge, or soft-delete published records through authorized lifecycle actions;
- prevent an ordinary submitter or claimed operator from permanently erasing a real public record,
  independent contributions, audit history, or legal and safety evidence.

Release gate:
- registration, verification, login, logout, and recovery tests pass;
- spa create, read, update, draft deletion, withdrawal, submission, approval, rejection,
  publication, closure, archive, restoration, duplicate, and authorization tests pass;
- one user cannot edit or delete another user's draft or submission;
- random identifier and slug uniqueness are enforced and collision handling is tested;
- accepted spa facts retain source, revision, and actor history;
- public pages do not expose private evidence, draft data, stack traces, or secrets;
- forms are keyboard accessible, errors are understandable, and supported layouts are usable;
- registration, authentication, and spa-submission rate limits and abuse protections are active;
- a clean restore, backup, rollback, and production-safe error check succeeds.

Not required for Version 1.00:
- maps, advanced search, comparisons, reviews, ratings, comments, provider claiming, booking,
  payments, advertising, mobile, desktop, or broad international expansion.

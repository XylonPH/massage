Title: Massage Nexus Features
Document Version: 2.61
Date Created: 2026-06-30
Revision Date: 2026-07-20
Document Description: This is the master working inventory of website features and feature details planned for Massage Nexus. It serves as the owner's memory and planning checklist before and during implementation: the grouped feature summaries explain the major systems, while the Detailed Working Feature Inventory preserves the smaller pages, fields, actions, workflows, tools, controls, and possible additions that must not disappear merely because a separate specification exists. This file is not a Roadmap, implementation strategy, changelog, release log, database schema, or list of document revisions.

Platform Scope: This file covers the public Massage Nexus website and its browser-based user, provider, seller, advertiser, contributor, research, translation, editorial, Support, moderation, and administration workspaces. It may mention mobile, desktop, APIs, and integrations where the website must support or connect to them, while platform-specific behavior remains in the separate application documents.

Platform Boundary: Massage Nexus documents massage, spa, wellness, bodywork, beauty, and closely adjacent services, providers, people, products, content, and communities. It may retain ordinary, premium, budget, closed, historical, disputed, sensitive, clinical, gray-area, or adult-adjacent information when necessary for accurate classification, research, safety, moderation, professional-boundary education, and lawful public understanding. Massage Nexus does not promote exploitation, harassment, illegal activity, unsafe solicitation, or deceptive commercial practices.

File Maintenance:
- Keep accepted feature summaries concise but complete.
- Preserve detailed inventory items unless the user rejects them, merges them, or confirms that another item fully replaces them.
- Do not delete detailed items merely because a dedicated specification exists.
- Add new ideas to the most logical inventory group.
- Remove duplication only after confirming that no unique requirement is lost.
- Implementation status belongs in the Simple Checklist.
- Dependency and release order belong in the Roadmap.

GROUPED FEATURE SUMMARIES
================================================================================

1. PUBLIC WEBSITE, DISCOVERY, AND ACCESS
----------------------------------------

1. Homepage and Public Website
The public website is the canonical Massage Nexus destination and provides the homepage, public navigation, discovery, current content, featured records, campaigns, Help, policies, and account entry. It remains useful without registration and adapts selected areas for signed-in users and approved professional roles. Responsive behavior does not remove the fuller browser-based tools needed for research, content, provider management, translation, moderation, Support, and administration.

2. Guided Browsing
Guided Browsing lets users explore by location, Service Family (from Service Classification), normalized service, Spa Type, therapist practice context, price range, facilities, availability, ratings, promotions, products, articles, or popularity.

3. Smart Search and Filters
Smart Search and Filters help users find services, spas, therapists, products, sellers, articles, reviews, and discussions through keywords and structured filters that leverage the classification taxonomies.

4. Contact and Inquiry
Contact and Inquiry gives users approved ways to reach a spa, therapist, seller, or platform representative. Contact information can include visibility, verification, source, and correction controls.

5. Maps and Directions
Maps and Directions help users find an establishment or understand a provider's service area while protecting private residential locations.

6. Comparison
Comparison lets users compare services, spas, therapists, products, sellers, prices, facilities, ratings, availability, and booking options side by side. Where applicable, comparisons should respect Market Class / Grade context for fair evaluation.

7. Recommendations
Recommendations help users discover relevant services, spas, therapists, articles, products, promotions, and discussions with transparent sponsored and organic distinctions.

8. Theme Customization
Users may choose supported appearance themes while preserving accessibility and consistent interface behavior.

9. Accessibility
Accessibility applies across public pages, account flows, profiles, search, maps, media, booking, Support, workspaces, translations, Comments, Discussions, and administrative tools. The platform should support screen readers, keyboard navigation, scalable text, captions, contrast, reduced motion, understandable labels and errors, accessible forms, and non-visual alternatives. Accessibility is a system-wide requirement rather than a one-sentence interface preference.

2. DIRECTORY PROFILES, MENUS, PRODUCTS, AND MEDIA
-------------------------------------------------

10. Service Profile
Service Profiles give each normalized service concept its own structured reference page. A page may explain the Service Sector, Service Domain, Service Family, preferred and alternate names, history and cultural context, typical methods, named techniques, practitioner contact body parts, client positions, treatment supports, tools, products, preparation, safety, contraindications, aftercare, professional scope, related qualifications, establishments offering it, therapists capable of performing it, provider menu items and prices, related products, media, articles, reviews, ratings, relationships, and sources. A Service Profile remains separate from a spa's establishment_service or an independent therapist's practitioner_service.

11. Spa Profile
Spa and Establishment Profiles give each spa, massage establishment, wellness center, service location, or supported adjacent provider a structured public page. Each profile may include identity, description, location, contact, hours, amenities, treatment areas, facilities, services, prices, therapists, media, reviews, ratings, discussions, booking-enablement status, policies, promos, and verification labels. Profiles can show claimed, verified, unclaimed, closed, moved, renamed, disputed, or historical status. An unclaimed profile may support private planning, private history, an invitation to claim, and a clearly unconfirmed inquiry, but it must not imply live availability, provider acceptance, payment, or a confirmed reservation. Spa Market Class provides review context only and must never automatically raise or lower ratings.

12. Therapist Profile
Therapist and Practitioner Profiles give massage therapists, freelance therapists, home-service therapists, spa-based therapists, trainees, and other supported practitioners their own structured presence. A profile may include stable professional identity, approved public name or username, bio, practitioner_capability records, direct practitioner_service menu items, practice settings, service areas, schedules, availability, approved contacts, credentials, affiliations, establishment-specific rank, context-specific Therapist Service Market Class, media, reviews, ratings, discussions, and booking enablement. A therapist profile remains independent of one employer and may exist before it is claimed. Stable profile identity rather than username prevents duplicate therapist records when employment changes. Therapist Service Market Class provides review context only and must never automatically raise or lower ratings.

13. Product Profile and Discovery
Product Profiles explain massage, spa, beauty, wellness, equipment, supply, aftercare, and related products independently of one seller. They may include brand, category (from Product Classification), grade, versions, variants, ingredients, materials, claims, allergens, warnings, specifications, lifecycle, authenticity, images, documents, sellers, prices, reviews, ratings, related services, and related providers. Product Grade provides review context only and must never automatically raise or lower ratings. Product offers and prices remain seller-specific.

14. Seller Profile
Seller Profiles give each seller, distributor, or product-owner account its own structured public page, separate from the products it lists. A profile may include seller type, business information, product list and featured products, contact information, social links, shipping/delivery or pickup options, media gallery, review and rating summary, discussions, verification and claim labels, and report/save/follow/share actions. Seller Profiles remain separate from individual Product Profiles, which may be sold by more than one seller.

15. Provider Services, Menus, and Pricing
Provider Services, Menus, and Pricing preserve the exact commercial menu items sold by establishments and independent therapists. establishment_service stores an establishment's commercial offering. practitioner_service stores an independent therapist's commercial offering. practitioner_capability separately records that a therapist can competently perform a normalized service even when that therapist does not independently sell it. Menu items may contain branded names, mapped normalized services, duration, price, variants, combinations, packages, series, courses, add-ons, facility access, included and selectable products, paid upgrades, eligible therapists, delivery context, intake requirements, booking rules, and availability. Provider menu items remain separate from the universal Service Profile.

16. Secure Media Gallery
Media Gallery displays approved public, member-only, selected-user, owner-only, moderator-only, or otherwise restricted photos, videos, documents, menus, service media, and product media. Restricted files are protected by server authorization and are not merely hidden through JavaScript or CSS. Media may include source, permission, visibility, approval, copyright, and AI-use labels.

17. Promos, Vouchers, and Gift Cards
Promos, Vouchers, and Gift Cards allow approved providers and sellers to publish limited-time commercial offers connected to services, products, locations, campaigns, and conditions.

3. EDITORIAL, NEWS, LEARNING, RESEARCH, AND BRAND EXPERIENCES
-------------------------------------------------------------

18. Articles and Resources
Articles and Resources help readers understand massage, services, spa etiquette, therapist credentials, safety, pricing, facilities, aftercare, wellness products, business operation, and practical decisions. Articles can connect to related services, spas, therapists, products, reviews, discussions, and learning materials. Where relevant, articles should reference the classification taxonomies for consistency.

19. News
News reports timely and attributable developments involving Massage Nexus, spas, therapists, services, wellness businesses, regulation, research, safety, events, products, technology, and related communities. News preserves sources, publication and update time, location, corrections, responses, and distinction between reporting, analysis, opinion, platform announcements, press releases, and sponsored material.

20. Massage Campus
Massage Campus is the structured learning area of Massage Nexus for clients, therapists, providers, contributors, correspondents, and staff. It may contain course catalogs, modules, lessons, guides, glossaries, demonstrations, quizzes, knowledge checks, assessments, learning paths, instructor or author information, progress records, completion signals, and discussion connections. Campus results may support badges, contributor development, or internal readiness, but they must remain separate from government credentials, professional licenses, school-issued qualifications, and provider verification.

21. Quizzes, Assessments, and Learning Progress
Quizzes and knowledge checks support Massage Campus, onboarding, contributor development, campaigns, and light community engagement. Assessments may use questions, scenarios, practical checks, attempts, feedback, passing rules, progress, and completion records. They do not create a professional license or formal qualification unless an authorized external institution separately provides one.

22. Surveys and Polls
Surveys and Polls gather structured opinions, needs, satisfaction, market observations, and community feedback from users, providers, therapists, contributors, advertisers, and other approved groups. Participation, anonymity, sampling, consent, incentives, publication, privacy thresholds, and repeat measurement must be defined. Results may inform planning and public Statistics but must not be presented as representative of an entire market without an appropriate methodology.

23. The Resting Leaf
The Resting Leaf is the original comic and character world of Massage Nexus, using short visual stories to show spa situations, client habits, wellness humor, and everyday service moments. It is a supporting brand and content extension, not a core platform directory or transactional feature.

24. Mascots and Platform Characters
Mascots and Platform Characters give Massage Nexus a friendly visual identity through recurring approved characters. They can appear in comics, campaigns, onboarding, badges, seasonal events, help moments, gamification, and selected interface areas. They are supporting brand elements, not core platform features.

25. Relaxation Room
Relaxation Rooms are themed live or scheduled spaces where users can listen, read, chat, relax, or participate in light wellness activities. A room may include looping instrumental music, calming visuals, sponsored wellness moments, guided prompts, live chat, campaign events, or seasonal themes. These are supporting brand experiences rather than core directory features.

26. Data Research, Provenance, and Field Verification
Data Research supports direct visits, interviews, official sources, provider confirmation, map leads, social accounts, menus, prices, historical evidence, correspondent assignments, and community research. Each important fact may preserve source, observation date, verification date, confidence, status, contradiction, and revision. Google Maps and similar services are treated as leads rather than complete or current authorities. Absence from a map does not prove nonexistence. Global expansion is organized by priority city or tourism region rather than shallow country-wide ingestion, beginning with the Philippines and later pilots in Thailand, Indonesia, the United States, the United Arab Emirates, Vietnam, Japan, and other approved markets. Research AI may organize and compare evidence but cannot invent or automatically verify facts.

4. ACCOUNTS, PERSONALIZATION, COMMUNITY, AND TRUST
--------------------------------------------------

27. Account, Membership, Registration, and Authentication
Massage Nexus uses a shared network identity together with a separate Massage Nexus membership, eligibility, and policy-acceptance record. Registration, login, email verification, recovery, protected usernames, secure password hashing, sessions, devices, TOTP, passkeys, and later approved external identities belong to this feature. Public browsing remains available without registration, while normal member actions require the appropriate verification, standing, age, and permission.

28. User Profiles
User Profiles give registered users a personal identity with approved visibility controls for activity, reviews, contributions, saves, follows, bookings, points, badges, and other supported information. Every registered user is considered a client by default, even when the same account also acts as a therapist, operator, seller, contributor, advertiser, editor, moderator, or administrator. The private User Workspace may preserve cross-provider bookings, manually recorded past services, imported history, reusable preferences, budgeting, analytics, loyalty progress, and data export without exposing those records on the public User Profile.

29. User Workspace
The private User Workspace organizes bookings, manually recorded service history, saved preferences, receipts, budgets, personal analytics, loyalty, exports, account controls, private notes, and other cross-provider records. It remains separate from the public User Profile. Every account retains its client context even when the same person also acts as a therapist, operator, seller, contributor, advertiser, editor, moderator, Support agent, or administrator.

30. Saved Items and Following
Users can save or follow services, spas, therapists, products, sellers, articles, searches, and discussions.

31. Notifications
Notifications may appear through the website, future app, email, SMS, or push channels for bookings, reviews, contributions, security, verification, payments, refunds, waitlists, followed records, and provider operations. Ordinary notifications must not expose private intake, health information, exact residential addresses, compensation details, or other sensitive content on a lock screen, generic calendar, or unauthorized staff view.

32. Messaging
Messaging supports approved inquiries and conversations with safety, reporting, moderation, permission, and spam controls.

33. Comments
Comments provide lightweight responses attached to Articles, News, Reviews, profiles, products, comics, research pages, and other approved targets. They support replies, role labels, reactions, translation, editing, reporting, moderation, and notification while remaining separate from experience-based Reviews, numeric Ratings, formal Corrections, Support tickets, and longer Discussions.

34. Community Discussions
Discussions and Community Q&A support questions, replies, reactions, helpful votes, official participation, and moderation.

35. Reviews
Reviews allow users and editors to publish structured experiences about supported targets while preserving visit, service, provider, price, disclosure, media, verification, and response context. Reviews must be understood in the context of the target's Market Class / Grade (Spa, Therapist, or Product). Market Class / Grade provides context only and must never automatically raise or lower a rating.

36. Criteria Ratings
Ratings support structured criteria for applicable profile types and simpler reactions for selected content. Ratings must always be interpreted in the context of the relevant Market Class / Grade.

37. Contributions
Contributions allow users to submit records, corrections, sources, media, translations, duplicate reports, closure reports, and other data improvements through reviewable workflows. Contributions that affect classification fields should respect the stable codes defined in the classification documents.

38. Gamification
Gamification adds Nexus Points, badges, rank paths, progress, achievements, Embers, Inklings, and other approved participation mechanics while protecting quality and trust. A completed eligible booking may emit a reward event, but Booking must not directly manipulate reward balances without shared validation and anti-abuse rules. Provider-specific loyalty programs—such as visit stamps, service counts, packages, memberships, or provider points—remain separate from Massage Nexus currencies, badges, Reputation, ratings, and reviews.

39. Points, Badges, Ranks, and Reputation
The reputation system rewards useful participation while separating self-management, conflicts of interest, moderation, and official activity from public rewards.

40. Chat and Live Conversation
Chat areas may support scheduled rooms, community events, provider inquiries, and approved AI personas with safety and moderation.

41. Verification and Trust Signals
Verification shows which profiles, contacts, documents, credentials, media, visits, and claims were checked without exposing private evidence.

42. Safety, Privacy, and Reporting
Safety, Privacy, and Reporting let users report wrong information, harassment, spam, impersonation, suspicious reviews, unsafe behavior, exploitation, solicitation, misleading media, privacy violations, and other problems. Sensitive, intimate, sensual, sexual-wellness, adult, clinical, and disputed service information may require age, jurisdiction, access, visibility, consent, payment, and moderation controls rather than silent erasure from the knowledge base. Booking intake remains separate from ordinary booking data and is protected through minimum disclosure, role authorization, retention rules, encryption or equivalent safeguards, and audit where appropriate.

5. BOOKING, SCHEDULING, AND ROLE WORKSPACES
-------------------------------------------

43. Booking, Availability, and Appointments
Booking, Availability, and Appointments use a massage-first but service-flexible appointment engine. The system may support Personal Appointment Plans, unconfirmed inquiries, provider-approved requests, reserved appointments, paid reservations, provider-created manual bookings, imported appointments, and walk-ins.

A booking may contain one booking party and several service participants, each with a separate service line, duration, therapist choice, pressure and body-area preferences, products, attire, draping, accessibility needs, intake, consent, price, and completion status. Establishments may allow a required therapist, preferred therapist, provider assignment, no preference, or selection later. Independent therapist booking already identifies the therapist but may still require a professional context, location, or delivery setting.

Claimed and configured providers may enable request approval, instant confirmation, external booking, waitlists, walk-ins, deposits, payment, and provider policies. Unclaimed providers cannot receive a normal confirmed Massage Nexus reservation or platform payment.

Booking may use privacy-preserving eligibility rules before provider disclosure, separate private intake, minimum necessary and time-limited therapist access, consent snapshots, multilingual structured options, original plus labeled free-text translation, resource scheduling, travel, queues, cancellation, refunds, tips, provider loyalty, private satisfaction feedback, rebooking, analytics, therapist compensation, and provider-finance events. Booking may support any approved Massage Nexus provider menu item, with service-specific form sections rather than one generic appointment form.

44. Calendar Sync
Users, establishments, and therapists may connect supported bookings or availability with external calendars. Synchronization may include recurring availability, exceptions, confirmed bookings, travel, leave, breaks, resource reservations, and conflict handling. Sensitive intake must never be placed in a generic calendar title or description.

45. Therapist Workspace
The Therapist Workspace manages approved profile information, independent menu items, capabilities, affiliations, availability, credentials, media, booking requests, assigned intake access, consent, schedules, travel settings, walk-ins where supported, messages, payment status, cancellations, private feedback, loyalty, compensation, tips, payout information, and analytics according to permission. The same account retains its Personal client context and private cross-provider history.

46. Operator Workspace
The Operator Workspace manages establishment information, commercial menu items, prices, therapists, facilities, bookable resources, schedules, booking enablement, intake templates, eligibility rules, staff permissions, queues, walk-ins, waitlists, assignment, payment status, cancellation, refund, provider loyalty, private feedback, therapist compensation, provider finance, media, responses, promotions, and analytics. Reception, therapist, manager, finance, and owner permissions remain distinct.

47. Seller Workspace
The Seller Workspace manages seller identity, product relationships, offers, prices, media, fulfillment, customer questions, promotions, orders where supported, reports, and analytics. Product identity and evidence remain separate from seller-specific availability, price, and commercial claims. Seller access is permission-bound and does not grant control over independent Reviews or Ratings.

48. Advertiser Workspace
The Advertiser Workspace manages approved campaigns, creative assets, budgets, invoices, placement, targeting boundaries, disclosures, performance, and Support. Advertising remains separate from organic classification, verification, Ratings, Reviews, and editorial conclusions. Campaign tools expose only the audience and performance data that the advertiser is authorized to receive.

49. Steward and Editor Tools
Trusted contributors and editors can improve incomplete, unclaimed, historical, disputed, or community-managed records through controlled permissions.

50. Contributor, Correspondent, Researcher, Translator, and Editor Workspaces
Specialized workspaces organize assignments, sources, field visits, evidence, drafts, Corrections, translation, review decisions, media, publication, and contribution history. Each role receives only the tools and records needed for the actual task. One person may hold several roles, but permissions, credit, conflict handling, and audit remain distinct.

51. Moderator, Support Agent, and Administrator Workspaces
Internal workspaces provide moderation queues, tickets, complaints, appeals, restricted evidence, account actions, data tools, configuration, analytics, audit, incident response, and platform operations. High-impact actions require the correct authority, confirmation, logging, and human review. Administrator access is not treated as a shortcut around privacy, evidence, or policy.

6. SUPPORT, BUSINESS, ANALYTICS, LANGUAGE, AI, AND INTEGRATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------

52. Support Center and Knowledge Base
The public Support Center provides searchable task-based guidance for visitors, members, providers, therapists, sellers, advertisers, contributors, and staff. Approved AI may answer routine documented questions using current knowledge and authorized context. Help articles, known issues, service status, contact routes, and feature-specific guidance remain versioned and multilingual according to readiness.

53. Ticketing, Complaints, and Technical Problem Handling
Private tickets handle unresolved questions, account issues, advertiser and provider Support, billing, complaints, accessibility, privacy, security, moderation appeals, technical problems, and bug reports. AI and staff classify, prioritize, gather evidence, respond, and escalate according to risk and authority. A confirmed software defect may become a sanitized private GitHub Issue, but the ticket and its personal or commercial evidence remain in Massage Nexus.

54. Premium Listings
Premium tools may provide richer presentation, analytics, booking, media, and branding while remaining distinct from organic trust or rating.

55. Advertising and Sponsored Placements
Paid promotion must be clearly disclosed and must not silently replace organic classification, rating, verification, or editorial judgment.

56. Analytics, Statistics, and Measurement
Analytics uses governed metric and event definitions rather than unexplained counters. Authorized users may view personal, provider, profile, search, contact, booking, schedule, resource, queue, waitlist, promotion, review, loyalty, payment, finance, compensation, research, translation, referral, social-presence, and engagement analytics according to permission. Public statistics may show aggregated directory coverage, market research progress, source freshness, translation coverage, and social observations with clear definitions, dates, minimum-group protections, and methodology. Client analytics remain private. Provider analytics remain limited to authorized provider records. Intake, private messages, precise residential locations, and cross-provider client history are excluded from ordinary analytics.

57. Multilingual Support and Translation Management
English is the source and fallback language. Filipino is the primary local language, with Taglish treated as an approved Filipino style or presentation mode. Cebuano and Hiligaynon are separate Philippine languages. International priority may include Indonesian, Spanish, Brazilian Portuguese, Japanese, Thai, Vietnamese, Korean, Chinese script variants, French, German, Arabic, and later market-specific languages. Static interface text uses semantic translation keys. Dynamic content preserves original language and source revision. Discussions and comments are translated on request, labeled, cached, and invalidated after edits rather than translated into every language automatically. Community translation is reviewable. Legal, privacy, security, payment, intake, consent, and safety translations require stronger human review. Coverage is measured by critical workflow and content area.

Static interface language, dynamic translated content, original user content, language subdomains, fallback, translation credit, and AI-assisted translation are handled separately. Taxonomy labels and descriptions should use stable translation keys. Booking can translate structured preferences and policies while preserving both the original and clearly labeled translation of free-text notes. Safety-critical translations must not silently replace the original and may require clarification.

58. AI-Assisted Platform Tools
Approved AI may assist brainstorming, writing, translation, summarization, classification suggestions, duplicate detection, moderation triage, search, support, booking-note translation, and Neural Agent experiences. AI use requires permission, validation, privacy controls, usage limits, cost disclosure where applicable, and appropriate AI labels. AI-assisted classification suggestions must reference stable codes. AI must not diagnose a client, make an unreviewed high-impact eligibility decision outside accepted rules, expose sealed intake, prove serious misconduct, or permanently remove a person without the required human process.

59. Cache and Temporary Data
Caching reduces repeated database, search, translation, AI, media, analytics, and external-service work while keeping authoritative data in its proper store. Cache keys and invalidation account for environment, target, locale, permission, revision, and visibility. Private, restricted, booking, ticket, payment, security, mobile, and desktop caches require tighter isolation, expiration, cleanup, and failure behavior.

60. Platform APIs
The platform may provide separate First-Party App, Partner, and approved Public APIs while sharing the same business rules, Models, Actions, Services, Policies, validation, eligibility, privacy, payment, and scheduling logic. Booking APIs may support plans, requests, participants, availability, assignment, waitlists, walk-ins, eligibility outcomes, consent, payments, refunds, feedback, and rebooking according to scope. Mobile and partner clients never connect directly to the production database.

61. Branded Portal
Branded Portal allows an approved spa, therapist, school, training institution, organization, or selected partner to present a customized public website through a Massage Nexus subdomain, custom domain, or approved white-label surface. It continues to use shared accounts, normalized profiles, permissions, learning, booking, content, and workspace systems. The customized portal does not replace the authoritative Massage Nexus profile or weaken platform trust, moderation, verification, and disclosure rules.

18. NEURAL AGENTS, NEXUS CAST, MASCOTS, AI PERSONAS, AND COMICS (SUPPORTING BRAND ELEMENTS)
================================================================================


18.1 Four Separate Systems
--------------------------
Neural Agents, Nexus Cast, and Mascots are architecturally and creatively separate systems and must not be conflated in data models, content, or interface copy:
- Neural Agents — editorial writing-voice personas (tone, humor, article-writing style, chat style) used for written content such as articles and review-organization assistance. They are not visual characters and do not appear on-screen.
- Nexus Cast — recurring illustrated visual characters used in comics, mockups, and staged marketing or demonstration scenes. They are visual actors, not writers, and are not Neural Agents.
- Mascots — non-human brand characters (e.g., Nexus, Embi, Leafy) used for onboarding, gamification, badges, and playful interface moments.
- AI personas / chat experiences — conversational surfaces that may draw on a Neural Agent's voice; these are a delivery channel, not a fourth character roster.

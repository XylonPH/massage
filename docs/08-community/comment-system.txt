================================================================================
1. PURPOSE
================================================================================

Comments provide lightweight responses that do not require a full Review or separate
Discussion.

Examples:
- responding to an article;
- asking a question about a review;
- noting a small factual detail about a spa;
- commenting on a therapist or product without claiming a complete service experience;
- reacting to a comic;
- replying to another comment.

Comments do not replace:
- structured Reviews;
- Ratings;
- formal data Corrections;
- Support tickets;
- private Messages;
- long-form Discussions.

================================================================================
2. ELIGIBLE TARGETS
================================================================================

Comments may attach to:
- article;
- review;
- spa or establishment;
- therapist;
- service;
- product;
- seller;
- promotion;
- comic;
- public research page;
- public statistics page;
- approved media;
- another explicitly enabled record.

Each target may:
- enable or disable comments;
- require login;
- limit who may comment;
- apply a moderation profile;
- allow or disallow replies;
- close comments after a defined condition.

================================================================================
3. COMMENT, REVIEW, AND DISCUSSION
================================================================================

Use a Review when evaluating an actual experience, service, provider, product, or transaction
through the Review System.

Use a Comment for:
- a question;
- a brief observation;
- a response to content;
- added context;
- information that does not merit a full Review.

A Comment:
- does not affect Rating;
- must not imitate Rating through unsupported numeric scoring;
- may be invited into the Review workflow when appropriate.

Use a Discussion when:
- the topic needs a title;
- several people may contribute over time;
- longer conversation is expected;
- category discovery or subscriptions are useful.

A moderator may link a growing comment conversation to a new Discussion.

================================================================================
4. COMMENT RECORD
================================================================================

A comment may contain:
- identifier;
- target type and identifier;
- author account;
- original language;
- content;
- parent comment;
- root thread;
- depth;
- status;
- visibility;
- created and edited dates;
- deletion date and reason;
- moderation state;
- NSFW level;
- spoiler state;
- pinned state;
- provider, author, staff, or moderator label;
- reaction counts;
- reply count;
- report count;
- source revision;
- translation availability;
- audit references.

Final storage belongs in database design.

================================================================================
5. REPLIES
================================================================================

Use limited nesting.

Recommended maximum visible depth:
- three levels.

Deeper replies may display as a flattened continuation while preserving parent relation.

Rules:
- the author must be able to view the parent;
- deleted-parent behavior preserves understandable context;
- blocked users follow blocking rules;
- closed comments reject new replies;
- provider and staff responses are labeled.

================================================================================
6. CONTENT AND FORMAT
================================================================================

Comments may include:
- plain text;
- approved links;
- mentions;
- limited emoji;
- short quoted excerpts;
- approved media later.

Comments must not include:
- spam;
- undisclosed advertising;
- harassment;
- doxxing;
- impersonation;
- threats;
- illegal solicitation;
- prohibited sexual content;
- private client information;
- unsupported serious accusation;
- malicious code;
- unsafe HTML;
- excessive copyrighted copying.

Suggested maximum:
- approximately 4,000 characters.

Longer content should become a Discussion or Review where appropriate.

================================================================================
7. EDITING AND DELETION
================================================================================

Authors may edit under policy.

Display Edited when meaning may have changed.

Moderators retain revision history.

A materially changed comment with replies may preserve prior text or show a revision notice.

Deletion types:
- author removed;
- moderator removed;
- target removed;
- account removed;
- legal restriction;
- private retention where required.

Do not destroy evidence needed for moderation, appeal, security, or legal retention without the
approved process.

================================================================================
8. VISIBILITY
================================================================================

Possible visibility:
- public;
- registered users;
- target participants;
- provider workspace;
- moderator only;
- hidden pending review;
- restricted adult;
- removed placeholder.

Ordinary comments on public targets remain public unless policy requires restriction.

Restricted or NSFW text must not appear in ordinary previews, search snippets, email subjects,
or lock-screen notifications.

================================================================================
9. ROLE-LABELED RESPONSES
================================================================================

Possible labels:
- Provider Response;
- Therapist Response;
- Author Response;
- Staff Response;
- Moderator Notice.

A role label identifies relationship, not truth.

Providers may not:
- suppress lawful criticism;
- demand private information publicly;
- retaliate;
- offer undisclosed compensation for removal;
- impersonate customers.

================================================================================
10. REACTIONS AND SORTING
================================================================================

Possible reactions:
- helpful;
- like;
- insightful;
- question.

Reactions:
- require an eligible account;
- are reversible;
- are rate-limited;
- do not determine factual truth;
- may inform sorting;
- are protected from manipulation.

Possible sorting:
- oldest;
- newest;
- most helpful;
- provider responses;
- staff notices.

Pinned content must not hide criticism.

================================================================================
11. TRANSLATION
================================================================================

Preserve original language.

Offer Translate on demand.

Machine translation:
- is labeled;
- may be cached;
- provides Show Original;
- becomes stale after edit;
- may be reported.

Do not pretranslate every comment into every language.

================================================================================
12. NOTIFICATIONS
================================================================================

Possible notifications:
- reply;
- mention;
- reaction summary;
- provider response;
- moderator action;
- thread closure.

Users control notification preferences.

Notifications must protect:
- NSFW content;
- private targets;
- complaints;
- blocked-user context;
- sensitive personal information.

================================================================================
13. MODERATION
================================================================================

Possible actions:
- allow;
- warn;
- reduce reach;
- hide pending review;
- remove;
- lock replies;
- mark spam;
- request evidence;
- redirect to Support;
- redirect to Review;
- redirect to Discussion;
- restrict account;
- escalate.

AI may flag:
- spam;
- harassment;
- solicitation;
- personal data;
- NSFW;
- duplicate text;
- suspicious links;
- serious accusation.

AI does not make serious final decisions independently.

================================================================================
14. REPORTING AND APPEAL
================================================================================

Report reasons:
- spam;
- harassment;
- personal information;
- impersonation;
- prohibited content;
- false serious allegation;
- illegal solicitation;
- undisclosed promotion;
- wrong target;
- copyright;
- other triaged.

Eligible moderation actions may be appealed.

Disagreement is not automatically abuse.

================================================================================
15. CORRECTION AND SUPPORT REDIRECTION
================================================================================

A comment saying profile information is wrong does not directly change the profile.

Offer:
- Submit Correction;
- Add Source;
- Report Closed;
- Report Moved;
- Report Duplicate.

The correction enters Data Research and Verification.

Technical, account, billing, privacy, security, or complaint matters enter Support.

================================================================================
16. ANTI-ABUSE
================================================================================

Use:
- rate limits;
- verification and account-standing signals;
- duplicate detection;
- link limits;
- spam filters;
- block and mute;
- report thresholds;
- coordinated-activity detection;
- temporary posting limits;
- moderator review.

Do not require public legal identity merely to reduce spam.

================================================================================
19. CURRENT DIRECTIONS
================================================================================

- Use Comments for lightweight responses that do not merit a full Review or Discussion.
- Allow Comments on articles, Reviews, spas, therapists, products, services, comics, and other
  approved records.
- Comments do not affect Ratings.
- Keep reply nesting limited.
- Preserve original language and translate on demand.
- Label provider, author, therapist, staff, and moderator responses.
- Route factual corrections to Data Research.
- Route technical and account problems to Support.
- Route long conversations to Discussions.

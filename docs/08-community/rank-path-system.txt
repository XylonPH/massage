================================================================================
1. RANK PATH PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

Rank Paths are fictional internal progression systems. They are not real-world credentials,
licenses, verification, employment titles, professional qualifications, or workspace authority.

A user may progress in multiple paths at the same time. The account is not limited to one rank.
Users may choose which eligible rank title to feature publicly, while progress in other paths
remains recorded according to privacy settings.

Rank progression may use:
- Nexus Points;
- eligible activity type;
- approved contribution counts;
- path-specific requirements;
- badges or milestones.

Ranks cannot be purchased directly with Embers or Inklings. Spendable currencies may unlock rank
cosmetics but not the rank itself. Reputation and administrative roles do not automatically map to
a fictional rank.

The term Path is accepted. Avoid Track for this system unless a later naming decision replaces it.

8. PROGRESSION AND DISPLAY DIRECTION
================================================================================

Each path requires a versioned progression definition. Exact thresholds remain unresolved. A
definition should identify eligible event families, minimum quality or approval state, exclusions,
milestones, demotion or correction behavior, and the title unlocked at each level.

Revoked, rejected, duplicated, fraudulent, self-dealing, quarantined, or otherwise ineligible
activity does not count merely because a raw activity counter increased. Correcting a source event
must trigger safe recomputation without erasing history.

The public profile may show one selected rank title and optionally a path icon or progress summary.
It must label the title as Massage Nexus progression where confusion with a credential or authority
is possible. Users may hide rank presentation without deleting progression history.

Rank progress must not be embedded as an unbounded structure in user_main. The future authoritative
progress record should support one user plus one path, progression-definition version, eligible
totals, current level, selected-display state, recomputation time, and audit references. Final
collection names and structure guides remain unresolved.

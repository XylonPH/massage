This document is separate from the Review Writing guide. A review is written experience content. A rating is the score or reaction connected to a target.

Core Principle
--------------

Massage Nexus ratings should help users understand quality without allowing simple popularity, self-promotion, spam, or repeated voting to dominate the result.

The system should support quick reactions, detailed experience ratings, editorial ratings, booking/session ratings, public score summaries, and future award calculations while keeping the public display understandable.

Rating Families
---------------

Massage Nexus uses two main rating families.

1 to 10 Ratings
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The following targets use a 1 to 10 rating system:

- spa;
- therapist;
- product.

These are the serious quality ratings. They may affect official scores, profile display, browse/search ranking, future awards, badges, and reputation-related systems.

Thumbs Up / Thumbs Down Ratings
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The following targets use thumbs up and thumbs down only:

- article;
- review;
- news;
- guide;
- comic or comic strip;
- comment;
- discussion thread;
- media upload;
- translation.

These are content reaction ratings. They are not 1 to 10 scores. The public question is Helpful?
with Helpful and Not Helpful choices; thumb icons may support the control but do not replace its
accessible text label.

Helpful means the content assisted the user in understanding, deciding, or completing the task for
which it was presented. Not Helpful does not by itself mean false, abusive, unsafe, or deserving of
moderation. A user may optionally provide a private reason. Reporting false, abusive, unsafe, or
policy-violating content remains a separate action.

Not Included as Rateable Items for Now
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The following should not be treated as normal rateable items in this version:

- profile correction;
- service listing;
- factual contribution;
- submitted establishment information;
- submitted therapist information;
- submitted product information.

These are contributions that may need verification, approval, or contribution scoring, but not public thumbs up/down rating in this version.

Rating, Review, and Reputation
------------------------------

These should remain separate.

Concept    | Meaning
-----------+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Rating     | The score or reaction given by a user, editor, correspondent, owner, or system-supported actor.
Review     | A written experience-based evaluation.
Reputation | A long-term trust signal affected by ratings, helpfulness, moderation, behavior, and contribution quality.

When someone rates a review with thumbs up or thumbs down, they are rating the review content.

That content rating may later affect the Review Author’s reputation, but the rating itself is not the same as reputation.

A single Not Helpful reaction must not directly create a reputation penalty. Reputation uses
weighted, capped, cohort-aware, abuse-checked patterns and confirmed moderation outcomes according
to the Reputation System.

When someone rates a spa, therapist, or product, they are rating the target, not automatically rating the owner, seller, or business account personally.

Rating Visibility and Anonymity
-------------------------------

Standalone registered-user ratings are anonymous publicly by default.

This means:

- the public should not see who rated;
- spa owners should not see who rated their spa;
- therapists should not see who rated them;
- sellers or product owners should not see who rated their product;
- authors should not see exactly who rated their content;
- users can see their own rating history;
- admins and authorized moderators may see rating records internally when needed.

Anonymous public display does not mean anonymous to the system.

Exceptions and Privacy Controls
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

- a review-linked rating follows the public byline or anonymous-publication choice of the review;
- an editorial rating is attributed to Massage Nexus or the approved editorial identity rather
  than presented as an anonymous ordinary-user rating;
- a self-rating or connected-party rating is internally labeled as connected, but the public must
  not expose a staff member or other connected person through a small identifiable breakdown;
- provider dashboards use delayed or time-bucketed summaries and minimum cohort sizes before
  showing filtered results;
- exact rating timestamps, booking identifiers, client attributes, and small service slices must
  not allow a provider to infer the rater;
- private rating identity and audit history require purpose-limited access, audit logging, and the
  approved retention, objection, restriction, and erasure or blocking rules.

1 to 10 Rating Modes
--------------------

Spa, therapist, and product ratings may use either a simple rating or a criteria rating.

Simple Rating
~~~~~~~~~~~~~

A simple rating is one overall score from 1 to 10.

It is faster to submit but less useful than a criteria rating.

Criteria Rating
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

A criteria rating offers five target-specific 1 to 10 criteria. A user may select Not Observed or
Not Applicable rather than guessing.

It provides richer data, supports fairer scoring, and can support future category-based awards.

For official scoring, a criteria rating should count more strongly than a simple rating.

Completeness Weight
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Rating Mode             | Completeness Weight
------------------------+--------------------
Five answered criteria  | 1.00
Four answered criteria  | 0.80
Three answered criteria | 0.60
Simple rating           | 0.60

A submission with fewer than three answered criteria is treated as a simple rating only when the
user supplies an explicit overall score; otherwise it remains incomplete and does not affect the
official score. Each public criterion summary must show its own eligible response count.

If simple and criteria data exist for the same experience and source, eligible criteria data is
preferred. User-period selection across different experiences follows the deterministic rules in
User-Period Rating.

Spa Rating Criteria
-------------------

Spa ratings use five criteria.

Criterion   | Meaning
------------+-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cleanliness | Hygiene, linens, smell, bathroom condition, visible sanitation, and general physical cleanliness.
Comfort     | Ambience, room setup, noise, temperature, relaxation, and how easy it is to settle into the experience.
Privacy     | Room separation, discretion, draping-related environment, safety, and whether the client feels properly protected.
Service     | Reception, booking, waiting, handoff, staff handling, organization, and overall service flow.
Value       | Whether the experience fits the price, class, service promise, and user expectation.

Use Service, not “Service Flow.”

Spa criteria are for spa or massage-establishment experience. They should not be used as generic criteria for all establishments in every future project. A restaurant, clinic, hotel, or different business type may need its own criteria.

Therapist Rating Criteria
-------------------------

Therapist ratings use five criteria.

These criteria apply to both spa-employed therapists and freelance therapists.

Criterion       | Meaning
----------------+------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Technique       | Massage skill, bodywork quality, flow, and ability to perform the intended service.
Pressure        | Pressure control, adjustment, sensitivity, and ability to respond to the client’s requested intensity.
Professionalism | Hygiene, boundaries, presentation, respect, and appropriate conduct.
Communication   | Listening, checking in, explaining when needed, and understanding the client’s requests.
Reliability     | Punctuality, focus, consistency, preparedness, and not repeatedly leaving, delaying, or interrupting the session.

Therapist ratings should focus on the therapist, not the spa environment.

Pressure may be Not Applicable for a service where pressure is not a meaningful dimension.
Reliability should evaluate matters reasonably attributable to the therapist rather than a delay,
handoff, or scheduling failure controlled by the establishment.

Product Rating Criteria
-----------------------

Product ratings use five criteria.

Criterion     | Meaning
--------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Quality       | Material, build, formulation, durability, consistency, and general product condition.
Effectiveness | Whether the product does what it claims or is expected to do.
Safety        | Skin or body comfort, irritation risk, stability, safe use, and user confidence.
Usability     | Ease of use, practicality, handling, fit, packaging, and convenience.
Value         | Whether the product is worth the price.

Product criteria must work across many product types, including oils, tools, chairs, towels, uniforms, massage equipment, spa accessories, and senior-care or wellness-related items.

Product ratings do not normally decay over time. If a product significantly changes, it should be treated as a new product or product version.

Safety, Privacy, and Professionalism scores are experience opinions rather than moderation or
legal findings. A low score may offer a separate private report action, but it must not
automatically publish an accusation, declare a product unsafe, or trigger a disciplinary finding.

1 to 10 Rating Scale Labels
---------------------------

Massage Nexus uses a 1 to 10 rating scale for spa, therapist, and product ratings. The score should remain numeric first, but each number may also have a short label for interface display, explanation, filters, visual assets, and public-facing interpretation.

The current accepted rating labels are:

Score | Label     | General Meaning
------+-----------+---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1     | Fractured | Very poor, broken, unsafe, misleading, or deeply unsatisfactory.
2     | Dim       | Poor and clearly below acceptable expectations.
3     | Low       | Weak, disappointing, or seriously lacking.
4     | Uneven    | Mixed but leaning negative; some acceptable parts, but too many problems.
5     | Fair      | Acceptable but ordinary, limited, or not especially recommendable.
6     | Promising | Some good qualities, but still needs improvement or consistency.
7     | Good      | Solid, useful, and generally positive.
8     | Strong    | Clearly good, reliable, and worth considering.
9     | Excellent | Very high quality with only minor issues, if any.
10    | Signature | Exceptional, memorable, and strongly representative of the best experience expected for its class.

The label does not replace the numeric score. Public display may show both, such as:

Code block (text):
    8 / 10 — Strong

For criteria ratings, each answered criterion receives a 1 to 10 numeric score. The overall event
score is the arithmetic mean of answered criteria. Not Observed and Not Applicable do not enter the
numerator or denominator.

Integer labels describe integer submissions. When a decimal event or public score uses a label,
round the score to the nearest integer using half-up rounding only for label selection. Preserve
and display the decimal score separately so the label does not imply false precision. Localization
must preserve the numeric meaning and must not silently make 5 or 6 the neutral point; the
mathematical midpoint of a 1-to-10 scale is 5.5.



No Thumbs Up / Down for Spa, Therapist, or Product
--------------------------------------------------

Spa, therapist, and product targets should not have thumbs up/down as their main rating system.

They should use:

- simple 1 to 10 rating; or
- criteria-based 1 to 10 rating.

Optional “would recommend” voting is not part of this version.

Who Can Rate
------------

Guests
~~~~~~

Guests should not be allowed to submit official ratings.

Guests may browse ratings and may later have temporary analytics interactions, but guest activity should not affect official scores.

Registered Users
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Registered users may rate.

New registered users may rate and may receive stronger screening until they build trust. Their
documented Reputation Level supplies T_i. Do not apply an undocumented additional weight merely
because the account is new; any future account-age factor requires an explicit formula revision.

Editorial and Review Correspondents
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Editorial ratings and Review Correspondent ratings may carry higher weight because they are more structured and are expected to follow platform standards.

Owners, Therapists, Sellers, and Connected Parties
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Connected parties may rate their own connected record. Self-rating is an accepted Massage Nexus
feature, but it is a disclosed connected signal rather than a neutral customer rating and receives
very low official weight.

This allows engagement without allowing self-promotion to control the official score.

Examples:

- a spa owner may rate their own spa;
- a therapist may rate their own therapist profile;
- a seller or product owner may rate their own product;
- a connected staff member may rate a spa they are attached to.

Rules for self and connected ratings:

- the relationship is recorded and the applicable Connection Modifier is mandatory;
- a self or connected rating cannot unlock an official public score by itself or satisfy the
  independent-rater threshold;
- it may contribute its bounded XCES weight after the public-score threshold is satisfied;
- it earns no Nexus Points, Embers, Inklings, rating milestone, Trusted Rater eligibility,
  Reputation gain, award eligibility, or other participation reward;
- it must not masquerade as an independent customer review or rating;
- alternate-account self-rating, concealed relationships, coordinated staff rating, and coercion
  remain manipulation and may invalidate the rating;
- an official response, factual correction, private self-assessment, and public self-rating remain
  separate actions.

Actor and Connection Weights
----------------------------

The scoring weight accounts for both the actor's platform role ($A_i$) and their connection/relationship ($K_i$) to the target. These weights are multiplied: $w_i = A_i \times K_i \times \dots$

Platform Role Weight ($A_i$):

Role                                           | Weight
-----------------------------------------------+-------
Editorial rating / Review Correspondent rating | 2.00
Normal registered user                         | 1.00

Relationship Connection Modifier ($K_i$):

Relationship                                   | Weight
-----------------------------------------------+-------
Unconnected user                               | 1.00
Connected staff                                | 0.15
Owner / self-rating                            | 0.05

User Reputation modifies the normal registered-user trust factor only through T_i as defined below.

Editorial rating should remain the strongest ordinary rating type.

Connected staff and owner/self ratings should not unlock a public official score by themselves.

Former ownership, employment, contractor, seller, sponsor, family, household, and other material
relationships require disclosure and a separately approved connection rule. When connection is
unknown or under review, the rating does not receive the Unconnected User modifier by default.

Reputation Weight
-----------------

Normal registered-user rating weight increases or decreases based on the user’s Reputation Level. Reputation affects the trust weight used in XCES, but it should not make normal user ratings stronger than editorial ratings.

Reputation Level | Label         | XCES Trust Weight
-----------------+---------------+------------------
1                | Unsettled     | 0.50
2                | Warming Up    | 0.75
3                | Balanced      | 1.00
4                | Grounded      | 1.10
5                | Steady Hands  | 1.20
6                | Trusted Touch | 1.30
7                | Guiding Hand  | 1.40
8                | Nexus Pillar  | 1.50

Editorial rating / Review Correspondent rating remains stronger than ordinary user reputation weight.

For an editorial or Review Correspondent rating, T_i is 1.00. Editorial quality control is
represented by A_i rather than multiplied again through the contributor's personal Reputation.
The Connection Modifier still applies when an editorial actor has a material relationship to the
target.

If a user has a reputation status modifier such as Under Review, Limited, Frozen, or Restricted, the effective trust weight may be paused, reduced, disabled, or temporarily forced lower according to the Reputation System document.

The ordinary user's T_i is captured when the rating becomes active so later Reputation gains do
not retroactively amplify old ratings. A later moderation restriction, confirmed manipulation, or
account invalidation may reduce or disable earlier influence through an audited recalculation.
Rating-derived Reputation from an event must never increase that same event's captured T_i.

Source Weight
-------------

A rating source affects how strongly the rating should count.

Working source weights:

Rating Source                                       | Source Weight
----------------------------------------------------+-------------
Massage Nexus verified completed booking/session    | 1.25
Review-linked rating                                | 1.00
Confirmed external or on-site visit                 | 1.00
Direct profile rating not tied to a visit or review | 0.75
User-entered manual history rating                  | 0.50
Unknown or unresolved source classification         | Not score-eligible until resolved

A user-entered manual history rating may be useful for the user’s personal history, but it should have lower official weight unless verified.

Every score-eligible spa, therapist, or product rating requires the user to attest to a relevant
real experience or product use. Direct Profile Rating means the experience is not tied to a
verified visit, booking, or review; it does not authorize rating solely from profile appearance,
price, identity, photographs, reputation, or disagreement with another person's review.

A spa or therapist manually encoding their own record should not create rewards and should not become a strong official rating signal. An allowed self-rating uses the applicable experience source weight together with the Owner / Self-Rating Connection Modifier; it does not receive a special high-trust source.

Payment is not the trust fact. A free, prepaid, package, loyalty, insurance-supported, promotional,
or otherwise non-cash session may use the verified completed-session source when completion and
the rater-to-experience relationship are verified. A payment record without completed service does
not qualify.

Recency Weight
--------------

Spa and therapist quality can change over time. A spa may improve cleanliness, staffing, management, service flow, or facilities. A therapist may improve skill, communication, consistency, or professionalism.

For spa and therapist ratings, use a smooth recency weight based on the experience-completion
instant rather than submission or edit time.

Recency anchors:

Age of Experience | Recency Weight
------------------+---------------
0 days            | 1.00
83 days           | 0.75
166 days          | 0.50
365 days           | 0.25
Over 365 days     | 0.25

Linearly interpolate between anchors. This avoids an abrupt score change at a band boundary.
A manual-history rating must preserve the claimed experience date. If the experience date cannot
be reasonably established, it receives the manual-history source weight and the minimum recency
weight rather than appearing recent. Editing a rating does not refresh recency.

Product ratings use:

Product Rating         | Recency Weight
-----------------------+---------------
Default product rating | 1.00

The 83-Day Rating Cycle
-----------------------

Massage Nexus uses an 83-day cycle for spa and therapist rating freshness, reward eligibility, and user-period aggregation.

The 83-day cycle is a rolling separation interval of exactly 83 × 24 hours evaluated from UTC
experience-completion instants. It is not a monthly, quarterly, platform-wide, or first-rating-
anchored reset. Historical user-period summaries remain eligible with recency decay; the interval
prevents one user from creating two active summaries for the same target less than 83 days apart.

Explanation for Spa Owners and Therapists
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Massage Nexus uses an 83-day rating cycle because spa and therapist quality can change over time, but not usually overnight.

Eighty-three days is close to three months: long enough to limit repeated influence and short
enough to reflect several genuine experiences in a year. Because the interval is rolling, it does
not create one platform-wide reset. Abuse controls must not depend on secrecy or unpredictability.

Xylon Calibrated Experience Score
---------------------------------

The official weighted rating formula is called:

Code block (text):
    Xylon Calibrated Experience Score

Short code:

Code block (text):
    XCES

XCES is currently governed for Massage Nexus. Reuse in another Xylon project requires a separately
approved shared specification and project-specific calibration rather than copying these weights
unchanged.

It is most useful for service or experience-based targets such as spas, therapists, restaurants, service providers, locations, and similar real-world experiences.

Product ratings may use a modified XCES without recency decay.

Rating Event Score
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

For a criteria rating:

Code block (text):
    rᵢ = sum of answered criterion scores ÷ number of answered criteria

For a spa rating:

Code block (text):
    rᵢ = (Cleanliness + Comfort + Privacy + Service + Value) ÷ 5

For a therapist rating:

Code block (text):
    rᵢ = (Technique + Pressure + Professionalism + Communication + Reliability) ÷ 5

For a product rating:

Code block (text):
    rᵢ = (Quality + Effectiveness + Safety + Usability + Value) ÷ 5

For a simple rating:

Code block (text):
    rᵢ = submitted overall score

Rating Event Weight
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The rating event weight is:

Code block (text):
    wᵢ = Aᵢ × Tᵢ × Sᵢ × Cᵢ × Rᵢ × Kᵢ

Where:

Symbol | Meaning
-------+---------------------------
Aᵢ     | actor or role weight
Tᵢ     | trust or reputation weight
Sᵢ     | source weight
Cᵢ     | completeness weight
Rᵢ     | recency weight
Kᵢ     | connection weight

Use 1.00 only when the specification explicitly defines a factor as not applicable. Missing,
unknown, conflicting, or unresolved source, connection, eligibility, or moderation information
must not silently receive full weight.

Before weight calculation, a rating must be active, attributable to an authenticated eligible
account, attached to one valid target and experience context, within the allowed numeric range,
and not rejected, revoked, superseded, quarantined, duplicate, or invalidated for abuse. A rating
that fails eligibility receives no official weight but may remain in restricted audit history when
lawfully necessary.

Factor Evaluation and Versioning
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

- r_i and C_i come from the active rating revision; edits retain prior revisions for the approved
  audit period and do not create a new experience;
- A_i and ordinary-user T_i are captured when the event first becomes Active;
- S_i may change through audited experience verification, reversal, or source correction;
- K_i may change when a material relationship is discovered, corrected, started, or ended under
  the applicable connection policy;
- R_i is recalculated from the preserved experience-completion instant at the summary as-of time;
- moderation and eligibility may reduce weight to zero and later restore it after an approved
  reversal;
- every summary retains the scoring-formula version, prior version, as-of instant, and rebuild
  reason needed to reproduce the result.

Official XCES Formula
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

First calculate the eligible weighted mean:

Code block (text):
    M = Σ(rᵢ × wᵢ) ÷ Σ(wᵢ)

Then apply a confidence prior:

Code block (text):
    XCES = (Σ(rᵢ × wᵢ) + (m × P)) ÷ (Σ(wᵢ) + m)

Where:

Symbol | Meaning
-------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------
M      | Eligible weighted mean before confidence adjustment.
P      | Current target-family prior mean; use 5.50 until a governed prior exists.
m      | Prior strength; current working value is 5.00 effective weight units.

The prior must be versioned and computed from an eligible representative independent population rather than
changed opportunistically for one target. A prior change triggers an audited summary rebuild.
The public must not display a prior-only score. If Σ(w_i) is zero, the result is Not Enough Ratings
rather than division by zero.

Readable version:

    Weight eligible ratings, combine them with a small target-family prior, and let observed
    independent experience progressively outweigh that prior as effective signal grows.

User-Period Rating
------------------

To prevent one user from overpowering a spa or therapist score through repeated ratings, one user
contributes no more than one active user-period rating per target in any rolling 83-day span.

This is called a user-period rating.

Across rating history, a user may have:

- direct profile rating;
- review-linked rating;
- completed booking/session rating;
- confirmed external/on-site visit rating;
- user-entered manual history rating.

The system resolves them deterministically before public XCES is computed:

1. Exclude ineligible, revoked, superseded, rejected, quarantined, and duplicate events.
2. Group records representing the same experience. Prefer an eligible criteria rating over a
   simple rating for that same experience; prefer the strongest verified source when duplicate
   records represent that same experience.
3. Sort the remaining experience ratings from newest to oldest by experience-completion instant.
4. Select the newest rating as a user-period rating, then suppress other ratings for that user and
   target whose experience instant is less than 83 × 24 hours before the selected rating.
5. Repeat step 4 with the newest remaining unsuppressed rating until no events remain. This creates
   a sequence of historical user-period ratings separated by at least 83 days.
6. If experience instants tie, select the strongest source, then the highest completeness, then
   the latest original submission instant, then the lowest stable rating-event identifier.
7. Use each selected event's score and applicable factor snapshots for its user-period rating.
   Suppressed events remain excluded from XCES without being destroyed.

If the selected event is later revoked or invalidated, recompute the user-period rating from the
remaining eligible events. Editing, deleting, revoking, or resubmitting must not reset the
experience date or create another reward.

Priority Within a User-Period
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The strongest sources should influence the user-period rating more than weaker sources.

Source priority used only for same-experience or exact-time tie resolution:

1. Massage Nexus verified completed booking/session rating;
2. review-linked rating;
3. confirmed external or on-site visit rating;
4. direct profile rating;
5. user-entered manual history rating.

This limits one user to roughly four active summaries per year while allowing historical experience
to decay rather than disappear. Source strength affects the selected event's weight rather than
permanently overriding a newer experience.

Session and Booking Ratings
---------------------------

Completed booking or session ratings should be supported.

A completed spa booking may produce:

- a spa rating;
- a therapist rating, if the therapist is known.

A completed freelance therapist booking may produce:

- a therapist rating.

A booking/session rating is tied to a real service event and may feed the target’s user-period rating.

Rating Experience Context
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Each target rating preserves, when applicable:

- stable target identifier and target type;
- establishment branch, professional context, or product version;
- normalized service and provider menu item;
- therapist when known and permitted;
- price and currency context;
- experience-completion, original-submission, and last-edit instants;
- booking, session, review, or external-visit evidence reference;
- source, verification, disclosure, connection, moderation, and eligibility states;
- rating mode, answered criteria, Not Observed or Not Applicable selections, and overall score;
- factor snapshots and scoring-formula version.

Public context must remain privacy-preserving. Exact booking, client, payment, intake, or private
location details do not become public rating metadata.

Booking and Session Reward Rule
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Session ratings may earn rewards only when they come from a real qualifying activity and pass the
reward, moderation, duplicate, and abuse checks in User Rewards and Gamification.

Reward eligibility and amounts are authoritative only in
docs/08-community/user-rewards-and-gamification.txt. This document does not duplicate the reward
table. Self, connected, manual-history, revoked, invalidated, quarantined, duplicate, and
reward-period repeat ratings do not earn rating rewards unless the rewards specification records a
narrow explicit exception; self-rating has no such exception.

Same-day cap:

Code block (text):
    Same user + same spa + same UTC reward-ledger day = one rewarded spa session rating only.
    Same user + same therapist + same UTC reward-ledger day = one rewarded therapist session rating only.

A user may still submit additional ratings if the system allows it, but the additional same-target same-day ratings do not earn additional rewards.

Rating Changes, Deletion, and Revocation
----------------------------------------

Users may change or revoke their own ratings.

Rules:

- only the active rating should affect the current score;
- restricted prior history may be retained for the approved audit and abuse-detection period;
- changing a rating should not create repeat rewards;
- deleting or revoking and then re-rating should not create repeat rewards within the active reward period;
- spa and therapist rating reward eligibility may refresh after a new valid 83-day cycle and a new experience;
- product ratings normally do not refresh unless the product or product version changes.

Public Score Display
--------------------

A public official score should display only when there is enough independent eligible rating
signal. Connected influence is applied only after this threshold is satisfied.

Working display rule:

Display a public XCES when both conditions are met:

- at least five distinct independent users have an active eligible user-period rating; and
- their combined effective weight before the confidence prior is at least 3.00.

One or more editorial ratings may display a separately labeled Editorial Score before the XCES
threshold is met. Editorial signal does not masquerade as an independent community consensus or
unlock the official score by itself.

Re-evaluate the threshold after revocation, moderation, expiration, connection correction, merge,
or rebuild. If it is no longer satisfied, hide the public XCES, stop applying connected influence,
and return to Not Enough Ratings while preserving the eligible history.

Do not unlock the public score using only:

- owner/self-ratings;
- connected staff ratings;
- unverified internal history entries.

After unlocking, the combined effective weight of all current self and connected-party ratings is
capped at the lower of:

Code block (text):
    10 percent of current independent effective weight
    0.50 effective weight units

Apply proportional scaling when connected signal exceeds the cap. This preserves allowed
self-rating while preventing a large staff or ownership group from overpowering independent
experience. Apply the cap to connected w_i values before the XCES numerator and denominator are
calculated, and store both uncapped and effective connected weight for restricted audit and
explainability.

Before the threshold is reached, display:

Code block (text):
    Not enough ratings yet.

Recommended public display:

Code block (text):
    8.3 / 10
    Based on 27 active independent user-period ratings
    Provisional

Display Provisional until at least 20 distinct independent users and 15.00 independent effective
weight units exist. A provisional score may appear on a profile but must not
outrank mature scores solely by its point estimate or qualify a target for a received-rating award.

When connected ratings contribute, disclose Includes Low-Weight Connected Signals without
revealing a person through an unsafe small breakdown. Public summaries should also show rating
distribution, freshness, criteria-specific response counts, and verified-experience proportion
when cohort size permits.

Display Precision Rule
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

- Public display score: round half-up to exactly one decimal place, for example 8.3 / 10.
- Internal score calculation: use deterministic fixed-decimal arithmetic with at least six decimal
  places. Do not use binary floating-point behavior as the scoring contract.
- Ranking tie-breaks must use approved confidence, effective signal, freshness, and stable final
  identifiers rather than hidden extra display decimals alone.

Rating Summaries and System Performance
---------------------------------------

The site should not recompute ratings from all raw records on every page load.

Use stored summaries.

Possible summary layers:

- rating event records;
- user-period rating summaries;
- daily rating summaries;
- weekly rating summaries;
- monthly rating summaries;
- yearly rating summaries;
- current public rating summary;
- criteria-specific summaries.

Computation Schedule
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Current public summaries update incrementally after an eligible rating state change. A scheduled
UTC reconciliation verifies stored summaries against eligible source records and applies recency
changes. Historical summaries are created only when required by an approved report or feature.

Working reconciliation schedule:

Summary         | Computation Timing
----------------+-------------------------------------------------------------
Current summary | Event-driven update plus nightly UTC reconciliation
Daily summary   | After the previous UTC day closes, when required
Weekly summary  | Every Monday UTC for the previous Monday to Sunday, when required
Monthly summary | First UTC day of the month for the previous calendar month, when required
Yearly summary  | January 1 UTC for the previous calendar year, when required

Do not use the 23rd-of-the-month rule.

Provide an authorized, audited, idempotent rebuild operation for formula changes, restored or
corrected records, target merges or splits, prior changes, moderation reversals, and summary
recovery. It must support dry-run comparison and must not silently rewrite raw rating history.

Raw, Editorial, Recent, and Official Scores
-------------------------------------------

These terms may be useful internally.

Score Type      | Meaning
----------------+-----------------------------------------------------------
Raw average     | Simple average with no weighting.
Editorial score | Score from editorial or Review Correspondent ratings only.
Recent average  | Score from recent ratings only.
Official score  | Public XCES score using the weighted formula.

For public display, show the official score first.

Other score types may be internal, analytical, or future-facing.

Rating Visual Asset Direction
-----------------------------

Massage Nexus rating visuals should not use a normal star-rating system.

The current asset direction uses a connected pulse / nexus node visual language. This connects the rating interface to the Massage Nexus identity of linked experiences, trust signals, and service quality rather than generic stars.

The rating asset system includes:

- square rating token assets for compact score display;
- horizontal rating pill assets for profile cards, review cards, and rating summaries;
- CSS rating components for live UI use;
- rating choice controls for selecting 1 to 10;
- a rating meter for public score summaries;
- transparent SVG asset backgrounds for use in light and dark mode.

The visual direction should remain:

- professional;
- readable;
- scalable;
- CSS/SVG-friendly;
- not fantasy-styled;
- not star-based;
- not dependent on generated image text.

The asset set uses the accepted 1-to-10 labels defined earlier in this document. Presentation
assets do not change the XCES formula or criteria-rating structure.


Rating Rewards
--------------

User Rewards and Gamification is the sole authority for rating reward amounts, verified-
experience bonuses, daily and periodic caps, and reward-ledger behavior. Rating reward values must
not be duplicated here.

This system supplies eligibility facts to the reward ledger. It must prevent repeat rewards for
editing, revoking and resubmitting, repeat target activity inside the reward period, duplicate or
fake experiences, alternate accounts, self or connected ratings, invalidated ratings, and
reward-farming behavior. A scoring weight does not imply reward eligibility, and reward receipt
does not increase rating weight.

Rating Abuse Prevention
-----------------------

Possible abuse patterns include:

- owners asking staff to rate;
- fake accounts;
- fake bookings;
- repeated same-day ratings;
- coordinated rating campaigns;
- self-rating with alternate accounts;
- low-effort thumbs up/down farming;
- product seller manipulation;
- suspicious exact matching with editorial ratings;
- rating without real experience;
- rating competitors unfairly.
- provider coercion, retaliation, or incentives tied to a required score;
- copying a visible editorial or public criterion score rather than giving an independent rating;
- changing experience dates to refresh recency or rewards;
- concealing a current or former material connection.

Controls include:

- registered-user requirement;
- actor and connection weights;
- reputation weights;
- source weights;
- recency weights;
- same-day session reward cap;
- user-period aggregation;
- public display threshold;
- daily reward cap for content reactions;
- private audit history;
- moderation and abuse detection.

Rating State, Moderation, and Appeal
------------------------------------

Scoring distinguishes at least Draft, Active, Quarantined, Revoked by User, Rejected, Superseded,
and Invalidated states. Only Active eligible ratings affect XCES. Providers may report suspected
fraud or factual context without seeing private rater identity or automatically removing a
negative score. Users may challenge an invalidation or connection classification. Material
moderation reversals trigger an audited user-period and target-summary rebuild.

The platform preserves the reason, actor, time, evidence reference, and appeal result for a state
change. Retention remains limited to the approved fraud, safety, legal, support, and audit purpose;
private history is not retained indefinitely merely because it may be useful.

Rating Badges
-------------

The Badge System owns Anchored Milestone Sequence values, badge families, and badge award rules.
This system supplies eligible rating counts. Criteria milestones count eligible
independent criteria ratings, not simple, self, connected, revoked, invalidated, quarantined,
duplicate, or reward-farming ratings. Content-reaction milestones count only eligible retained
Helpful or Not Helpful actions after applicable abuse and daily-cap rules.

Trusted Rater Badge
-------------------

The Trusted Rater badge should apply mainly to spa and therapist criteria ratings.

It should not be based on article votes, comment votes, media ratings, translations, or product ratings in this version.

Working eligibility:

- at least 30 eligible spa/therapist criteria ratings;
- at least 12 ratings overlap with later eligible editorial or sufficiently mature independent
  consensus targets;
- no active abuse, spam, or manipulation flags;
- average alignment score of at least 80 percent.

Alignment Formula
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

For each shared criterion:

Code block (text):
    difference = absolute(user_criterion_score - editorial_criterion_score)
    criterion_alignment = max(0, 1 - (difference / 4))

Then average all eligible criterion alignments.

Only criteria-based ratings are eligible for alignment scoring. Simple 1-to-10 ratings are completely excluded from alignment calculations.

Also exclude self and connected ratings, ratings submitted after the comparison criterion was
shown to that user when independent judgment cannot be established, targets without sufficient
comparison confidence, and comparison ratings later overturned or materially corrected. Use a
leave-one-out consensus so the user's own rating never helps create the benchmark used to judge
that rating.

Example:

Code block (text):
    Editorial Cleanliness: 9
    User Cleanliness: 8
    Difference: 1
    Alignment = 1 - (1 / 4) = 0.75

Exact matching is not always ideal. If a user matches visible editorial or consensus ratings too
perfectly too often, that may be suspicious and should be reviewed.

Alignment measures calibration to a later trusted reference, not objective truth. Trusted Rater
eligibility must also consider reversal rate, target diversity, rating distribution, connection
history, and manipulation findings. The badge is cosmetic evidence of an eligibility milestone;
it does not itself increase XCES weight or grant permission. The formula may be refined later if
badge-specific validation requires adjustment.

Rating Effects on Search and Browse
-----------------------------------

Ratings should affect search and browse ranking, but should not fully control it.

Only a score meeting the approved ranking-confidence threshold may contribute as a positive
organic ranking signal. Not Enough Ratings and provisional scores must not be treated as low
quality, and a provisional high point estimate must not outrank a mature score solely because it
is numerically higher.

XCES may become one ranking input alongside:

- service match;
- location match;
- profile completeness;
- availability or open status;
- review count;
- recent activity;
- verified information;
- sponsorship or promoted placement, clearly labeled;
- random discovery rotation.

The exact search and browse ranking formula should be defined separately.

Sponsored or promoted placement must use a separately labeled placement lane or explicit
sponsorship treatment. Payment must not alter XCES, confidence, organic rating order, rating
eligibility, or public rating labels.

Rating Effects on Reputation
----------------------------

Content ratings may affect the author’s reputation.

Examples:

- thumbs up on an article may help the author;
- thumbs down on a review may hurt or reduce the review author’s reputation effect;
- useful review ratings may help Review Authors over time;
- shared content with multiple authors should share reputation effects.

The exact reputation formula should be defined in the Reputation documentation.

Spa, therapist, and product target ratings should not automatically become personal reputation for owners or sellers, although they may later affect profile trust, badges, awards, or business standing.

Target Lifecycle, Context, and Versioning
-----------------------------------------

- a rating remains attached to the exact establishment branch, therapist identity, professional
  context, or product version represented by the experience;
- relocation, ownership change, therapist disaffiliation, service-context change, reformulation,
  or product revision does not silently rewrite rating context;
- a material product change creates or connects a distinct product version. Version ratings may
  have a separately governed parent-product rollup, but must not be transferred silently;
- target merges, splits, mistaken-identity corrections, and unmerges require relationship review
  before a rating moves or contributes to a new summary;
- a closed, retired, historical, or removed target may preserve eligible historical ratings while
  clearly identifying the represented period and disabling new activity when appropriate;
- suppression or erasure of public target information may also require rating delisting or de-
  identification without treating every restricted audit dependency as publicly displayable.

Awards and Received-Rating Badges
---------------------------------

Spas and therapists may later receive badges or awards based on ratings.

Received-rating badges and awards require mature non-provisional signal, minimum independent
experience and freshness, abuse and connection review, and an approved eligibility period. Self
and connected ratings do not satisfy award thresholds or nomination eligibility even though their
bounded weight may contribute to an already-unlocked XCES.

Possible future examples:

- Highly Rated Spa;
- Top Rated Spa;
- Rising Spa;
- Client Favorite;
- Top Rated Therapist;
- Rising Therapist;
- Trusted Therapist;
- Massage Nexus Award Nominee;
- Massage Nexus Award Winner.

Products do not need the same received-rating badge system in this version.

The award system should be documented separately.

Implementation Acceptance Cases
-------------------------------

Before production use, automated and independently reviewed examples must verify:

- no eligible weight and all-zero eligible weight return Not Enough Ratings without division by
  zero;
- five independent ratings below the effective-weight threshold do not unlock XCES;
- one editorial rating displays only the labeled Editorial Score before XCES unlocks;
- allowed self-ratings retain bounded low influence, never unlock a score, and never earn rewards,
  Reputation, Trusted Rater credit, milestones, badges, or awards;
- excess connected signal is proportionally capped;
- the rolling selection sequence preserves at least 83 days between one user's active summaries,
  and revocation restores the correct previously suppressed event;
- editing or resubmitting does not refresh experience recency or rewards;
- Not Observed and Not Applicable criteria do not enter an event average;
- confidence-prior, fixed-decimal, half-up display, and stable tie-break calculations are
  deterministic across supported runtimes;
- recency interpolation is continuous at 83, 166, and 365 days;
- target merge, split, unmerge, product-version change, moderation reversal, and prior-version
  change rebuild the correct summaries without rewriting raw history;
- provider-facing cohorts, timestamps, and filters do not reveal an anonymous rater;
- repeated, duplicate, alternate-account, connected, and reward-replay attempts fail safely.

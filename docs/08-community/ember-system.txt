================================================================================
1. MEANING AND SCALE
================================================================================

Embers are the scarce, higher-value spendable platform credit. Their clearest use is bounded AI
credit, with selected higher-value feature credits and non-trust benefits allowed under explicit
cost, failure, refund, and budget rules. Embers are more valuable and materially harder to earn than
Inklings; routine clicking, reactions, ratings, polls, surveys, or ordinary participation award zero
Embers.

Embers may be earned only through rare approved sources and may later be purchased through an
approved payment product. Purchase is not active until pricing, consumer disclosure, accounting,
refund, expiry, tax, fraud, and payment obligations are implemented. Embers are not Nexus Points,
Inklings, Reputation, a transferable cash wallet, legal tender, or a cash-out asset.

================================================================================
2. APPROVED USE DIRECTION
================================================================================

Possible cosmetic and participation uses:
- avatar borders, profile frames, backgrounds, and decorations;
- comment flair and badge display slots;
- Rank, theme, and event cosmetics;
- limited ad-reduction or no-ad access;
- event or campaign benefits that do not affect trust.

Possible feature-credit uses:
- limited booking-system credits;
- limited messaging or SMS quota;
- limited promotion or listing-feature credits;
- limited profile enhancements or feature trials;
- small campaign participation benefits.

Small spas, practitioners, and business accounts may redeem Embers for bounded access to selected
premium-like tools. Ember support does not replace the paid business model or create a permanent
entitlement; server, messaging, storage, AI, support, and operational costs still require sustainable
monetization.

================================================================================
3. AI USE
================================================================================

An approved AI feature may use a paid-plan allowance, limited free quota, pay-as-used credit,
campaign credit, or bounded Ember redemption. Possible features include review drafting, profile or
description improvement, practitioner biography assistance, response drafting, massage-type
explanation, content suggestions, rating or review screening, and reward recommendations.

Before spending Embers, the interface must show the cost and require clear confirmation. The system
must prevent duplicate charges, define failure and refund behavior, distinguish draft output from
approved content, maintain provider fallback, and avoid charging for preventable platform failure.
Farmable rewards must not create unlimited AI use. Provider, model, queue, retry, quota, budget, and
safety behavior remain authoritative in the Runtime AI System and monetization specifications.

================================================================================
4. PROHIBITED PURCHASES
================================================================================

Embers must not buy:
- review influence or removal;
- rating weight or organic ranking;
- Reputation, trust labels, or verification;
- badge or Rank eligibility where the badge or Rank represents achievement;
- moderation, editorial, administrative, or workspace authority;
- automatic content, contribution, profile, or claim approval;
- professional credentials or factual relationships;
- permanent premium status.

Payment may offer approved convenience or presentation benefits, but it follows the same trust and
authority boundary.

================================================================================
5. ACCOUNTING AND OPEN DECISIONS
================================================================================

Every earn, spend, expiry, refund, correction, or reversal is an immutable user_reward event under
the Reward System. user_main may cache the current Ember balance for display only.

Open decisions:
- final earning rules and source caps;
- redemption catalogue and costs;
- expiry, refund, and failed-delivery rules;
- direct-purchase pricing, packages, accounting, and launch requirements;
- provider credit limits and monetization safeguards;
- AI quota conversion and model-access rules;
- transfer and gifting policy, which remain disabled unless separately approved.

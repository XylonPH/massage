DOCUMENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
This document defines the working public and member discussion system for Massage Nexus.

The Discussion System allows users, establishments, therapists, sellers, contributors, editors, moderators, and approved platform personas to ask questions, share information, respond, clarify, debate, and build community knowledge.

A discussion is different from a review, private message, live chat, news story, and profile correction.

================================================================================
1. PRIMARY GOALS
================================================================================

The Discussion System should:

- support useful public conversation;
- connect conversations to Services, establishments, therapists, Products, articles, news, reviews, and locations;
- support questions and answers;
- support official responses;
- support moderation and reporting;
- preserve edit history;
- discourage spam, harassment, manipulation, and unsafe solicitation;
- allow multilingual participation;
- support reputation without rewarding raw volume;
- remain readable and searchable;
- support accessibility;
- protect private information;
- allow AI assistance without allowing automated manipulation.

================================================================================
2. DISCUSSION VERSUS OTHER SYSTEMS
================================================================================

Discussion:
Public or group conversation organized around a topic.

Review:
Experience-based evaluation of an establishment, therapist, or Product.

Comment:
A reply attached to content or a discussion.

Private Message:
Direct non-public communication between approved participants.

Live Chat:
Real-time or near-real-time conversation.

Contribution:
Structured suggestion to change platform data.

Report:
Private request for moderation or safety action.

Question:
A discussion seeking an answer.

Discussion should not be used to bypass:

- review rules;
- correction workflow;
- private messaging boundaries;
- booking;
- verification;
- emergency support.

Official response labels may include:

- Establishment Response;

- Seller Response;
- Author Response;
- Editor Note;

- Neural Agent.

Official status should be based on permission and relationship, not self-selected text.

================================================================================
9. CREATING A DISCUSSION
================================================================================

Creation form may include:

- type;
- title;
- body;
- related record;
- language;
- tags;
- location;
- question status;
- poll;
- media;
- disclosure;
- anonymity request;
- notification preference.

The form should search for existing similar discussions before submission.

================================================================================
10. TITLE RULES
================================================================================

A title should:

- describe the topic;
- avoid all-caps;
- avoid clickbait;
- avoid personal private information;
- avoid unsupported accusation;
- remain understandable in search.

Poor title:

Help!!!

Better title:

Which massage type is usually gentler for a first visit?

================================================================================
11. BODY EDITOR
================================================================================

The discussion editor may support:

- paragraph;
- list;
- quotation;
- link;
- mention;
- emoji;
- limited image;
- content warning;
- code only where relevant to technical discussions.

Do not allow:

- script;
- arbitrary HTML;
- unsafe iframe;
- hidden tracking;
- unrestricted CSS.

The editor should be simpler than the article editor.

================================================================================
12. REPLIES AND THREADING
================================================================================

A reply may respond to:

- original discussion;
- another reply.

Recommended threading depth should remain limited for readability.

Possible direction:

- visible nesting up to three levels;
- deeper replies flattened with context link.

Each reply may contain:

- body;
- author;
- date;
- edited status;
- parent;
- reaction;
- report;
- moderation status.

================================================================================
13. QUESTIONS AND ACCEPTED ANSWERS
================================================================================

A Question discussion may allow an accepted answer.

The original author or authorized moderator may mark an answer accepted.

Accepted does not always mean officially verified.

Possible labels:

- Accepted by Author;
- Official Answer;
- Editor-Verified Answer;
- Community Answer.

The system should distinguish them.

================================================================================
14. HELPFUL VOTES AND REACTIONS
================================================================================

Possible reactions:

- Helpful;
- Not Helpful;
- Like;
- Thanks;
- Agree;
- Informative.

The first version should avoid too many reaction types.

Working direction:

- thumbs up;
- thumbs down;
- Helpful mark where needed.

Reactions may create small reputation signals according to the Reputation System.

They should not create large rewards or allow farming.

================================================================================
15. POLLS
================================================================================

A discussion poll may include:

- question;
- options;
- start;
- end;
- single or multiple selection;
- anonymous public result;
- result visibility;
- minimum account age;
- related record.

Polls should not be used as proof of factual truth.

Votes may be limited by account and anti-abuse checks.

Possible visibility:

================================================================================
20. EDITING
================================================================================

Authors may edit according to policy.

Possible edit rules:

- free editing during a short period;
- later edits create visible history;
- substantial edits show Edited;
- edit after replies preserves previous meaning;
- title change may require review;
- serious accusation changes require moderation.

An author should not rewrite a discussion after receiving replies in a way that makes respondents appear misleading.

================================================================================
21. EDIT HISTORY
================================================================================

Public history may show:

- edited date;
- summary;
- prior version where needed.

Internal history may preserve:

- exact before and after;
- reason;
- moderator action;
- source;
- IP or device context where justified.

Minor spelling edits may not need a detailed public diff.

================================================================================
22. DELETION AND ARCHIVE
================================================================================

Author deletion may:

- remove body;
- preserve reply structure;
- show Deleted by Author.

Moderator removal may:

- hide body;
- preserve evidence;
- show moderation reason where appropriate.

Archive may close old or inactive discussions while keeping them searchable.

Permanent erasure is reserved for legal, privacy, or retention requirements.

================================================================================
23. LOCKING
================================================================================

A discussion may be locked when:

- question is resolved;
- conversation is repetitive;
- abuse continues;
- legal risk is active;
- emergency information must remain stable;
- old thread receives spam;
- moderator review is underway.

Locked discussions remain readable unless separately restricted.

================================================================================
24. MERGING DUPLICATE DISCUSSIONS
================================================================================

Merge process:

1. identify duplicate topics;
2. select canonical discussion;
3. move or link useful replies;
4. preserve authorship;
5. redirect old URL;
6. record moderator action.

Do not merge discussions merely because they mention the same Service if the actual questions differ.

================================================================================
25. MODERATION
================================================================================

Moderation may address:

- spam;
- harassment;
- threats;
- impersonation;
- doxxing;
- fraud;
- explicit solicitation;
- illegal activity;
- unsafe medical advice;
- defamation;
- misinformation;
- hate;
- copyright;
- off-topic repetition;
- manipulation;
- brigading.

A negative opinion is not automatically abuse.

================================================================================
26. REPORTING
================================================================================

Users may report:

- discussion;
- reply;
- author;
- media;
- link.

Report reasons may include:

- spam;
- harassment;
- threat;
- private information;
- impersonation;
- false information;
- unsafe advice;
- explicit solicitation;
- illegal content;
- copyright;
- wrong category.

Reports are private.

================================================================================
27. MODERATION ACTIONS
================================================================================

Possible actions:

- no action;
- warning;
- request edit;
- label;
- limit visibility;
- remove media;
- remove reply;
- lock discussion;
- restrict author;
- suspend account;
- escalate;
- preserve evidence;
- refer to legal or safety process.

Serious actions should have:

- reason;
- evidence;
- reviewer;
- date;
- appeal;
- audit.

================================================================================
28. APPEALS
================================================================================

An author may appeal an eligible moderation decision.

Appeal may include:

- decision;
- reason;
- explanation;
- supporting evidence;
- requested outcome.

A different authorized reviewer should handle serious appeals when possible.

================================================================================
29. SAFETY AND SENSITIVE TOPICS
================================================================================

Sensitive discussions may involve:

- sexual boundaries;
- adult-adjacent services;
- harassment;
- assault;
- health;
- pregnancy;
- minors;
- disability;
- grief;
- death;
- self-harm;
- exploitation;
- illegal activity.

Possible controls:

- content warning;
- limited preview;
- member-only visibility;
- slower moderation;
- link restriction;
- identity protection;
- comment lock;
- specialist review.

The system should not allow discussion to become a marketplace for illegal or exploitative activity.

================================================================================
30. MEDICAL AND PROFESSIONAL ADVICE
================================================================================

Users may share experience and general information.

They should not present unsupported advice as diagnosis or guaranteed treatment.

Possible notice:

Community discussion does not replace medical, legal, or professional advice.

High-risk medical claims may be labeled, limited, or removed.

================================================================================
31. BUSINESS AND PROVIDER DISCUSSIONS
================================================================================

Providers may answer questions but may not:

- threaten critics;
- reveal client identity;
- fabricate testimonials;
- manipulate votes;
- advertise repeatedly outside approved areas;
- remove independent discussion.

Commercial promotion should use approved promotion or advertising systems.

================================================================================
32. REVIEWS AND DISCUSSIONS
================================================================================

A discussion about a review may:

- ask for clarification;
- discuss general lessons;
- allow an official response;
- identify correction needs.

It should not:

- reveal anonymous reviewer identity;
- coordinate harassment;
- relitigate moderation endlessly;
- replace the review response system.

================================================================================
33. NEWS DISCUSSIONS
================================================================================

News discussions may be enabled, limited, or disabled.

Special care is required for:

- active investigation;
- accusation;
- victim;
- minor;
- death;
- legal case;
- breaking emergency.

A moderator may pin verified updates.

================================================================================
34. MENTIONS
================================================================================

Mentions may notify:

- user;
- establishment;
- therapist;
- seller;
- official account.

Controls should prevent:

- mass mention;
- harassment;
- repeated tagging;
- fake official mention.

Users should be able to limit mention notifications.

================================================================================
35. FOLLOWING AND NOTIFICATIONS
================================================================================

Users may follow:

- discussion;
- category;
- related record;
- author.

Possible notifications:

- reply;
- mention;
- accepted answer;
- official answer;
- moderator note;
- lock;
- merge;
- report decision;
- followed topic update.

Users should control notification frequency.

================================================================================
36. SEARCH AND DISCOVERY
================================================================================

Discussion search may use:

- title;
- body;
- tag;
- category;
- related record;
- location;
- language;
- author;
- status;
- date.

Search results should exclude restricted and removed content.

Duplicate detection may suggest existing threads before creation.

================================================================================
37. SORTING
================================================================================

Possible sorts:

- latest;
- recently active;
- unanswered;
- most helpful;
- most discussed;
- followed;
- official answer;
- nearby;
- language.

Popularity should not permanently bury newer useful questions.

================================================================================
38. REPUTATION
================================================================================

Useful discussion activity may affect reputation through:

- helpful replies;
- accepted answers;
- accurate information;
- respectful participation;
- confirmed reports;
- editor quality checks.

Negative signals may include:

- spam;
- harassment;
- manipulation;
- repeated misinformation;
- malicious reports;
- deleted abuse.

Reputation should use weighted and capped signals.

Raw post count should not create high reputation.

================================================================================
39. REWARDS
================================================================================

Possible rewards:

- Nexus Points;
- Embers;
- Inklings;
- badges;
- contribution progress.

Rewards should have:

- daily caps;
- anti-farming controls;
- quality requirement;
- reversal when abuse is confirmed.

A low-effort reply should not earn the same as an accepted useful answer.

================================================================================
40. AI ASSISTANCE
================================================================================

AI may help:

- suggest similar discussion;
- summarize a long thread;
- suggest title;
- detect spam;
- flag harassment;
- identify unsafe advice;
- suggest translation;
- prepare moderator summary;
- surface unanswered questions.

AI must not:

- post fake community replies;
- manipulate votes;
- impersonate a human;
- decide serious punishment alone;
- reveal private information;
- fabricate consensus.

Neural Agent participation should be visibly labeled and controlled.

================================================================================
41. NEURAL AGENT PARTICIPATION
================================================================================

A Neural Agent may:

- answer approved general questions;
- explain a Service;
- summarize a discussion;
- ask a discussion prompt;
- guide users to resources;
- participate in scheduled events.

A Neural Agent should not:

- pretend to have visited a spa;
- write a personal review;
- claim human credentials;
- disclose private user data;
- dominate community conversation;
- replace official moderation.

================================================================================
42. TRANSLATION
================================================================================

Possible translation display:

- original;
- translated;
- original and translation;
- translate on request.

The original remains preserved.

Translation should show:

- source language;
- method;
- review status;
- stale status where relevant.

A translated reply should not alter links, names, dates, or factual values.

================================================================================
43. MEDIA
================================================================================

Discussion media may include:

- image;
- short video;
- document;
- screenshot;
- link preview.

Validate:

- file type;
- size;
- ownership;
- privacy;
- people;
- location;
- sensitive content;
- copyright;
- AI use.

Private documents should not be uploaded into a public discussion.

================================================================================
44. LINK HANDLING
================================================================================

Links may be:

- allowed;
- previewed;
- blocked;
- nofollowed;
- held for review.

Possible risky links:

- malware;
- phishing;
- illegal marketplace;
- explicit solicitation;
- tracking;
- misleading redirect;
- personal data.

New accounts may have stricter link limits.

================================================================================
45. ANTI-SPAM
================================================================================

Controls may include:

- email verification;
- account age;
- rate limit;
- duplicate detection;
- link limit;
- CAPTCHA where justified;
- reputation;
- moderation queue;
- hidden honeypot;
- device and behavior signals;
- block list.

Anti-spam should not make ordinary participation unnecessarily difficult.

If CAPTCHA is used, it must follow Accessibility and Inclusive Design Requirements section 6:
prefer honeypot and invisible behavioral checks first, and never deploy an image-only or
audio-only challenge with no accessible alternative.

================================================================================
46. RATE LIMITS
================================================================================

Possible limits:

- new discussions per hour;
- replies per minute;
- mentions per post;
- links per post;
- reports per day;
- votes per minute;
- media uploads per day.

Limits may depend on account trust and moderation history.

================================================================================
47. PRIVACY
================================================================================

Do not allow users to post:

- private phone without permission;
- private address;
- identity document;
- booking code;
- private message screenshot without review;
- exact therapist home location;
- private health record;
- private credential document.

Moderators need tools to remove exposed private information quickly.

================================================================================

The Discussion System should support:

- keyboard navigation;

- logical reply order;
- accessible editor;
- visible focus;
- descriptive link text;
- alt text;
- captions;
- content warnings;
- understandable moderation notices;
- no color-only status;
- reduced motion.

================================================================================
53. CURRENT WORKING DECISIONS
================================================================================

- Discussions are separate from reviews, private messages, live chat, contributions, and reports.
- Posting normally requires a registered account.
- Official responses are permission-based.
- Public anonymity may be allowed only in selected cases.
- Edit history prevents misleading rewrites.
- Useful replies may affect reputation through weighted and capped signals.
- AI may assist moderation and summaries but may not fake community participation.
- Negative opinions are not automatically moderation violations.
- Sensitive discussions may receive stronger controls.
- The first version should remain readable and avoid excessive reaction types or deep reply nesting.

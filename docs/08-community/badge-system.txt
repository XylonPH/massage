================================================================================
1. BADGE MEANING AND BOUNDARY
================================================================================

Badges are visible proof icons, normally small square or one-by-one images, showing an achievement,
milestone, participation, approved role presentation, verification, event, season, or legacy status.

Badges are separate from:
- workspace roles and permissions, which require effective user_access;
- rank paths, which are fictional progression titles;
- Reputation, which records trust signals;
- Nexus Points, Embers, and Inklings, which are reward values;
- awards, which may use a separate nomination and decision process.

A badge never grants administrative, editorial, moderation, verification, establishment, or
practitioner authority by itself. Revoking user access must remove the authority even if a
historical or presentation badge remains visible under an approved legacy rule.

================================================================================
2. BADGE FAMILIES
================================================================================

Accepted working families:
- Achievement Badge;
- Contribution Badge;
- Review Badge;
- Rating Badge;
- Campus Badge;
- Event Badge;
- Seasonal Badge;
- Participation Badge;
- Role Badge;
- Verification Badge;
- Legacy Badge.

Each badge definition should identify its stable key, family, title, description, eligibility rule,
progress measure when applicable, public-display rule, artwork reference, lifecycle, and whether an
award may expire or be revoked.

Achievement and participation badges recognize completed actions. Role badges present an approved
current or historical responsibility. Verification badges identify exactly what was checked.
Legacy badges preserve recognition that is no longer earnable without misrepresenting current
authority.

================================================================================
3. VERIFICATION AND ROLE BADGES
================================================================================

A Verification Badge means a specific identity, claim, contact detail, credential, attendance,
role, or business relationship was checked. It does not mean the user has broader authority in all
areas.

A Role Badge may be shown only from an approved role or relationship record. Users cannot grant
themselves Founder, Editor, Moderator, Verified, Owner, Practitioner, or similar badge presentation
through public profile text.

The badge must distinguish:
- current and effective role presentation;
- expired or ended historical recognition;
- verified factual relationship;
- platform access permission.

These meanings must not be collapsed into one Verified badge.

================================================================================
4. ANCHORED MILESTONE SEQUENCE
================================================================================

Anchored Milestone Sequence, abbreviated AMS, is the working reusable milestone progression
sequence. It may be used for badges, achievements, contribution milestones, seasonal missions,
reward thresholds, progression ladders, activity goals, and gamified unlocks.

An AMS starts with one or more early-engagement anchors, then continues with a generalized
Fibonacci-style progression in which later values are created by adding the two previous milestone
values. The early anchors are selected for the action's expected frequency and effort; one sequence
is not forced onto every activity.

Criteria-rating milestone sequence:

1, 10, 20, 30, 50, 80, 130, 210, 340, 550...

Thumbs-up/down milestone sequence:

1, 10, 50, 100, 150, 250, 400, 650, 1050...

The first milestones encourage early participation. Later milestones become harder without an
abrupt gap. A badge family must identify the accepted anchors and eligibility filter; raw counts
alone do not determine eligibility when abuse, duplication, connection, or moderation rules apply.

================================================================================
5. RATING BADGES
================================================================================

Rating badges use AMS and count only eligible independent ratings under the Rating System.

Criteria-rating badge families:
- Rating Milestone;
- Spa Rating Milestone;
- Therapist Rating Milestone;
- Product Rating Milestone.

Examples:
- First Rating;
- 10 Ratings;
- First Spa Rating;
- 10 Spa Ratings;
- First Therapist Rating;
- 10 Therapist Ratings;
- First Product Rating;
- 10 Product Ratings.

Thumbs-up/down badge families:
- Article Rater;
- Review Rater;
- News Rater;
- Guide Rater;
- Comic Rater;
- Comment Rater;
- Discussion Rater;
- Media Rater;
- Translation Rater.

Self, connected, revoked, invalidated, quarantined, duplicate, and reward-farming ratings do not
count. The Rating System owns rating-event eligibility and abuse checks; this document owns badge
families and milestone values.

================================================================================
6. TRUSTED RATER
================================================================================

Trusted Rater is a special rating-alignment badge rather than a raw volume milestone.

Current direction:
- it applies mainly to spa and therapist criteria ratings;
- it requires at least 30 eligible spa or therapist criteria ratings;
- it requires sufficient overlap with editorial-rated targets;
- it measures alignment with editorial consensus, not community popularity alone;
- suspicious exact-copy or coordinated patterns are flagged rather than rewarded.

The Rating System owns the alignment formula, comparison eligibility, minimum target diversity,
reversal handling, and abuse controls.

7. REVIEW BADGES
================================================================================

Working review badges:
- First Review;
- First Establishment Review;
- First Practitioner Review;
- First Product Review;
- 10 Reviews;
- 25 Reviews;
- 50 Reviews;
- 100 Reviews;
- Detailed Reviewer;
- Helpful Reviewer;
- Photo Review Contributor;
- Transparent Reviewer;
- Multi-Visit Reviewer;
- Review Correspondent;
- Reviewer of the Year.

Volume milestones count only eligible published reviews. Quality badges require their own measured
criteria and may not be inferred only from word count. Review Correspondent is shown only from an
approved assignment; Reviewer of the Year is an award-style decision and requires a defined period,
eligibility, and audit trail.

================================================================================
8. RECEIVED-RATING BADGES AND AWARDS
================================================================================

Spas and practitioners may receive badge or award recognition based on mature ratings.

Working examples:
- Highly Rated Spa;
- Top Rated Spa;
- Rising Spa;
- Client Favorite;
- Top Rated Therapist;
- Rising Therapist;
- Trusted Therapist;
- Massage Nexus Award Nominee;
- Massage Nexus Award Winner.

Products do not require the same received-rating badge system yet.

Received-rating recognition requires the Rating System's approved minimum independent sample,
confidence, freshness, anti-manipulation, and connected-rating rules. A provisional high score or a
small connected group cannot earn an official badge. Quarterly and annual award workflows remain a
separate unresolved Awards specification.

================================================================================
9. DISPLAY AND ARTWORK
================================================================================

Users may choose which eligible badges to feature publicly, subject to a bounded display limit.
Hiding a badge does not erase its authoritative award record. Revoked or invalidated badges must not
remain presented as current.

Artwork requires:
- a consistent icon grid and safe area;
- readable small-size silhouettes;
- light and dark surface testing;
- non-color meaning where status matters;
- localized accessible name and description;
- alt text that explains the badge meaning rather than decorative detail;
- versioned source assets and derivative sizes.

Badge artwork style, rarity, complete icon templates, and final display-slot limits remain open.

================================================================================
10. SURVEY AND COMMUNITY PULSE BADGES
================================================================================

Survey participation uses the existing Participation Badge family and Anchored Milestone Sequence;
it does not create a separate badge engine.

Community Pulse answer milestones use the high-frequency sequence:
1, 10, 50, 100, 150, 250, 400, 650, 1050...

Working labels:
- First Community Pulse;
- 10 Community Pulse Answers;
- 50 Community Pulse Answers;
- later labels matching the approved milestone value.

Full-survey completion milestones begin:
1, 10, 25, 50, 100...

Working labels:
- First Survey;
- 10 Surveys;
- 25 Surveys;
- 50 Surveys;
- 100 Surveys.

Only a unique eligible answer or completion counts. Editing, retrying, translating, importing,
testing, duplicating, reopening, or invalidating and resubmitting the same run answer does not add
badge progress. Microsoft Forms, paper, anonymous, administrative, ineligible, and invalidated
responses do not count. Badge progress is independent of whether the daily points or Inkling reward
limit has already been reached.

================================================================================
11. DATA DIRECTION
================================================================================

Badge definitions and user awards must not be embedded as growing arrays in user_main. The future
data design should separate reusable badge definitions from user badge awards or progress. A public
profile may cache a bounded presentation projection, but the award record remains authoritative.

An award record should be able to preserve:
- user or supported target;
- badge definition;
- qualifying milestone or decision;
- source records or computed eligibility version;
- awarded time and actor or job;
- expiry, revocation, and reason;
- public-display choice;
- audit and recomputation information.

badge_main is the reusable definition collection. user_badge stores one user's award, progress,
featured-display choice, expiry, revocation, and source eligibility. A bounded projection in
user_main may cache badge count and up to six featured badge identifiers, but user_badge remains
authoritative.

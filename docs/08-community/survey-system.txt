================================================================================
1. PURPOSE AND BOUNDARY
================================================================================

Surveys and polls collect lightweight, structured user opinion to guide platform decisions and
make participation feel active. A poll is normally a single question with predefined choices; a
survey may contain several questions. Both are platform-authored: they are not user-generated
discussions, reviews, or ratings, and answering one is not a review or rating of any target.

Survey results are opinion signals, not official platform statistics. Published aggregates must
follow the Analytics Statistics and Measurement System rules (approved aggregates, minimum group
sizes, no exposure of individual answers).

================================================================================
2. PLACEMENT
================================================================================

The current placement is the homepage right sidebar (Community Pulse). The same widget or its
survey items may later appear on other approved pages. Placement does not change ownership: the
survey record and its results belong to the Survey System regardless of which page displays it.

================================================================================
3. PARTICIPATION AND COMPLETION ROTATION
================================================================================

Participation:
- registered users participate normally and their completion state is recorded per user;
- guests may be shown polls and may answer casually where allowed, but guest answers do not
  create official recorded results and guests may be invited to sign in;
- one user answers a given survey or poll item once; changing an answer, where allowed, updates
  the same recorded response rather than creating a second one.

Completion rotation (accepted direction):
- after a user completes an item, the widget briefly shows the result or acknowledgment;
- after a short delay, if the user is still on the page, a new unanswered item replaces the
  completed one in place;
- rotation is per user and completion-aware: any page showing the widget serves items the user
  has not yet answered, not items they already completed;
- when no unanswered items remain for the user, the widget may show recent results, a neutral
  message, or hide, rather than repeating completed items;
- completion-aware rotation requires an identified user; guests may receive simple time-based
  rotation instead.

Replacing the completed item in place is a dynamic update and must follow Accessibility and
Inclusive Design Requirements: the result and the arrival of a new item are announced to
assistive technology (aria-live), and keyboard focus is not lost or hijacked by the swap.

================================================================================
4. RESULTS, PRIVACY, AND REWARDS
================================================================================

- individual answers are private by default; public display uses aggregates only;
- results shown to the user immediately after answering are the same aggregate view, not other
  users' individual answers;
- sensitive topics require the same privacy, minimum-group, and retention rules as other
  measurement data;
- poll participation may earn small rewards per the Reward System,
  subject to its caps and anti-farming rules; answering repeatedly is prevented by the one-answer
  rule rather than by reward caps alone.

================================================================================
5. ADMINISTRATION AND STORAGE
================================================================================

Survey administration (draft, question and response structure, audience, availability window,
translation, accessibility, moderation, preview, publication, closure, export authorization,
archive) belongs to the Admin Workspace editorial area per docs/11-workspaces/admin-workspace.txt.

Final survey and response collections, field names, and option codes are deferred and will be
defined through the database-structure and structure-guide process before implementation. The
current homepage Community Pulse widget is a visual preview; submissions are not stored yet.

================================================================================
6. OPEN DECISIONS

- multi-question survey presentation (inline, dedicated page, or both);
- targeting rules (audience, locale, role) beyond simple availability windows;
- how survey insights feed the research and product-decision workflow.

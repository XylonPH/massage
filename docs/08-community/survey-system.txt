================================================================================
1. PURPOSE AND TERMINOLOGY
================================================================================

The Survey System gathers structured opinions, needs, satisfaction, preferences, and research
signals. It supports platform planning, service improvement, longitudinal comparison, and approved
public aggregate results. A response is evidence about the respondents and method used; it must not
be described as representative of a wider population without an appropriate sample and methodology.

Survey
An instrument containing one or more ordered questions, normally presented as a dedicated form.

Poll
A single-question Survey System instrument. A Discussion poll remains owned by the Discussion
System because it is user-authored discussion content, not platform research.

Community Pulse
The compact platform presentation that offers one eligible poll or one eligible question from a
larger survey. Community Pulse is not a separate survey database and does not change the question's
run, revision, audience, answer, privacy, reward, or result rules.

Survey revision
The exact immutable wording, translations, question order, option keys, and validation contract.

Survey run
One scheduled or conducted period of an exact revision. A later run can ask the same logical
questions again and creates a new response period without rewriting the earlier result.

================================================================================
2. INSTRUMENT AND QUESTION DESIGN
================================================================================

Administrators create one complete survey instrument containing individually managed questions.
Each question has a stable question_key. Choice options have stable option_key values. Published
revisions are immutable; wording, options, order, validation, or translation changes require a new
revision. Existing runs and answers retain the exact revision they used.

Supported question types:
- single choice;
- multiple choice with explicit minimum and maximum selections;
- numeric scale, normally 1 through 10, with clear endpoint labels;
- ranking;
- image choice with equivalent text and alt information;
- yes or no only when two answers are genuinely exhaustive;
- short text;
- long text;
- bounded matrix questions after accessible linear and mobile alternatives are verified.

A question can be marked Community Pulse eligible. Short text, long text, sensitive, context-heavy,
or otherwise unsuitable questions remain full-form only. Community Pulse questions should be quick,
neutral, understandable without surrounding questions, and safe to show on the selected public page.

Questionnaires must:
- state purpose, expected duration, audience, closing date, identity mode, result use, and reward;
- avoid leading, double-barrelled, loaded, needlessly invasive, or ambiguous wording;
- include Not applicable, Prefer not to answer, Not sure, or no forced answer where appropriate;
- avoid collecting contact or health information merely because a free-text box exists;
- identify sensitive questions and apply stronger access, retention, and export controls;
- preview all branches and required-answer behavior before publication.

================================================================================
3. AUDIENCE AND ELIGIBILITY
================================================================================

Initial and supported audiences are defined by target_survey_audience:
- general registered user;
- client, which is a baseline audience available to every member;
- completed-booking client;
- practitioner;
- establishment operator;
- seller;
- Campus learner;
- Campus instructor;
- Campus administrator;
- contributor;
- editor;
- translator;
- advertiser.

Audience eligibility is derived from authoritative account, user_access, practitioner, booking,
seller, enrollment, and assignment relationships. It is not selected through a user-controlled
role switch. One user may qualify for several audiences and receives the combined eligible queue
without duplicate presentation of the same run question.

A run can target one or more audiences. The instrument must not present an operator, practitioner,
seller, instructor, or administrator question to a user whose authoritative relationships do not
establish that audience. Targeting rules are evaluated again when a run is served, not copied into
user_main as a permanent role claim.

================================================================================
4. PARTICIPATION, GUESTS, REPEAT RUNS, AND EDITING
================================================================================

Community Pulse may be visible to guests so public pages remain understandable and interactive.
A guest may select an option locally to preview the interaction, but submission requires login or
registration. No guest answer is sent to the server, counted, rewarded, stored in an aggregate, or
later attached automatically to an account. An unsubmitted selection may exist only in the current
browser tab and must not be treated as research data.

For registered participation:
- one survey_response exists per run and respondent_key;
- one current survey_answer exists per response and question_key;
- completing a question through Community Pulse saves it in the same response used by the full form;
- the full form shows that question as answered and prefills the saved answer;
- the respondent may review and edit while the run permits edits;
- an edit updates the same current answer and does not create another response, reward, badge count,
  vote, or aggregate participant;
- a question answered in an older run does not count as answered in a new run;
- closing or withdrawing a run stops participation according to the recorded lifecycle decision.

Community Pulse answers are research records. They do not automatically modify user_main booking
preferences or other account settings, and Community Pulse does not offer an optional action to do
so. A future preference-learning feature requires its own explicit purpose, consent, review, and
account-setting workflow.

================================================================================
5. COMMUNITY PULSE PRESENTATION AND ROTATION
================================================================================

Approved right-sidebar placements include the homepage and selected Article, Review, News, Guide,
Discussion, establishment, practitioner, service, and product pages. A page may omit the widget when
the layout, audience, sensitivity, fatigue rule, or eligible-question supply makes it inappropriate.

Serving sequence:
1. find open runs matching the user's authoritative audiences, language, time, and placement;
2. obtain Community Pulse-eligible questions from their immutable revisions;
3. exclude answers already present for that user and run;
4. apply campaign priority, fatigue, cooldown, sensitivity, and page-context rules;
5. return one eligible question.

The implementation must query eligible runs first and then perform an indexed answer lookup. It must
not scan every survey question or the user's full answer history on every page. A short-lived,
invalidatable per-user eligible-question queue may be cached, but stored responses remain authoritative.

After authenticated submission:
- acknowledge the saved selection;
- show the aggregate only when public results are enabled, no embargo applies, and the minimum
  response threshold is met;
- otherwise state that the answer was recorded and results will appear after enough responses;
- keep the result visible long enough to read;
- after approximately one minute, replace it in the same widget with the next eligible unanswered
  question when the user remains on the page;
- offer a clear Next action when useful;
- do not steal keyboard focus or make the result disappear while it is focused or being read;
- announce the status and later replacement through an appropriate accessible live region.

When no eligible unanswered question remains, hide Community Pulse or use the space for another
approved sidebar module. Do not repeat a completed question merely to keep the widget populated.

================================================================================
6. FULL SURVEY WORKSPACE EXPERIENCE
================================================================================

The User Workspace Survey area contains:
- Available;
- In Progress;
- Completed;
- Closed;
- Withdrawn.

Each invitation or record shows purpose, audience, estimated duration, closing date, progress,
identity mode, reward if any, answer-edit policy, and whether approved aggregates will be published.
High-priority research may be pinned in the workspace. Ordinary invitations use notifications and
must not become repeated blocking prompts.

One active broad full-survey invitation per audience is the normal maximum. A campaign may send an
initial invitation, one midpoint reminder, and one final reminder. Reminders stop after completion,
withdrawal, opt-out where applicable, run closure, or loss of eligibility. Notification delivery
uses the Notification System rather than a separate survey-invitation collection.

================================================================================
7. RESULTS, PRIVACY, AND ANALYTICS
================================================================================

Raw answers are private. Public display uses approved de-identified aggregates only. Community Pulse
defaults to a minimum of 20 eligible registered responses before showing results. A run may require a
higher threshold because of sensitivity, cohort risk, or methodology; it may not silently lower the
threshold below the Analytics System's approved minimum.

The threshold is necessary but not sufficient. Results remain suppressed when:
- the run is embargoed or results are not public;
- a subgroup can expose or strongly imply an individual answer;
- eligibility, duplicate, fraud, import, or methodology review is incomplete;
- the question is too sensitive for publication;
- the run or result has been withdrawn.

Result views may include:
- bar charts for controlled options;
- distributions and averages for numeric scales;
- ranking summaries;
- response and completion counts;
- completion funnels;
- trends across comparable runs;
- cohort comparisons only above the approved privacy threshold.

Charts must provide a text/table equivalent, readable labels, logical order, sufficient contrast,
non-color distinctions, keyboard access, and localized number formatting. Do not publish individual
free-text answers as an aggregate result. Qualitative responses require a separate approved research
or editorial analysis with redaction and quotation permission.

Exports may support CSV, XLSX, JSON, approved PDF summaries, and chart images. Aggregate export and
raw-response export are separate permissions. Raw exports require an explicit purpose, audit entry,
minimum necessary fields, protected delivery, and retention handling. Public exports contain no
respondent identifier or small disclosive cohort.

================================================================================
8. REWARDS AND BADGES
================================================================================

Routine Community Pulse reward:
- 1 Nexus Point;
- 5 Inklings;
- 0 Embers;
- only the first three otherwise eligible Community Pulse answers per user per UTC reward-ledger day.

The user may continue answering after the daily reward limit. The first eligible answer to the exact
run question creates the idempotent reward fact. Edits, retries, translations, imported responses,
anonymous responses, test responses, duplicates, invalidated responses, and reopened answers do not
earn another reward.

A full survey may grant one completion reward scaled to verified burden under a versioned campaign
rule. Questions already rewarded through Community Pulse must not be rewarded again through the
full-survey completion calculation. Routine surveys award no Embers. A rare, explicitly budgeted
research campaign may propose an Ember award only after Reward System and monetization approval.

Participation Badge milestones:
- Community Pulse answers use 1, 10, 50, 100, 150, 250, 400, 650, 1050 and continue through the
  approved high-frequency Anchored Milestone Sequence;
- full-survey completions use 1, 10, 25, 50, 100 before later reviewed milestones.

Working labels are First Community Pulse, 10 Community Pulse Answers, and corresponding later
milestones; and First Survey, 10 Surveys, 25 Surveys, 50 Surveys, and 100 Surveys. Only unique eligible
answers or completions count. Badge progress is separate from reward amounts.

================================================================================
9. ADMIN WORKSPACE
================================================================================

Survey administration belongs in /workspace/editorial/survey with results and approved database
operations available through permission-scoped linked views. Authorized functions include:
- create a stable survey or poll;
- edit an unpublished draft revision;
- add, edit, order, duplicate, and remove draft questions and options;
- preview desktop, mobile, Community Pulse, branch, required-answer, and result states;
- request, edit, review, approve, and mark stale translations;
- select audience, placement, identity mode, answer editing, threshold, embargo, and reward rule;
- create, schedule, open, close, withdraw, archive, and repeat runs;
- monitor invitations, response counts, completion, drop-off, import quality, and suppression;
- aggregate or rebuild results;
- export authorized aggregate or raw datasets;
- create and validate Microsoft Forms, paper, and administrative import batches;
- audit publication, withdrawal, export, import, reward, and high-risk actions.

Published revisions cannot be edited in place. Create a new revision instead. A draft with no run or
response reference may be hard-deleted by an authorized user after confirmation. A published survey,
run, revision, response, or import batch is archived, closed, withdrawn, or invalidated as appropriate;
it is not hard-deleted through ordinary administration. A response-bearing run must preserve
referential integrity and the recorded methodology.

Publication readiness blocks release when mandatory purpose, audience, question validation,
translation, accessibility, privacy, result, scheduling, or reward information is incomplete.

================================================================================
10. EXTERNAL MICROSOFT FORMS AND PAPER FIELDWORK
================================================================================

Prelaunch and off-platform research may use Microsoft Forms or printed questionnaires. Record that
work in Massage Nexus when it is part of the project's research history, even when the website was
not live at collection time.

Required preservation:
- stable survey_key, survey_revision_number, question_key, and option_key mapping;
- exact questionnaire wording and version used;
- intended audience, recruitment method, dates, locations where relevant, and consent wording;
- protected original Microsoft Forms export or paper source package;
- file checksum and controlled import batch;
- source_survey_response for each imported cohort;
- validation, rejected-row, duplicate, skipped, unreadable, corrected, and import notes;
- native, Microsoft Forms, paper, and administrative cohorts separable in analysis.

Do not attach anonymous or externally collected answers to a future Massage Nexus account without
specific informed permission and a lawful matching process. External anonymous respondents receive
no retroactive points, Inklings, Embers, or badges.

Paper forms use a survey/run identifier and non-account response serial. Data entry preserves the
original form, distinguishes blank, unreadable, corrected, and not-applicable answers, and uses
double entry or risk-based second-person verification. Destroying or retaining paper originals
follows the approved research retention schedule; entering the data is not permission to discard
the source immediately.

The practical setup and questionnaire-copy workflow is defined in
docs/14-research/survey-fieldwork-and-microsoft-forms-guide.txt.

================================================================================
11. CADENCE AND FATIGUE
================================================================================

Initial operating cadence:
- Community Pulse operates continuously when eligible inventory exists; add approximately two to
  four reviewed questions each month rather than replacing the entire bank;
- general registered-user survey: quarterly;
- practitioner, establishment operator, seller, contributor, Campus learner, Campus instructor,
  and Campus administrator broad surveys: semiannual initially;
- course or program feedback: event-triggered after completion;
- booking or Support satisfaction: event-triggered with eligibility and fatigue sampling;
- strategic research and market-direction survey: annual;
- prelaunch questionnaire: one conducted research run, repeated only when the instrument or decision
  need materially changes.

Cadence is a maximum planning rhythm, not a requirement to send a survey without a real decision
purpose. An audience should not receive another broad invitation while its current broad survey is
open. Community Pulse and event-triggered prompts apply cooldowns so engagement does not become
harassment or reward farming.

================================================================================
12. MULTILINGUAL RULES
================================================================================

Native surveys and Community Pulse are multilingual structured content. English is the required
source and fallback for the initial question bank. Every published native revision must contain
approved translations for the locales required by its release scope. Question and option keys remain
language-neutral and stable. Translation status is tied to the exact revision; changing source wording
makes affected translations stale and requires a new survey revision before future runs use it.

The initial Microsoft Forms and printed prelaunch packs are English-only by current decision. Their
English-only scope must be stated in methodology and must not be misrepresented as a multilingual
sample. If a translated external form is later created, preserve it as a separately versioned source
and map each language version to the same stable question and option keys where meanings are equivalent.

================================================================================
13. COLLECTIONS AND QUERY BOUNDARIES
================================================================================

The accepted collection direction is:
- survey_main: stable instrument identity and current-revision pointer;
- survey_revision: exact immutable multilingual title, description, questions, options, constraints,
  and review state;
- survey_run: one audience, placement, timing, identity, result, reward, and lifecycle configuration;
- survey_response: one respondent/run envelope, deduplication key, progress, source, and completion;
- survey_answer: one current answer per response and question;
- survey_aggregate: rebuildable question/run/cohort counts, charts, and suppression state;
- survey_import_batch: Microsoft Forms, paper, or controlled administrative import provenance and mapping.

Notifications and reminders reuse the Notification System. Reward events use user_reward. Badge
definitions and progress use badge_main and user_badge. Survey answer history and administrative
actions reuse the approved revision and audit systems where required. user_main may show bounded
survey statistics in a rebuildable activity summary, but it does not own response or answer arrays.

================================================================================
14. IMPLEMENTATION ACCEPTANCE
================================================================================

The implementation is acceptable when:
- a published revision cannot be changed in place;
- the same survey can run again in a later period without colliding with earlier completion;
- audience eligibility is authorization-derived and efficiently queried;
- a guest cannot create an official answer, result, reward, or completion record;
- Community Pulse and the full form share one current run answer per question;
- edits do not duplicate responses, rewards, badge progress, or aggregate participants;
- result threshold, embargo, suppression, and raw-export permissions are server-enforced;
- multilingual fallback and stale-translation behavior follow the Translation System;
- Microsoft Forms and paper imports preserve source, version, mapping, cohort, and audit;
- keyboard, screen-reader, reduced-motion, mobile, error, empty, loading, and replacement states pass
  accessibility review;
- reward, aggregate, and progress projections can be rebuilt from authoritative records.

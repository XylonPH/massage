DOCUMENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
This document defines analytics, statistics, metrics, events, reports, public aggregates,
research coverage, social-presence observations, privacy controls, and measurement governance
for Massage Nexus.

Analytics:
Measurements used to understand behavior, performance, operations, quality, and outcomes.

Statistics:
Aggregated descriptive results about the platform, directory, market, research coverage,
content, providers, services, and communities.

A number is not meaningful unless its definition, scope, timeframe, source, and update date
are known.

================================================================================
1. GOALS
================================================================================

The system should:
- help users understand their private activity;
- help providers understand authorized performance;
- help editors and researchers identify data gaps;
- help administrators operate the platform;
- support transparent public statistics;
- measure translation and market readiness;
- measure social and external presence;
- support advertising only with clear separation and consent;
- protect sensitive and private information;
- avoid misleading rankings;
- preserve metric definitions and revisions.

3. METRIC REGISTRY
================================================================================

Every metric should have:
- metric key;
- label;
- definition;
- purpose;
- owner;
- source event or record;
- numerator;
- denominator;
- unit;
- scope;
- dimensions;
- timeframe;
- time zone;
- inclusion rules;
- exclusion rules;
- privacy class;
- aggregation;
- minimum group threshold;
- update frequency;
- version;
- status;
- caveat;
- example.

Do not allow several teams to use the same label for different calculations.

Example:

metric:
provider.profile_view_unique

Definition:
Distinct authorized browser or account views of one public provider profile during the
selected period, after bot and duplicate-session filtering.

It is different from:
- raw page requests;
- search impressions;
- profile tab changes;
- staff preview;
- crawler access.

5. TIME, DATE, AND GEOGRAPHY
================================================================================

Reports must identify:
- event time;
- reporting time zone;
- local date if relevant;
- UTC storage where appropriate;
- period start and end;
- data freshness;
- partial period.

Geographic dimensions may include:
- country;
- region;
- province or state;
- city;
- district;
- tourism area.

Do not expose precise user home location in ordinary analytics.

Provider analytics may use the provider's public location or service area, not the private
residence of a client.

10. SOCIAL MEDIA AND EXTERNAL PRESENCE
================================================================================

Follower counts are observations, not permanent facts.

A social observation may contain:
- platform;
- account handle;
- account URL;
- target record;
- account type;
- official status;
- platform verification status;
- Massage Nexus verification status;
- follower count;
- following count;
- post count where available;
- engagement observations;
- observation date;
- source;
- capture method;
- notes;
- confidence.

Account types:
- establishment;
- branch;
- brand;
- therapist;
- creator;
- hotel or resort group;
- professional association;
- school;
- seller.

Do not store one universal social_followers field.

A ranking must specify platform, account type, geography, timeframe, metric, minimum
eligibility, last checked, and suspicious-follower handling.

Acceptable:
"Most followed on Instagram among tracked Philippine massage establishments as of July 2026."

Not acceptable:
"World's most popular spa."

Follower count is separate from engagement, business size, booking volume, rating,
professional quality, massage-related content percentage, awards, and geographic reach.

11. REFERRAL AND AI SEARCH ANALYTICS
================================================================================

Track approved referral sources:
- search engines;
- social sites;
- partner sites;
- ChatGPT and other AI search referrals when identifiable;
- newsletters;
- campaigns;
- direct;
- unknown.

Possible fields:
- referrer category;
- source;
- medium;
- campaign;
- landing page;
- language;
- country at approved granularity;
- conversion;
- bot classification.

Do not fingerprint users aggressively merely to improve attribution.

A crawler may create page requests without producing a human referral. Separate:
- bot crawl;
- search impression when available;
- referral visit;
- authenticated conversion.

================================================================================
14. RANKINGS
================================================================================

Possible rankings:
- most reviewed;
- highest rated with minimum review threshold;
- most saved;
- most followed;
- trending;
- most recently verified;
- strongest data completeness;
- most helpful contributor;
- most followed on a named social platform.

Every ranking needs:
- metric definition;
- geography;
- timeframe;
- eligibility;
- minimum count;
- anti-manipulation;
- tie handling;
- sponsored exclusion;
- update date.

Spa Market Class, Therapist Market Class, and Product Grade provide context but must not
automatically raise or lower a rating.

No unexplained "best in the world" label.

================================================================================
15. PRIVACY
================================================================================

Do not put in ordinary analytics:
- password;
- password hash;
- recovery token;
- session token;
- private message body;
- sealed intake;
- precise residential location;
- full payment card;
- private identity evidence;
- cross-provider client history;
- NSFW preference in public or advertiser targeting;
- sensitive health inference.

Use aggregation, pseudonymous identifiers where needed, minimum thresholds, retention limits,
role access, export controls, audit, and privacy review.

Analytics should not become a hidden second user profile.

================================================================================
16. CONSENT AND COOKIES
================================================================================

Determine which measurement is:
- essential operations;
- security;
- user-requested personal report;
- legitimate operational analytics where permitted;
- optional analytics;
- advertising.

Do not present essential security logging as optional analytics.

Optional analytics and advertising controls must follow the final cookie and privacy policy.

================================================================================
17. BOTS, SPAM, AND DATA QUALITY
================================================================================

Filter or label:
- search crawlers;
- AI crawlers;
- monitoring;
- uptime checks;
- staff previews;
- automated tests;
- duplicate events;
- spam accounts;
- suspicious follows;
- fraudulent reviews;
- referral spam.

Keep raw and cleaned counts distinct when useful.

Do not silently change historical published numbers without a correction note when the change
is material.

================================================================================
23. ACCEPTED DIRECTIONS
================================================================================

- Analytics and public statistics are separate presentations of governed measurements.
- Every metric has a definition, scope, timeframe, and version.
- Personal analytics are private.
- Provider analytics are permission-bound.
- Intake is excluded from ordinary analytics.
- Research coverage and market reality are not the same.
- Follower counts are dated platform-specific observations.
- Social popularity does not equal professional quality.
- Rankings require transparent eligibility and method.
- AI and search referrals may be measured without exposing private browsing behavior.
- Public statistics use aggregation and privacy thresholds.
- Detailed booking and finance metrics remain governed by their own systems.

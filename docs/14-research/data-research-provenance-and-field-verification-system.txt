DOCUMENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
This document defines how Massage Nexus discovers, researches, sources, verifies, updates,
disputes, and publishes data about spas, massage establishments, therapists, services,
products, sellers, locations, social accounts, and related records.

Massage Nexus should become more useful than a copied map directory by preserving:
- field-level sources;
- direct observations;
- interviews;
- current menus and prices;
- missing businesses;
- former names and locations;
- confidence;
- contradictions;
- last checked and last verified dates.

A map listing is a lead, not the authority.

================================================================================
1. RESEARCH GOALS
================================================================================

- find establishments and therapists missing from major maps;
- identify closed, moved, renamed, or reopened businesses;
- preserve current and historical evidence;
- document exact services, prices, facilities, and policies;
- support local on-site research;
- support trusted remote contributors;
- distinguish observed, reported, and verified facts;
- create transparent freshness;
- support global expansion by city and market;
- avoid illegal or unethical scraping;
- avoid exposing private residential information;
- create research tasks and quality analytics.

================================================================================
2. RESEARCH UNIT
================================================================================

Do not treat an entire country as one research task.

Use:

Country
-> priority metropolitan area or tourism region
-> city or municipality
-> district, neighborhood, landmark, or locality
-> establishment, therapist, service, or other target
-> field-level observations.

A useful, current Bangkok or Mandaluyong dataset is better than thousands of shallow,
unverified national records.

================================================================================
6. MAPS ARE LEADS
================================================================================

Google Maps and other map services are useful for discovering possible businesses, locating
approximate addresses, finding photos, public reviews, phone numbers, websites, and nearby
businesses.

They are not sufficient proof of:
- current operation;
- exact name;
- current hours;
- current price;
- service availability;
- branch relationship;
- therapist affiliation;
- exact map point;
- legitimacy;
- closure;
- nonexistence.

Absence from a map does not prove that a spa does not exist.

Presence on a map does not prove that it remains open.

Massage Nexus should preserve its own evidence and freshness.

Example:

Field:

Source:

Observed:

This permits one profile to contain hours verified directly, price photographed on-site,

================================================================================
9. CONFIDENCE
================================================================================

Possible confidence:
- unknown;
- low;
- medium;
- high;
- direct.

Confidence is based on evidence, freshness, source independence, and contradiction.

It is not based only on contributor reputation, number of copied websites, AI confidence, map
popularity, or social follower count.

Several websites may all copy the same outdated source.

================================================================================
10. FRESHNESS
================================================================================

Different fields age at different rates.

Often fast-changing:
- hours;
- phone;
- price;
- therapist roster;
- promotion;
- social account;
- booking availability.

Moderate:
- facilities;
- services;
- management;
- branch relationship.

Slow:
- origin;
- building location;
- historical name;
- normalized service identity.

Each field may have:
- observed_at;
- verified_at;
- review_due_at;
- stale_at.

The interface may display:
- last directly verified;
- last provider confirmed;
- last community checked;
- price last checked;
- hours last checked.

Do not use one profile-level "updated" date to imply every field is current.

================================================================================
11. CONTRADICTIONS
================================================================================

When sources conflict:
- preserve both observations;
- identify dates;
- compare source authority;
- contact provider if appropriate;
- request local verification;
- avoid silent overwrite;
- publish the safest qualified statement;
- mark disputed or uncertain;
- retain resolution history.

Example:
Official page says open 24 hours.
Recent direct visit finds the door closed at 2:00 a.m.
The record should preserve both and request verification rather than choose automatically.

12. DIRECT VISIT AND INTERVIEW
================================================================================

Field research may collect:
- current name;
- former name;
- exact location;
- landmark;
- hours;
- contacts;
- menu;
- price;
- facilities;
- room setup;
- accessibility;
- therapist count;
- accepted payment;
- booking;
- service policies;
- photos;
- interview notes;
- permission;
- visit date.

Researchers must:
- identify themselves honestly when required;
- respect no-photography rules;
- obtain permission for identifiable staff interviews and private interiors;
- avoid interrupting treatment;
- avoid covert recording;
- protect client privacy;
- distinguish observation from opinion;
- retain original field notes;
- label uncertain facts.

A review visit and a factual research visit may overlap, but their evidence and editorial
purposes remain distinct.

13. REMOTE RESEARCH
================================================================================

Remote research may use:
- official website;
- official social accounts;
- direct message;
- telephone;
- email;
- local contact;
- public registry;
- tourism source;
- booking page;
- map;
- archived page;
- public menu.

Remote research should:
- record access date;
- capture source title and relevant evidence;
- avoid copying copyrighted material unnecessarily;
- use facts rather than republish entire descriptions;
- respect terms and access controls;
- avoid high-volume prohibited scraping;
- identify machine translation;
- preserve original language.

================================================================================
14. SOCIAL MEDIA RESEARCH
================================================================================

Track social accounts separately from profiles.

Observation:
- platform;
- handle;
- URL;
- account type;
- official status;
- platform verification;
- Massage Nexus verification;
- follower count;
- following count;
- posts where available;
- massage-related content proportion when assessed;
- engagement observation;
- observed date;
- source;
- notes.

Follower counts change. Never store them as timeless facts.

Avoid:
"world's most popular therapist."

Prefer:
"Most followed on Instagram among tracked licensed massage therapists as of the observation
date."

The platform should not infer professional quality from followers alone.

================================================================================
15. THERAPIST RESEARCH
================================================================================

Therapist research requires stronger identity care.

Possible evidence:
- direct claim;
- establishment affiliation;
- professional page;
- credential;
- training;
- public interview;
- official schedule;
- competition or award;
- public social account.

Do not:
- create a public therapist identity from a private personal profile without a justified
  public-interest and privacy basis;
- expose residence;
- expose private phone;
- merge people only because names match;
- claim a credential without evidence;
- treat a spa roster photo as permanent employment.

Stable therapist identity must survive employment changes without creating duplicates.

================================================================================
16. PROVIDER CLAIM AND CORRECTION
================================================================================

A provider may:
- claim;
- verify contact;
- submit documents;
- correct facts;
- dispute;
- provide current menu;
- provide official media;
- identify former names;
- confirm closure or move.

Claim does not automatically erase community evidence.

Provider-supplied claims remain sourced and may require verification.

A provider cannot secretly remove a lawful critical review or historical fact merely by
claiming the profile.

================================================================================
17. USER AND CORRESPONDENT CONTRIBUTION
================================================================================

Research tasks may ask:
- Is this spa open?
- Photograph current menu with permission.
- Confirm hours.
- Verify map pin.
- Add missing establishment.
- Identify former name.
- Confirm wheelchair access.
- Confirm accepted payment.
- Confirm social account.
- Confirm whether therapist still works there.

Roles:
- ordinary contributor;
- trusted contributor;
- correspondent;
- translator;
- editor;
- verifier;
- moderator;
- administrator.

Rewards:
- only for approved useful work;
- no raw volume reward;
- no self-approval;
- conflict-of-interest disclosure;
- reversal for fraud or duplicate work.

================================================================================
18. AI RESEARCH
================================================================================

AI may:
- discover leads;
- summarize sources;
- extract candidate facts;
- compare sources;
- translate;
- suggest duplicates;
- flag stale records;
- prepare assignments.

AI may not:
- mark a business active without evidence;
- invent an interview;
- invent an on-site visit;
- fabricate a menu, price, address, therapist, or source;
- bypass blocked access;
- scrape prohibited content;
- expose private location;
- publish a serious allegation automatically.

Every AI-extracted fact remains tied to the source and review state.

================================================================================
19. CRAWLERS AND DATA ACCESS
================================================================================

Massage Nexus prefers:
- public discovery;
- citation;
- referral;
- controlled access;
- licensed APIs;
- protection from unrestricted bulk reuse.

Possible production direction:
- permit approved search crawlers on public pages;
- permit approved AI search crawlers when citation and referrals are useful;
- block training crawlers where separate supported controls exist and the final policy opts
  out;
- block workspace, account, message, booking, intake, moderation, research evidence, and
  restricted media;
- use noindex where necessary;
- rate-limit abusive automation;
- provide an approved API later.

Robots directives are not security. Malicious scrapers may ignore them.

Do not expose complete high-value datasets solely for crawling. Public pages may provide
useful summaries while full comparisons, live filters, verification history, booking,
community, and contributor tools remain on Massage Nexus.

================================================================================
20. COPYRIGHT, TERMS, AND ETHICS
================================================================================

Researchers must:
- collect facts lawfully;
- avoid copying entire copyrighted descriptions;
- quote sparingly and attribute when appropriate;
- respect website terms;
- respect robots and rate limits;
- use APIs when required;
- avoid bypassing authentication or paywalls;
- avoid collecting unnecessary personal data;
- protect minors;
- protect private homes and contact details;
- avoid harassment;
- correct errors;
- identify sponsorship or conflict.

Publicly available does not automatically mean unrestricted for every use.

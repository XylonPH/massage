The Therapist Workspace is part of /workspace and uses the same account, permission, revision, translation, and security systems as the rest of Massage Nexus.

================================================================================
1. PRIMARY GOALS
================================================================================

The Therapist Workspace should:

- let a therapist manage approved professional information;
- preserve separation between private identity and public professional identity;
- separate capability from direct commercial offerings;
- support several establishment affiliations;
- support independent practice;
- support schedule and availability;
- protect home addresses and private contact details;
- support credential submission and verification;
- support secure media controls;
- support review responses;
- support multilingual profile content;
- support future booking and mobile use;
- prevent an establishment from owning or silently rewriting a therapist's universal identity.

================================================================================
2. POSSIBLE USERS
================================================================================

- spa-based therapist;
- freelance therapist;
- independent practitioner;
- home-service therapist;
- mobile therapist;
- trainee;
- visiting expert;
- practitioner represented through several establishments;
- therapist assistant with delegated permission;
- agency or manager with limited delegated permission.

The public module may use Therapist as the familiar label while internal models may use Practitioner where broader coverage is needed.

================================================================================
6. PRIVATE IDENTITY
================================================================================

Private identity may include:

- legal name;
- birth date where required;
- verified email;
- verified phone;
- private address;
- identification documents;
- emergency contact;
- payment identity;
- tax information.

Private identity is not automatically visible to:

- public users;
- establishments;
- advertisers;
- other therapists.

Access is limited by purpose and permission.

================================================================================
7. CLAIMING THE PROFILE
================================================================================

Possible claim process:

1. therapist identifies the public profile;
2. therapist verifies account contact;
3. therapist submits proof;
4. verifier reviews the claim;
5. conflicts are resolved;
6. selected management permissions are granted;
7. claim status remains recorded.

Claiming the profile does not allow removal of independent reviews or historical affiliations.

================================================================================

Capability means the therapist can perform a normalized Service.

- training;
- experience;

- active status;

- direct commercial offering;

- credential;

================================================================================
20. REVIEWS AND OFFICIAL RESPONSES
================================================================================

Possible route:

/workspace/listing/therapist/{therapist-id}/response

The therapist may:

- view reviews;
- filter by establishment or Service;
- draft a response;
- publish an official response;
- report abuse;
- request factual correction;
- view response history.

The therapist may not:

- edit review text;
- alter ratings;
- discover unauthorized reviewer identity;
- threaten or pressure reviewers;
- remove a valid negative review.

================================================================================
21. BOOKINGS

Possible route:

/workspace/listing/therapist/{therapist-id}/booking

- accept;

- suggest time;

- see approved client notes;
- confirm arrival;

================================================================================
36. CURRENT WORKING DECISIONS
================================================================================

- Therapist Workspace is part of /workspace.
- Public professional identity and private legal identity remain separate.
- Capability is separate from direct commercial offering.
- Establishment affiliation is contextual and may be confirmed or disputed.
- Provider rank is not a universal credential or quality score.
- The therapist controls approved professional information.
- Establishments control their own schedules and menu eligibility, not the therapist's universal identity.
- Restricted media uses server-side authorization.
- One therapist may have several professional contexts.
- Booking and sensitive client notes are later-stage systems.

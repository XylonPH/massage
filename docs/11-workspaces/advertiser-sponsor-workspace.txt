DOCUMENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
This document defines the working private workspace for advertisers and sponsors using Massage Nexus.

It covers advertiser identity, sponsor identity, campaigns, placements, sponsored listings, sponsored content, creative media, targeting, budget, billing, approval, disclosure, analytics, policy, and support.

Advertising and sponsorship must remain visibly separate from organic ratings, verification, reputation, moderation, and editorial conclusions.

================================================================================
1. PRIMARY GOALS
================================================================================

The workspace should:

- let approved organizations create and manage advertising;
- support sponsors of articles, comics, rooms, events, campaigns, downloads, and learning material;
- show clear approval and publication status;
- provide budget and invoice information;
- provide useful analytics;
- require truthful disclosure;
- protect user privacy;
- prevent advertisers from influencing organic trust systems;
- support small local businesses and larger organizations;
- allow administrative review and policy enforcement.

================================================================================
2. PARTICIPANT TYPES
================================================================================

Advertiser
Pays for placement, promotion, visibility, or a campaign.

Sponsor
Provides money, products, services, venue, prizes, or another approved benefit supporting content, an event, a series, or a platform activity.

Provider Advertiser
A spa, therapist, seller, or product owner promoting their own approved record.

Agency
Manages advertising for one or more clients.

Brand
An organization promoting products, services, or awareness.

Institution
A school, association, clinic, hotel, resort, or another organization sponsoring relevant content or activity.

Platform Administrator
Reviews advertising, sponsorship, billing, and compliance.

One organization may be both advertiser and sponsor.

21. POSSIBLE PRICING METHODS
================================================================================

Possible methods:

Flat Fee
Fixed price for a placement or period.

Cost Per Thousand Impressions
Charge based on impressions.

Cost Per Click
Charge based on valid clicks.

Cost Per Action
Charge based on an approved action.

Sponsorship Package
Fixed package of deliverables.

Subscription
Recurring plan with included benefits.

The first implementation should preferably use simple manually approved flat-fee packages before advanced automated bidding.

23. APPROVAL WORKFLOW
================================================================================

Possible process:

1. organization creates draft;
2. organization reviews and submits;
3. automated validation checks format and prohibited content;
4. advertising administrator reviews;
5. evidence is requested if needed;
6. campaign is approved, revised, or rejected;
7. payment requirement is satisfied;
8. campaign is scheduled;
9. platform monitors delivery and policy;
10. campaign completes or is suspended.

Urgent same-day placement should not bypass safety review.

25. EDITORIAL INDEPENDENCE
================================================================================

Advertisers and sponsors cannot:

- change independent reviews;
- remove valid criticism;
- alter ratings;
- grant verification;
- control moderation;
- suppress required warnings;
- direct organic ranking secretly;
- demand a positive editorial conclusion.

A sponsor relationship should be recorded and disclosed.

27. PRIVACY
================================================================================

Advertisers should receive aggregated or appropriately limited analytics.

They should not receive:

- private user identity;
- exact private health information;
- private messages;
- unrelated booking details;
- private review drafts;
- private location history.

Tracking must follow policy and applicable law.

28. INVALID TRAFFIC AND FRAUD
================================================================================

- repeated automated click;
- self-clicking;
- bot impression;
- duplicate action;
- suspicious referral;
- manipulated conversion.

Invalid activity should not be billed as ordinary valid performance where the pricing model depends on it.

================================================================================
37. CURRENT WORKING DECISIONS
================================================================================

- Advertising and sponsorship use one permission-aware workspace area.
- Sponsorship is distinct from ordinary advertising but may share organization and billing tools.
- Sponsored content must be disclosed.
- Advertising does not alter organic ratings, verification, moderation, or editorial conclusions.
- Private user data is not provided to advertisers.
- The first implementation should use simpler manually approved packages.
- Creative and claims require review.
- Organization members receive limited permissions.
- AI may assist creative work but does not approve itself.

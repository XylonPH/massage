Title: Massage Nexus User Workspace
Document Version: 0.74
Date Created: 2026-07-16
Revision Date: 2026-07-21
Document Description:
This document defines the general authenticated User Workspace for Massage Nexus and the
relationship between the shared network account, Massage Nexus membership, additive personal,
professional, operational, and administrative capabilities, language, security, research, translation, analytics, and
content-access preferences. It also preserves the consolidated Client Service History,
analytics, budgeting, data-portability, import, export, and therapist-identity-portability
direction.

Consolidation Note:
This document incorporates and supersedes:
- Massage Nexus - 35 - Client Service History Analytics and Data Portability v0.10

Every registered user is considered a client by default. Therapist, establishment operator,
seller, contributor, advertiser, editor, moderator, and administrator capabilities are additive
roles, direct permissions, or scoped assignments attached to that same user account.

This document also includes the complete Client Service History, analytics, budgeting,
data-portability, import, export, and therapist-identity-portability direction formerly stored
in a separate planning document.

The workspace begins at /workspace. It is one permission-aware system rather than several unrelated websites for members, therapists, operators, sellers, contributors, advertisers, editors, moderators, and administrators.

A user sees the combined areas and actions allowed by the account's effective permissions and
record assignments. The user does not choose a role or context to make those capabilities appear.

================================================================================
1. PRIMARY GOALS
================================================================================

The User Workspace should:

- give every registered user one clear private home;
- treat every user as a client by default while allowing additional professional and operational roles;
- preserve one cross-provider booking and service history for the user;
- adapt to the user's permissions and managed records;
- keep personal activity organized;
- support profile, privacy, language, security, and notification settings;
- support saved items, follows, reviews, ratings, contributions, and bookings;
- support multiple roles without requiring several accounts;
- support multiple establishments or professional affiliations;
- remain usable on desktop and mobile browsers;
- become the basis for future mobile application features;
- avoid exposing private information through public routes.

================================================================================
2. ONE ACCOUNT, ADDITIVE RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================

One user may be:

- client or ordinary member by default;
- reviewer;
- contributor;
- correspondent;
- therapist;
- establishment owner;
- establishment manager;
- seller;
- advertiser;
- translator;
- editor;
- moderator;
- administrator.

The workspace does not force the user to sign in separately for each role, and it does not expose
a role or context switcher. Navigation, dashboard cards, queues, and managed records are composed
automatically from all effective permissions and scoped assignments.

For example, one person may simultaneously see ordinary client activity, their own practitioner
profile, two establishments they manage, and an assigned editorial queue. They should not need to
select "therapist," "operator," or "editor" first. Every record action still performs its own
authorization check; hiding navigation is not authorization.

3. WORKSPACE ROOT
================================================================================

Primary route:

/workspace/home

The home page may show:

- relevant account and assignment summary;
- important alerts;
- upcoming booking;
- pending task;
- unread notification;
- message summary;
- contribution status;
- managed-profile status;

- saved item;
- reputation summary;
- quick actions.

The dashboard should not display every possible card to every user.

Current implementation state (2026-07-21): the workspace shell is implemented behind
auth + verified + EnsureActiveMember. /workspace redirects to /workspace/home, which renders a
Personal-context dashboard (greeting via display name with username fallback, account summary,
My Reviews and My Articles counts linking to workspace-integrated areas, a direct New Article
action, and a claim invitation card). /workspace/profile edits the public display_name and
profile_biography
(user_main structure
guide records both fields), and /workspace/setting shows account facts and toggles the separate
marketing email opt-in. The Article area supports private drafting, language selection, ordered
custom or account-linked public bylines, shared owner access, structured sources, related database
records, revision history, review submission, and owner-initiated unpublishing. Article credit and
Article ownership are separate: appearing in a byline does not grant workspace access. Context
switching, notifications, messages, saved, following, history,
booking, and the remaining areas stay unimplemented; the dashboard names them as coming.

36. CURRENT WORKING DECISIONS
================================================================================

- /workspace is the single authenticated workspace root.
- Capability areas are permission-aware views, not separate role workspaces or websites.
- One account may hold several additive roles, direct permissions, and record assignments.
- Every user is considered a client by default.
- Client identity and service history remain available even when the user also holds professional or administrative capabilities.
- Private information is not exposed merely because a workspace page exists.
- Workspace actions use the shared Edit System.
- Managed-record permission is checked per record.
- Public profile and private identity remain separate.
- Mobile clients use the same backend permission system.
- The workspace should show only relevant navigation and cards.
- The workspace has no role or context switcher; the interface combines all effective access automatically.
- Ownership, investment, management, operational access, and administrative access are distinct concepts.
- One establishment may have several investors, owners, managers, operators, and representatives; one user may relate to several establishments.
- Contributions and relationship declarations never grant workspace access without review.

38. USER AS CLIENT BY DEFAULT
================================================================================

Every registered Massage Nexus user is a client by default.

This means the account may:
- create Personal Appointment Plans;
- submit eligible booking requests;
- receive provider confirmations;
- maintain private service history;
- record manual past visits;
- import and export eligible personal records;
- view spending and service analytics;
- maintain reusable booking preferences;
- receive provider loyalty and Massage Nexus rewards;
- submit ratings and reviews;
- add professional, business, contributor, or administrative roles later.

Client is not an exclusive role. A therapist remains a client when booking for themselves.
An establishment owner remains a client. An administrator remains a client outside administrative
actions. Professional and managed capabilities must not replace or erase the user's client activity.

42. PROVIDER VISIBILITY
================================================================================

A provider may view:
- bookings involving that provider;
- walk-ins entered by that provider;
- provider-confirmed legacy records under that provider;
- client-shared history specifically authorized for the current service.

A provider may not view:
- client history with another provider;
- private imported records;
- unrelated spending;
- unrelated intake;
- cross-provider cancellation history;
- client notes not shared.

An unclaimed profile receives no client identity or history access.

Anonymous aggregate may state:
"12 users privately recorded a past visit."

Aggregate rules:
- minimum count threshold;
- no names;
- no therapist;
- no exact date;
- no health data;
- no private notes;
- clearly labeled user-recorded and not provider-confirmed;
- opt-out where required.

45. THERAPIST PORTABILITY
================================================================================

A therapist profile is independent of one establishment.

Claimed therapist identity may use:
- stable therapist profile ID;
- unique public username or slug;
- verified user-account link.

An establishment adds or invites the existing therapist profile. It must not create a duplicate merely because the therapist changed employment.

Therapist history may distinguish current affiliation, former affiliation, independent work, contractor, trainee, and temporary assignment.

Provider booking data remains with provider and client according to policy. Therapist public identity, credentials, capabilities, reviews, and approved portable professional information remain connected to the therapist profile.

48. CORRECTION AND DISPUTE
================================================================================

A client may correct private manual record, imported mapping, note, product, price, or therapist link.

A confirmed provider record requires correction request, provider response, evidence, amendment, and audit.

Historical records are not silently overwritten when original fact matters. Use amendment or correction history.

49. DELETION, RETENTION, AND ANONYMIZATION
================================================================================

Private manual history may be deleted by the client unless legal or security hold applies.

Confirmed booking deletion follows provider, payment, tax, dispute, privacy, and platform retention rules.

Analytics may retain anonymized aggregates after personal deletion where allowed.

Provider-local import requires lawful basis and notice; Massage Nexus must not assume all legacy data may be uploaded.

50. PERSONAL DEFAULTS VERSUS HISTORY
================================================================================

Client booking defaults are current reusable settings. Service History records past events. They are not the same.

A past pressure selection may be offered as suggestion but must not automatically become permanent preference without client action.

Time-sensitive health intake is not copied into Service History.
